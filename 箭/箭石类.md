'''箭石類'''動物（[[學名|學名]]：{{lang|la|'''Belemnoidea'''}}），舊作'''箭石下綱'''，是一種已經滅絕[[頭足綱|頭足綱]]生物的分支，生活在[[泥盆紀|泥盆紀]]至[[白堊紀|白堊紀]]之間。箭石在許多方面都與現代的[[魷魚|魷魚]]相當接近<ref>{{cite journal|title=The Middle Eocene Belosaepia ungula (Cephalopoda: Coleoida) from Texas: structure, ontogeny and function|journal=Journal of Paleontology|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-paleontology/article/middle-eocene-belosaepia-ungula-cephalopoda-coleoida-from-texas-structure-ontogeny-and-function/EC5F6450ED5FC7FA43696F93937FA3B4|date=2010/03|volume=84|issue=2|doi=10.1666/09-018r.1|pages=267–287|issn=0022-3360|language=en|accessdate=2018-04-02|author=Thomas E. Yancey, Christopher L. Garvie, Mary Wicksten}}</ref>，而且與現代的[[烏賊|烏賊]]關係密切。箭石也擁有[[墨囊|墨囊]]<ref>Lehmann, U. 1981. ''The Ammonites: Their life and their world''. London: Cambridge University Press.</ref>，不過牠們有10條大約相同長度的[[觸腕|觸腕]]，其中並沒有特別長的觸腕<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://doi.wiley.com/10.1111/j.0031-0239.2004.00395.x|pages=983–998|title=The Jurassic Belemnite Suborder Belemnotheutina|journal=Palaeontology|volume=47|issue=4|date=2004-07-01|issn=1475-4983|language=en|accessdate=2018-04-02|doi=10.1111/j.0031-0239.2004.00395.x|author=Peter Doyle, Emma V. Shakides}}</ref>。

箭石的學名"belemnoid"是來自於[[希臘文|希臘文]]βέλεμνον（''belemnon''，意指「飛鏢或箭頭」），以及另一希臘字είδος（''eidos''，意指「形式」），即「箭頭形狀」的動物。<ref>''Webster's New Universal Unabridged Dictionary.'' 2nd ed. 1979.</ref>

箭石類動物包括了[[箭石目|箭石目]]、[[閉箭石目|閉箭石目]]、[[溝箭石目|溝箭石目]]和[[Diplobelida|Diplobelida]]。

== 演化歷史 ==
箭石類在[[侏羅紀|侏羅紀]]與白堊紀時數量相當多，所以牠們的化石在[[中生代|中生代]]的海洋岩層中相當豐富，經常伴隨著[[菊石類|菊石類]]出現。不過箭石也跟菊石一起在白堊紀末期滅絕了。箭石是由[[桿石目|桿石]]於泥盆紀時期演化而來，從[[密西西比紀|密西西比紀]]（或[[早石炭世|早石炭世]]）至[[白堊紀|白堊紀]]末之間的地層中可以發現良好的箭石化石，其他頭足類如[[桿菊石|桿菊石]]、[[鸚鵡螺亞綱|鸚鵡螺類]]及[[稜菊石目|稜菊石類]]等也可發現。

== 解剖學 ==
Belemnoids possessed a central [[phragmocone|phragmocone]] made of [[aragonite|aragonite]] and with negative buoyancy.<ref name=Monks1996>{{cite journal|title=The Function of the Belemnite Guard|journal=Paläontologische Zeitschrift|doi=10.1007/bf02988082|url=https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/BF02988082|date=1996-11-01|volume=70|issue=3-4|language=en|pages=425|issn=0031-0220|accessdate=2018-04-02|author=Neale Monks, J. David Hardwick, Andrew S. Gale}}</ref>  To the rear of the creature was a heavy [[calcite|calcite]] guard whose main role appears to have been to counterbalance the front (towards the head) of the organism; it positions the centre of mass below the centre of buoyancy, increasing the stability of the swimming organism.<ref name=Monks1996/>  The guard would account for between a third and a fifth of the length of the complete organism, arms included.<ref name=Monks1996/>

Like some modern squid, belemnoid arms carried a series of small hooks for grabbing [[prey|prey]]. Belemnoids were efficient [[carnivore|carnivore]]s that caught small [[fish|fish]] and other marine animals with their arms and ate them with their [[cephalopod_beak|beak-like jaws]]. In turn, belemnites appear to have formed part of the diet of marine [[reptile|reptile]]s such as [[Ichthyosaur|Ichthyosaur]]s, whose fossilized [[stomach|stomach]]s frequently contain [[Phosphate_minerals|phosphatic]] hooks from the arms of cephalopods.

== 生態學 ==
Belemnoids were effectively neutrally buoyant, and swam in near-shore to mid-shelf oceans.<ref name=Monks1996/>  Their fins could be used to their advantage in all water speeds; in a gentle current they could be flapped for propulsion; in a stronger current they could be held erect to generate lift; and when swimming rapidly by jet propulsion they could be tucked in to the body for streamlining.<ref name=Monks1996/>

== 化石保存 ==
[[File:Fossil-Belemnoidea-complete.jpg|thumb]]
[[File:Hibolites_barnacle_borings.JPG|thumb]][[莫內瓦|莫內瓦]]及[[特魯埃爾|特魯埃爾]]附近的[[侏羅紀|侏羅紀]]地層出土的[[希波箭石|希波箭石]]（''Hibolites hastatus''）護甲化石，其表面的[[藤壺|藤壺]]{{tsl|en|Rogerella|袋狀鑽孔跡}}（''Rogerella''）顯現出牠死後軀體在海底待了很長一段時間。]]

箭石形成化石的部份大多是其結實的「護甲」，通常呈拉長的子彈形。(though in some subgroups the rostrum may only exist as a thin layer coating the phragmocone).The hollow region at the front of the guard is termed the ''alveolus'' 護甲後端的中穴內包覆或連接著[[圓錐|圓錐]]狀的{{tsl|en|Phragmocone|閉錐}}，而閉錐就是箭石調節浮力的氣室，大多數箭石標本的護甲末端中穴與閉錐部份常會被壓碎，沒有完整的保存下來，只有保存較好的標本能保留下來。Projecting forwards from one side of the phragmocone is the thin ''pro-ostracum''.

While belemnoid phragmocones are [[Homology_(biology)|homologous]] with the shells of other [[cephalopods|cephalopods]] and are similarly composed of [[aragonite|aragonite]], belemnoid guards are evolutionarily novel and are composed of [[calcite|calcite]] or [[aragonite|aragonite]], thus tending to preserve well. Broken guards show a structure of radiating calcite fibers and may also display concentric [[growth_rings|growth rings]].

The guard, phragmocone and pro-ostracum were all internal to the living creature, forming a skeleton which was enclosed entirely by soft muscular tissue. The original living creature would have been larger than the fossilized shell, with a long streamlined body and prominent eyes. The guard would have been in place toward the rear of the creature, with the phragmocone behind the head and the pointed end of the guard facing backward.

The guard of the belemnoid ''[[Megateuthis_gigantea|Megateuthis gigantea]]'', which is found in [[Europe|Europe]] and [[Asia|Asia]], can measure up to {{convert|46|cm}} in length, giving the living animal an estimated length of {{convert|3|m|ft}}.

Very exceptional belemnoid specimens have been found showing the preserved soft parts of the animal.  Elsewhere in the fossil record, bullet-shaped belemnite guards are locally found in such profusion that such deposits are referred to semi-formally as "belemnite battlefields" (cf. [[orthoceras|"orthocone orgies"]]).  It remains unclear whether these deposits represent post-mating mass death events, as are common among modern [[cephalopods|cephalopods]] and other [[semelparous|semelparous]] creatures.

== 用途 ==
The [[isotope_geochemistry|stable isotope]] composition of a belemnoid rostrum from the [[Peedee_Formation|Peedee Formation]] ([[Cretaceous|Cretaceous]], southeast USA) has long been used as a global standard (Peedee Belemnite, "PDB") against which other [[isotope_geochemistry|isotope geochemistry]] samples are measured, for both [[isotopes_of_carbon|carbon isotopes]] and [[isotopes_of_oxygen|oxygen isotopes]].

Some belemnoids (such as ''Belemnites'' of [[Belemnitida|Belemnitida]]) serve as [[index_fossil|index fossil]]s, particularly in the Cretaceous [[Chalk_Formation|Chalk Formation]] of Europe, enabling [[geologist|geologist]]s to date the age the [[rock_(geology)|rocks]] in which they are found.
