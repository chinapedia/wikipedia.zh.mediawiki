'''亨利·尼古拉斯·约翰·冈瑟'''（{{lang-en|Henry Nicholas John Gunther}}，1895年6月6日 – 1918年11月11日）是一名[[美國陸軍|美國陸軍]]士兵，也是[[第一次世界大战|第一次世界大战]]中牺牲的最后一名士兵<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/magazine/7696021.stm |title=The last soldiers to die in World War I |first=John |last=Hayes-Fisher |publisher=[[BBC_News|BBC News]] |date=2008-10-29 |accessdate=2012-10-24|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140106174710/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/magazine/7696021.stm|archivedate=2014-01-06}}</ref><ref name="Meyer">{{cite journal|url=http://www.marylandlife.com/articles/the-unknown-soldier |last=Meyer |first=Eugene |title=The Unknown Soldier |journal=Maryland Life |date=2008-11-01 |accessdate=2012-10-25 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101229024315/http://www.marylandlife.com/articles/the-unknown-soldier |archivedate=2010-12-29 }}</ref><ref name="Sun">{{cite news|last=Rodricks|first=Dan|authorlink=Dan Rodricks|title=The sad, senseless end of Henry Gunther|url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/2008-11-11/news/0811100097_1_henry-gunther-11th-month-war-i|journal=[[Baltimore_Sun|Baltimore Sun]]|date=2008-11-11|accessdate=2012-10-24}}</ref>。他死于1918年11月11日上午10点59分，[[停战协议|停战协议]]生效前一分钟<!-- He was killed one minute before the [[Armistice_with_Germany|armistice]] at 11 am.<ref>{{cite book|first=Joseph E.|last=Persico|authorlink=Joseph E. == 战时服役 ==
1917年9月冈瑟入伍并获委任加入第313团，该团别称“巴尔的摩自己人”，是{{link-en|美國陸軍第79步兵師|79th Infantry Division (United States)}}第157旅的一部分。
作为补给中士，他在他的部队中负责服装配给，并于1918年7月抵达法国成为{{link-en|美国远征军|American Expeditionary Forces}}的一员。之后因他写了一封危险性的家书，在信中他描述了前线的“悲惨状况”，并告诫他的朋友想尽办法避免被征兵入伍，信最终被审查员截下。结果，他从军士降为[[二等兵|二等兵]]。<ref name="Sun"/><ref name="Find A Grave"/><ref name="Persico 134"/>
<!--
Gunther was drafted in September 1917 and was appointed to the 313th Regiment, which had the nickname "Baltimore's Own" and was part of the 157th Brigade of the [[79th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)|79th Infantry Division]]. As a supply [[Sergeant#United_States|sergeant]], he was responsible for clothing in his military unit, and arrived in [[France|France]] in July 1918 as part of the [[American_Expeditionary_Forces|American Expeditionary Forces]]. A critical letter home, in which he reported on the "miserable conditions" at the front and advised a friend to try anything to avoid being drafted, was intercepted by the censor. As a result, he was demoted from sergeant to [[Private_(rank)#United_States|private]].<ref name="Sun"/><ref name="Find A Grave"/><ref name="Persico 134"/>-->

冈瑟所在的A连，在1918年9月12日抵达前线。正如其他在{{link-en|默兹-阿尔贡战役|Meuse-Argonne Offensive}}前线的盟军部队一样，A连也卷入了11月11日上午的战斗<ref>[[#Persico|《Eleventh Month, Eleventh Day, Eleventh Hour: Armistice Day, 1918: World War I and Its Violent Climax》，pp. 134-135]]</ref>。与德军停战的[[康边停战协定|协定]]是在凌晨5:00签署的，但它只会在上午11:00开始生效。在[[绍蒙德旺当维莱尔|绍蒙德旺当维莱尔]]的村庄中，冈瑟所在的[[班|班]]接近一个由两挺德军[[机枪|机枪]]组成的路障。冈瑟站起身来，不顾他的军士及密友欧内斯特·鲍尔的命令，并一边挥动他的刺刀一边大声指责。

德国士兵知道停战协议将在一分钟内生效，试图挥手驱赶冈瑟未果，遂开枪射击了“一两发”<ref name="Sun"/>。因为靠机枪太近，冈瑟被当场中枪身亡<ref name="Find A Grave"/><ref name="Persico 351">[[#Persico|《Eleventh Month, Eleventh Day, Eleventh Hour: Armistice Day, 1918: World War I and Its Violent Climax》，p. 351]]</ref>。作家[[詹姆斯·凱恩|詹姆斯·M·凯恩]]，也是《巴尔的摩太阳报》的记者在采访了冈瑟战友们后写道：“在被降级之后，冈瑟做了大量反思，之后就执迷于在长官和战友面前表现以消除以往的不良影响。” <ref name="Sun"/>

[[约翰·潘兴|约翰·潘兴]]将军在翌日的{{link-en|通报表彰|Mentioned in Despatches}}中提及冈瑟为美国在第一次世界大战中牺牲的最后一人<ref name="Persico 351"/>。陆军追授恢复他中士军衔，嘉奖其英勇行为并授予他[[杰出服役十字勋章|杰出服役十字勋章]]。巴尔的摩的一个{{link-en|海外战争退伍军人协会|Veterans of Foreign Wars}}以他命名<ref name="Meyer"/><ref name="Sun"/><ref>[[#Persico|《Eleventh Month, Eleventh Day, Eleventh Hour: Armistice Day, 1918: World War I and Its Violent Climax》，p. 394]]</ref>。

冈瑟的遗体在1923年回到美国，并埋葬在巴尔的摩的至圣救主公墓<ref name="Meyer"/><ref name="Find A Grave"/>。随后的调查显示，在第一次世界大战的最后一天，在停战协定从签署直至生效期间，约11000人被打伤或打死——远远超过平常<ref>[[#Persico|《Eleventh Month, Eleventh Day, Eleventh Hour: Armistice Day, 1918: World War I and Its Violent Climax》，p. 378]]</ref>。
<!--
Gunther's unit, Company A, arrived at the front on September 12, 1918. Like all Allied units on the front of the [[Meuse-Argonne_Offensive|Meuse-Argonne Offensive]], it was still embroiled in fighting on the morning of November 11.<ref>Persico, pp. 134-135.</ref> The [[Armistice_with_Germany|Armistice with Germany]] was signed by 5:00 am, but it would only come into force at 11:00 am. Gunther's squad approached a roadblock of two German [[machine_gun|machine gun]]s in the village of [[Chaumont-devant-Damvillers|Chaumont-devant-Damvillers]]. Gunther got up, against the orders of his sergeant and close friend, Ernest Powell, and charged with his [[bayonet|bayonet]]. The German soldiers, aware of the [[armistice|armistice]] that would take effect in one minute, tried to wave Gunther off. He kept going and fired "a shot or two".<ref name="Sun"/> When he got too close to the machine guns, he was shot and killed instantly.<ref name="Find A Grave"/><ref name="Persico 351">Persico, p. 351.</ref> The writer [[James_M._Cain|James M. Cain]], then a reporter for the ''[[Baltimore_Sun|Baltimore Sun]]'', interviewed Gunther's comrades afterward and wrote that "Gunther brooded a great deal over his reduction in rank, and became obsessed with a determination to make good before his officers and fellow soldiers."<ref name="Sun"/>

General [[John_J._Pershing|John J. Pershing]]'s [[Mentioned_in_Despatches|order of the day]] on the following day mentioned Gunther as the last American killed in the war.<ref name="Persico 351"/> The Army posthumously restored his rank of sergeant and awarded him a Divisional Citation for Gallantry in Action and the [[Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_States)|Distinguished Service Cross]]. A [[Veterans_of_Foreign_Wars|Veterans of Foreign Wars]] post in Baltimore was named after him.<ref name="Meyer"/><ref name="Sun"/><ref>Persico, p. 394.</ref>

Gunther's remains were returned to the United States in 1923 and buried at Most Holy Redeemer Cemetery in Baltimore.<ref name="Meyer"/><ref name="Find A Grave"/> Subsequent investigations revealed that on the last day of World War I, between the signing of the armistice and its entry into force, about 11,000 men were wounded or killed - far more than usual.<ref>Persico, p. 378.</ref>-->
