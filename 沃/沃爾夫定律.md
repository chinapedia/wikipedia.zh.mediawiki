'''沃爾夫定律'''（{{lang-en|'''Wolff's law'''}}）是由十九世紀時德國的[[解剖學|解剖學]]家及[[外科|外科]]醫生{{link-en|朱利叶斯·沃尔夫|Julius Wolff}}提出的理論，認為健康的人類或動物的[[骨骼|骨骼]]會適應所在部位需承受的負載<ref>{{cite news |author=[[Anahad_O'Connor|Anahad O'Connor]] |coauthors= |title=The Claim: After Being Broken, Bones Can Become Even Stronger |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2010/10/19/health/19really.html?ref=science |quote=This concept — that bone adapts to pressure, or a lack of it — is known as Wolff’s law. ... there is no evidence that a bone that breaks will heal to be stronger than it was before.|work=[[New_York_Times|New York Times]] |date=October 18, 2010 |accessdate=2010-10-19 }}</ref>。若特定骨骼的負載增加了，骨骼會慢慢的變強壯，來承受該負載<ref>{{cite journal |year=1994 |last1=Frost |first1=HM |journal=The Angle Orthodontist |volume=64 |issue=3|pages=175–188 |pmid=8060014 |title=Wolff's Law and bone's structural adaptations to mechanical usage: an overview for clinicians |doi=10.1043/0003-3219(1994)064<0175:WLABSA>2.0.CO;2}}</ref>。骨中{{link-en|小梁|trabecula}}的內在結構會產生適應性的變化，而骨的外層皮層也會隨之變化<ref>Stedman's Medical Dictionary</ref>，因此也有可能使骨骼變粗。反之亦然，若骨骼負載減少，骨骼也會變細，因為該骨骼的新陳代謝較不需要，而且沒有刺激來產生可以維持骨質量的{{link-en|骨的再建|bone remodeling}}<ref name="Wolff1">Wolff J. "The Law of Bone Remodeling". Berlin Heidelberg New York: Springer, 1986 (translation of the German 1892 edition)</ref>。
