'''海登海姆1846第一足球俱乐部'''（{{lang-de|'''1. FC Heidenheim 1846'''}}）是位於[[德國|德國]][[巴登-符腾堡州|巴登-符腾堡州]]东部[[海登海姆县|海登海姆县]]首府[[海登海姆|海登海姆]]的足球會，現時在[[德国足球丙级联赛|德国足球丙级联赛]]參賽。

== 歷史 ==
The current day club was formed in [[2007|2007]] through the separation of the football section from parent association ''Heidenheimer Sportbund'', a larger sports club that has 5,800 members in 27 departments. The independence of the football side allows it to operate under the stricter economic standards set for professional clubs which are members of the [[German_Football_Association|German Football Association]] (Deutscher Fußball-Bund or German Football Association).

''Heidenheimer SB'' itself was founded through the [[1972|1972]] merger of ''TSB Heidenheim'' and ''VfL Heidenheim''. The club's origins go back to the August 14, 1846 establishment of the gymnastics club ''Turngemeinde Heidenheim'', which folded in [[1852|1852]], but was re-constituted under the same name in [[1861|1861]]. The club was re-named ''Turnverein Heidenheim'' in [[1872|1872]].

A football department was created within the association on July 8, 1911 and became an independent side known as ''VfR 1911 Heidenheim'' on August 21, 1922. The swimming club ''Schwimmverein 04 Heidenheim'' joined ''VfR'' in [[1936|1936]] to form ''VfL Heidenheim 04''. In [[1949|1949]], following [[World_War_II|World War II]], these two clubs went their separate ways, the swimmers under their original name, and the footballers as ''VfL Heidenheim 1911''.

In the meantime, parent club ''TV 1846 Heidenheim'' was joined on July 13, 1935 by ''SpVgg Heidenheim'' and then on April 3, 1937 merged with ''1. Sportverein 1900 Heidenheim'' - which was known as ''Athletenklub Hellenstein'' until [[1920|1920]] - to become ''TSV 1846 Heidenheim''. After the war ''TSV'' was united with ''Turnerbund Heidenheim 1902'' whose history was as a worker's club. ''TB'' was established on December 21, 1902 and was re-named ''Turnerbund Heidenheim'' on August 6, 1904. This club merged with ''Arbeiterturnverein 1904 Heidenheim'' on March 8, 1919. Like other worker's clubs, ''TB'' was considered as politically unreliable by the [[Nazi|Nazi]] regime and was dissolved in [[1933|1933]]. It was re-established after the war and on February 3, 1946 joined ''TSV 1846 Heidenheim'' to form ''TSB 1846 Heidenheim''.

The May 27, 1972 merger of ''TSB'' and ''VfL'' brought all these threads together, returning the footballers to the fold of the original gymnastics club. ''Heidenheimer SB'' and predecessor ''VfL Heidenheim'' played in the [[Amateurliga_Württemberg|Amateurliga Württemberg]] (III) from [[1963|1963]]-[[1975|75]] and again from [[1976|1976]]-[[1979|79]]. Regional cup wins led to the team's participation in the opening round of the [[DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]] (German Cup) in [[1975|1975]], [[1978|1978]], and [[1980|1980]], before the side slipped into lower level competition.

The club has since recovered and in [[2004|2004]] advanced to the [[Oberliga_Baden-Württemberg|Oberliga Baden-Württemberg]]. A successful season finish in 2008 saw the club being promoted to the [[Regionalliga_Süd|Regionalliga Süd]]. Having simultaneously won the [[Württemberg_Cup|Württemberg Cup]], Heidenheim was allowed to participate in the first round of the [[DFB-Pokal|DFB-Pokal]] in the following season, where the team lost 0:3 to [[VfL_Wolfsburg|VfL Wolfsburg]]. In 2009, Heidenheim finished first in the Regionalliga Süd and got promoted to the [[3._Liga|3. Liga]].

FC Heidenheim welcomed Premiership team [[Birmingham_City|Birmingham City]] to their stadium for a friendly game in July 2007. The final score was 1-0 to the Blues courtesy of a well taken Mikael Forssell strike.

== 主場球場 ==
海登海姆自1973年6月開始已在「艾比球場」（Albstadion）進行主場賽事，初時容量8,000人。在球隊升班到[[德丙|德丙]]後，為符合參賽規定，球場擴建到可容10,000人的德丙最低要求，並獲[[盧森堡|盧森堡]]的职工福利住房股份公司（GAGFAH）[[贊助|贊助]]冠名稱為「职工福利住房竞技场」（GAGFAH-Arena）。
