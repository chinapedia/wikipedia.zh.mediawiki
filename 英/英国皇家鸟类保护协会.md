英国皇家鸟类保护协会（RSPB）是[[英国|英国]]的一个慈善组织，<ref>[http://apps.charitycommission.gov.uk/ShowCharity/RegisterOfCharities/searchresulthandler.aspx?chyno=207076 Royal Society for the Protection of Birds, Registered Charity no. 207076]<span contenteditable="false">  at the </span>Charity Commission<span contenteditable="false"></span></ref><ref>[http://www.oscr.org.uk/search-charity-register/charity-extract/?charitynumber=SC037654 Royal Society for the Protection of Birds, Registered Charity no.]</ref> 成立于1889年。 它不断通过组织公共宣传活动、[[请愿|请愿]]和协会的自然保护区来促进鸟类和环境的保护。<ref name="do?"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[https://web.archive.org/web/20070102193600/http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/fdIII/what_does_the_rspb_do_.asp "What does the RSPB do?"]</cite></ref>

英国皇家鸟类保护协会拥有超过1300名员工、18000名志愿者和100多万会员（包括195,000青年团员），它已成为欧洲最大的保护野生动物的慈善机构。<ref name="about"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/ "About the RSPB"]. </cite></ref>英国皇家鸟类保护协会有许多地方团体和约200个自然保护区。<ref name="reserves"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/reserves/ "Reserves"]. </cite></ref>

== 历史 ==
[[File:Fletcher_moss_croft2.jpg|thumb]]
1889年，艾米莉·威廉姆森在[[曼彻斯特|曼彻斯特]]迪兹伯里的自宅（现在的弗莱彻苔藓植物园）創立了羽衣聯盟（The Plumage League）<ref name="milestones"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/history/milestones.asp "Milestones"]. </cite></ref>，成立宗旨是反对用凤头鸊鷉和海鸥的皮與羽毛制作裘衣。<ref name="history"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/history/index.asp "History of the RSPB"]. </cite></ref>該組織之後與毛皮和羽毛聯盟（Fur and Feather League）在克羅伊登合併成現今的英國皇家鳥類保護協會。

英国皇家鸟类保护协会的创始成员都是女性一种带有异国风情的帽子，这种帽子由皮毛制成。她们全力反对使用这种帽子。<ref name="history"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/history/index.asp "History of the RSPB"]. </cite></ref>

英国皇家鸟类保护协会成立时，制作帽子的羽毛的贸易额非常大。在1884年第一季度，大约有7000极乐鸟的毛皮被进口到英国，此外还有西印度和巴西的40万鸟和东印度的36万只鸟。{{Template:Sfnp|Adams|2004|p = 189}}

英国皇家鸟类保护协会不断吸引一些社会地位较高的妇女的支持。渐渐地，协会也开始吸引许多其他影响力较高的人物，且不分男女，如鸟类学家教授Alfred Newton。协会得到了较好的发展，吸引了许多新成员。成立仅15年后，协会便得到了爱德华七世的皇家宪章。英国皇家鸟类保护协会还帮助英国议会推出了禁止用羽毛制作服装的法律。<ref name="milestones"><cite class="citation web" contenteditable="false">[http://www.rspb.org.uk/about/history/milestones.asp "Milestones"]. </cite></ref>

== 主要活动 ==
现在，英国皇家鸟类保护协会与公务员一起工作，并同这些公务员一起向政府提供关于环保的政策。 在英国，它是协助编写官方所有已发现鸟类的保护状态列表的组织之一。

1903年4月，杂志第一次出版，当时杂志的名字叫做Bird Notes and News（ISSN0406-3392）1947年，名字被改为''Bird Notes''。。

1947年，的标题改为''Bird Notes''。在20世纪50年代，杂志每年发布四次，（每逢三月，六月，九月和十二月的第一天发布一次）。Each volume covered two years, spread over three calendar years. For example, volume XXV (25), number one was dated Winter 1951, and number eight in the same volume was dated Autumn 1953.

From the mid-1950s, many of the covers were by Charles Tunnicliffe. Two of the originals are on long-term loan to the Tunnicliffe gallery at Oriel Ynys Môn, but in 1995 the RSPB sold 114 at a [[蘇富比|Sotheby's]] auction, raising £210,000, the most expensive being a picture of a partridge which sold for £6,440.<ref name="Birds16:01">RSPB ''Birds'' magazine, Vol 16 No 01, February–April 1996, page 10</ref>

==== ''Birds'' ====
''Bird Notes''' successor ''Birds'' ({{Template:ISSN|1367-983X}}) replaced it immediately, with volume 1, number 1 being the January–February 1966 edition. Issues were published quarterly, numbered so that a new volume started every other year.

The Autumn 2013 edition, dated August–October 2013, being vol. 25 no. 7, was the last.<ref name="Ward"><cite class="citation journal" contenteditable="false">Ward, Mark (Autumn 2013). </cite></ref>

== 财政情况 ==
英国皇家鸟类保护协会主要依靠协会成员的资助来获取资金。截至2006年，协会超过50％的资产来自订阅杂志、捐赠和遗赠，合计53669000英镑。<sup>[1]</sup>作为一个已注册过的慈善机构，the organisation is entitled to gift aid worth an extra £0.28 on every £1.00 donated by income taxpayers.<sup>[32]</sup> It also receives contractual payments from clean energy utilities and financiers of renewable energy solutions, when its members sign up as clients.<sup>[33]</sup> The bulk of the income (£63.757 million in 2006) is spent on conservation projects, maintenance of the reserves and on education projects, with the rest going on fundraising efforts and reducing the pension deficit, worth £19.8 million in 2006.

The RSPB is funded primarily by its members; in 2006, over 50% of the society's £88 million income came from [[subscription|subscription]]s, [[donation|donation]]s and [[Inheritance|legacies]], worth a total of £ 53.669 million.<ref name="annual" /> As a [[registered_charity|registered charity]], the organisation is entitled to [[gift_aid|gift aid]] worth an extra £0.28 on every £1.00 donated by [[Income_tax|Income_tax]] payers.<ref name="GA">{{cite web|url = http://www.rspb.org.uk/supporting/giftaid/index.asp|title = Gift Aid|publisher = RSPB|accessdate = 19 February 2007|deadurl = yes|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20070222173947/http://www.rspb.org.uk/supporting/giftaid/index.asp|archivedate = 22 February 2007}}</ref> It also receives contractual payments from [[Sustainable_energy|clean energy]] utilities and financiers of [[Renewable_energy|Renewable_energy]] solutions, when its members sign up as clients.<ref name="dm">{{cite news |last = Dellingpole|first = James|title = RSPB makes a killing... from windfarm giants behind turbines accused of destroying rare birds|url = http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2305197/RSPB-makes-killing--windfarm-giants-turbines-accused-destroying-rare-birds.html|accessdate = 23 December 2013|newspaper = Daily Mail|date = 7 April 2013}}</ref> The bulk of the income (£63.757 million in 2006) is spent on conservation projects, maintenance of the reserves and on education projects, with the rest going on fundraising efforts and reducing the pension deficit, worth £19.8 million in 2006.
