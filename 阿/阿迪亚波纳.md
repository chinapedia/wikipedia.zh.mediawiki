'''阿迪亚波纳'''（{{lang-grc|Ἀδιαβηνή}}；{{lang-syc|ܚܕܝܐܒ}}；[[中古波斯语|中古波斯语]]：Nodshēragān<ref>Richard Nelson Frye, 1984, ''The History of Ancient Iran'': Volume 3, Part 7 - Page 222</ref> ；[[亚美尼亚语|亚美尼亚语]]：Նոր Շիրական）是[[亚述|亚述]]一个古老的王国<ref name="Chronicle of Arbela">{{cite web|title=The Chronicle of Arbela|url=http://www.eacna.org/Apostolic%20Line.pdf|format=PDF|quote=In 115, the Romans invaded Adiabene and named it Assyria.|access-date=2007-10-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040428131344/http://www.eacna.org/Apostolic%20Line.pdf|archive-date=2004-04-28|dead-url=yes|df=}}</ref><ref>''The Biblical Geography of Central Asia: with a General Introduction'', by {{tsl|en|Ernst Friedrich Karl Rosenmüller||Ernst Friedrich Karl Rosenmüller}}. Page 122.</ref><ref>In Memory of Rabbi and Mrs. Carl Friedman: Studies on the Problem of Tannaim in Babylonia (ca. 130–160 C. E.) Author(s): Jacob Neusner Source: Proceedings of the American Academy for Jewish Research, Vol. 30 (1962), pp. 79–127.</ref><ref>Ammianus Marcellinus, another fourth-century writer. In his excursus on the Sasanian Empire, he describes Assyria in such a way that there is no mistaking he is talking about lower Mesopotamia (Amm. Marc. XXIII. 6. 15). For Assyria, he lists three major cities-Babylon, Ctesiphon and Seleucia (Amm. Marc. xxIII. 6. 23), whereas he refers to Adiabene as 'Assyria priscis temporibus vocitata' (Amm. Marc. xxIII. 6. 20).</ref>，首都为[[阿尔贝拉|阿尔贝拉]]。

阿迪亚波纳统治者于公元1世纪皈依[[犹太教|犹太教]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Gottheil|first=Richard
|title=Adiabene|url=http://jewishencyclopedia.com/articles/801-adiabene|work={{tsl|en|Jewish Encyclopedia||Jewish Encyclopedia}}|accessdate=8 November 2011}}</ref>。王后{{tsl|en|Helena of Adiabene|阿迪亚波纳的海伦娜|海倫娜}}还移居[[耶路撒冷|耶路撒冷]]，并在[[大卫城|大卫城]]北部[[圣殿山|圣殿山]]南侧为自己，以及儿子兴建{{tsl|en|Izates bar Monobaz|埃沙德斯·巴尔·蒙罗巴斯}}与{{tsl|en|Monobaz II|蒙罗巴斯二世}}兴建了宫殿。此外，她还协助以色列与罗马作战<ref>{{cite journal |last=Neusner |first=Jacob |title=The Conversion of Adiabene to Judaism: A New Perspective |journal=Journal of Biblical Literature |volume=83 |issue=1 |pages=60–66 |jstor=3264908 |date=1964}}</ref>。
