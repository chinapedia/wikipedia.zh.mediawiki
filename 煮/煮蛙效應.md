'''煮蛙效应'''（或'''温水煮青蛙'''）源于一只[[青蛙|青蛙]]被慢慢[[烹刑|煮死]]的[[轶事|故事]]。其论述为如果将一只青蛙放进沸水中，它会跳出来，但如果将它放进冷水中慢慢加热，将会察觉不到危险而被煮死，这个故事经常被用来[[隐喻|比喻]]人们不能或不愿注意逐渐产生的威胁或对此作出反应。 

虽然一些19世纪的实验显示，如果加热足够缓慢，那么论述属实<ref name="Offerman 2010">{{Harvnb|Offerman|2010}}</ref><ref name="Sedgwick1888">{{Harvnb|Sedgwick|1888|p=399}}</ref>，然而根据现代生物学家的研究发现这个论述是错误的：如果把青蛙放入水中逐渐加热，它依然会跳出<ref name="fastcompany">{{Cite web|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/magazine/01/frog.html|title=Next Time, What Say We Boil a Consultant|accessdate=2006-03-10|date=October 1995|work=Fast Company Issue 01|ref=harv|postscript=<!-- Bot inserted parameter. Either remove it; or change its value to "." for the cite to end in a ".", as necessary. -->{{inconsistent citations}}}}</ref><ref name="ecoviews">[http://srel.uga.edu/outreach/ecoviews/ecoview021118.htm "The legend of the boiling frog is just a legend"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151231220125/http://srel.uga.edu/outreach/ecoviews/ecoview021118.htm |date=2015-12-31 }} by Whit Gibbons, ''Ecoviews'', November 18, 2002, retrieved November 24, 2015</ref>。

== 科学 ==
作为推动科学发展的一部分，在十九世纪时，曾有人做过一些逐渐加热水，然后观察青蛙反应的实验。1869年，为了寻找「灵魂所在的地方」，德国生理学家{{Le|弗里德里希·戈尔茨|Friedrich Goltz}}实验并证明，被取出大脑的青蛙会停留在逐渐加热的水中，而身体结构完整的青蛙则在水温达到25°C时试图跳出逐渐加热的水中。<ref name="Offerman 2010" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jamesfallows.theatlantic.com/archives/2009/07/guest-post_wisdom_on_frogs.php|title=Guest-post wisdom on frogs|accessdate=2009-07-22|author=[[James_Fallows|James Fallows]]|date=21 July 2009|work=[[The_Atlantic|The Atlantic]]|ref=harv|postscript=<!-- Bot inserted parameter. Either remove it; or change its value to "." for the cite to end in a ".", as necessary. -->{{inconsistent citations}}}}</ref>

其他的实验表明，青蛙并没有试图跳出逐渐加热的水中。1872年海茵茨曼（Heinzmann）论证了如果水慢慢加热，那么一个正常的青蛙将不会试图逃跑<ref>{{Harvnb|Sedgwick|1888|p=390}}</ref>，并在1875年被弗拉切尔（Fratscher）证明<ref>{{Harvnb|Sedgwick|1888|p=394}}</ref>。

戈尔茨在10分钟内，将水的温度从17.5°C加热到56°C，水温平均每分钟上升3.8°C，他的实验表明正常的青蛙试图逃跑；而海茵茨曼（Heinzmann）将青蛙从大概21°C加热到37.5°C，速率小于每分钟0.2°C<ref name="Offerman 2010" />。在“由温度变化引起反射刺激改变的青蛙（On the Variation of Reflex Excitability in the Frog induced by changes of Temperature,1882）”{{Le|威廉·汤普森·塞奇威克|William Thompson Sedgwick}}写道：“在一次实验中温度以每秒0.002℃的速度升高，两个半小时后青蛙就一动不动，一命呜呼了”。<ref>Edward Scripture, ''[http://books.google.com/books?id=Hr0aAAAAMAAJ&dq=The+New+Psychology The New Psychology]'' (1897): [http://books.google.com/books?pg=PA300&vq=frog&dq=The+New+Psychology&id=Hr0aAAAAMAAJ#v=onepage&q=%20&f=false]. The original 1872 experiment was cited in: Sedgwick, "On the Variation of Reflex Excitability in the Frog induced by changes of Temperature," ''Stud. Biol. Lab. Johns Hopkins University'' (1882): 385. "in one experiment the temperature was raised at a rate of 0.002 °C. per second, and the frog was found dead at the end of 2? hours without having moved."</ref>

1888年塞奇威克（Sedgwick）解释了在这些实验中使用不同的加热速率得到不同的结果之间明显的矛盾：“真相似乎是如果加热足够缓慢，那么即使是正常的青蛙也不会产生运动反射；如果速率更快一些，发生在足以被称为“缓慢”的速率，在任何情况下也不会获得正常青蛙的反应”。<ref name="Sedgwick1888" />

现代声音倾向于否认这种现象是真的。 1995年，[[哈佛大学|哈佛大学]]生物系教授{{Le|道格拉斯·梅尔顿|Douglas A. Melton}}说：“如果你把一只青蛙放到沸水中，它不会跳出，而会死亡。如果你把它放到冷水中，它会在水变热之前跳出——它们不会坐以待毙。”[[美国国立自然历史博物馆|美国国立自然历史博物馆]]两栖爬行动物馆馆长乔治·R·祖格（George R. Zug）博士也斥驳了这种说法“如果青蛙有逃走的手段，它一定会全身而退。”<ref name="fastcompany" />

在2002年，[[俄克拉何马大学|俄克拉何马大学]]动物学退休教授维克托·H·哈金森（Victor H. Hutchison）说：“传言完全不正确！”。他描述称许多蛙类的{{Le|临界热极值|Critical thermal maximum|最大承受温度}}已经被现代实验研究确定：如果水以每分钟2°F或1.1°C加热，如果青蛙所处容器可以使青蛙跳出，青蛙会因设法逃离而变的越来越来活跃，并最终跳出。<ref name="ecoviews" />

=== 比喻 ===
{{Quote box|quote=如果你将一只青蛙放进沸水里，它必然会拼命地想办法攀爬出来。但是如果你轻轻地将其放置在不温不火的一锅水里并降低加热速度，它将十分坦然地漂浮着。随着水温逐渐升高，青蛙会陷入安宁的恍惚之中，酷似我们中的一个热水澡，过不了多久，它的脸上露出了笑容，它将没有抵抗允许自己被煮死。|source=[[丹尼尔·奎因|丹尼尔·奎因]][[B的故事|B的故事]]的故事文本。|width=40%|align=right}}

温水煮青蛙的故事通常用以比喻告诫人们要注意逐渐产生的变化，免得最终落得个悲惨的下场。它被视为[[滑坡谬误|滑坡谬误]]的一类论据说法，作为对{{le|蔓延常态|creeping normality}}的警示。它也被用于商业上以加强需要逐渐接受变化的观念。<ref name="volokh">{{Cite journal|title=The Mechanisms of the Slippery Slope|last=Volokh|first=Eugene|authorlink=Eugene Volokh|journal=[[Harvard_Law_Review|Harvard Law Review]]|issue=4|doi=10.2307/1342743|year=2003|volume=116|pages=1026–1137|jstor=1342743|ref=harv}}</ref>相反，“温水煮青蛙综合症”这个描述有时会被用来作为维持现状的阴谋陷阱的简略表达。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/comment/columnists/guest_contributors/article437601.ece|title=What is ... 'boiling frog syndrome'?|author=David Bolchover|date=March 26, 2005|work=[[The_Times|The Times]]|location=London|ref=harv|postscript=<!-- Bot inserted parameter. Either remove it; or change its value to "." for the cite to end in a ".", as necessary. -->{{inconsistent citations}}}}</ref>

这个故事被多次引用被用来说明广泛不同的观点。包括：在1960年[[冷战|冷战]]时期对[[苏联|苏联]]的同情<ref>{{cite news|title=Report from Washington|last=Trohan|first=Walter|date=6 June 1960|newspaper=[[Chicago_Tribune|Chicago Tribune]]|page=2|quote=The frog dropped into boiling water has sense to leap out, but the frog dropped into cold water can be cooked to death before he realizes he is in serious trouble. So it is with us Americans and our civilization in this mounting crisis. We must beware of those who want to thaw the cold war out at any cost. We may be cooked before we realize what has happened.}}</ref>；1980年关于[[生存主义|生存主义]]者认为文明将会崩溃<ref>Quoted in {{cite news|title=Area Survivalists Circle Wagons for Coming Armageddon; Survivalists Prepare to Ride Out Armageddon; Fearing Economic Chaos, Advocates Store Food, Buy Gold, Silver|last=Recchia|first=Cammille|date=25 August 1980|newspaper=[[Washington_Post|Washington Post]]|page=C1|quote=That's what's happening to us. Things are getting worse and worse, so we don't really notice what's happening. Whatever happens will happen slowly, and we won't have time to jump out.}}</ref>；在20世纪90年代关于应对[[气候变化|气候变化]]和停止[[家庭暴力|家庭暴力]]<ref>{{cite journal|title=Human Effects of Climate Change: Excerpts from a Lecture Given to the Society on 26 March 1990|last=Tickell|first=Crispin|journal=[[The_Geographical_Journal|The Geographical Journal]]|issue=3|doi=10.2307/635534|year=1990|volume=156|pages=325–329 [p. 325]|quote=This is not an experiment I wish to commend, but it has lessons for another animal—ourselves. If drastic change takes place abruptly, we notice and react to it. If it takes place gradually, over a few generations, we are hardly aware of it, and by the time that we are ready to react, it can be too late.|ref=harv}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=The Verbally Abusive Relationship: How To Recognize it and How to Respond|last=Evans|first=Patricia|publisher=Adams Media|year=1996|isbn=1-55850-582-2|location=Holbrook, MA|page=111|quote=We are not inclined to notice gradual changes. This is how most partners adapt to verbal abuse. They slowly adapt until, like frog number two, they are living in an environment which is killing to their spirit.}}</ref>。它也被[[自由意志主义|自由主义]]者用以警告[[公民权利和政治权利|公民权利]]被缓慢侵蚀<ref name="volokh" />。

在1996年小说{{Le|B的故事|The Story of B}}中，环保作家{{Internal link helper/en|丹尼尔·奎因|Daniel Quinn}}用了一个章节使用温水煮青蛙作为比喻来描写人类历史，人口增长和粮食过剩<ref>{{Cite book|title=The Story of B|last=Quinn|first=Daniel|year=1996|isbn=0-553-37901-1|chapter=The Boiling Frog|ref=harv|postscript=<!-- Bot inserted parameter. Either remove it; or change its value to "." for the cite to end in a ".", as necessary. -->{{inconsistent citations}}}}</ref>。在1997年灾难电影{{Le|烈焰狂峰|Dante's Peak}}中[[皮尔斯·布鲁斯南|皮尔斯·布鲁斯南]]饰演的哈利·道尔顿（Harry Dalton）在参考积累的火山复苏的征兆分析时提到了这个效应<ref>{{cite video|people=[[Pierce_Brosnan|Pierce Brosnan]] (Star), [[Roger_Donaldson|Roger Donaldson]] (Director), [[Leslie_Bohem|Leslie Bohem]] (Writer)|date=1997|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0118928/|title=Dante's Peak|medium=Motion picture|location=USA}}</ref>。[[阿尔·戈尔|阿尔·戈尔]]在一期纽约时报使用一版[[社论对页版|社论对页版]]刊登这个故事<ref name="Gore NYT">{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1989/03/19/opinion/an-ecological-kristallnacht-listen.html|title=An Ecological Kristallnacht. Listen.|last=Gore|first=Albert|date=March 19, 1989|newspaper=New York Times|accessdate=14 Sep 2015}}</ref>，在他的演讲和2006年电影[[难以忽视的真相|难以忽视的真相]]中用这个故事来描述对[[全球变暖|全球变暖]]的无知，在电影中青蛙在受到伤害之前获救<ref>{{cite video|people=[[Al_Gore|Al Gore]] (Writer), [[Davis_Guggenheim|Davis Guggenheim]] (Director)|date=2006|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0497116/|title=An Inconvenient Truth|medium=Motion picture|location=USA}}</ref>。此引用被作家兼导演{{Le|乔恩·库克西|Jon Cooksey}}在他2010年喜剧纪录片的名称{{Le|如何煮青蛙|How to Boil a Frog}}参照讽刺<ref>{{cite video|people=[[Jon_Cooksey|Jon Cooksey]] (Writer/director)|date=2010|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1455659/|title=How to Boil a Frog|medium=Motion picture|location=Canada}}</ref>。

克里斯托弗·布鲁克迈尔（Christopher Brookmyre）使用“煮青蛙（Boiling a Frog）”作为他小说的标题<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brookmyre.co.uk/books/boiling-a-frog/|title=Boiling a Frog|date=August 8, 2002|last=Brookmyre|first=Christopher}}</ref>，小说使用使用逐渐加深的政治腐败作为背景以达到恐怖的效果。

法律教授和法律评论员{{Le|尤金·沃洛克|Eugene Volokh}}在2003年评论道不管现实中的青蛙会作何行为，煮水煮青蛙的故事作为[[隐喻|比喻]]很有用，并将它和[[鸵鸟|鸵鸟]][[鸵鸟效应|将头埋在沙子里]]的比喻做比较<ref name="volokh" />。诺贝尔经济学奖获得者和纽约时报[[社论对页版|社论对页版]]撰稿人[[保罗·克鲁格曼|保罗·克鲁格曼]]在2009年7月的专栏上引用故事作为比喻，同时指出现实中的青蛙会有不同的表现<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/13/opinion/13krugman.html|title=Boiling the Frog|last=Krugman|first=Paul|date=2009-07-13|work=The New York Times|accessdate=2010-04-26}}</ref>。自2006年以来记者{{Le|詹姆斯·法洛斯|James Fallows}}一直倡导人们停止复述这个故事，称它为一个“愚蠢的谣传”和“[[迷思|迷思]]”<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jamesfallows.theatlantic.com/archives/2006/09/the_boiledfrog_myth_stop_the_l.php|title=The boiled-frog myth: stop the lying now!|accessdate=2009-06-27|date=13 March 2007|last=Fallows|first=James|authorlink=James Fallows|work=[[The_Atlantic|The Atlantic]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jamesfallows.theatlantic.com/archives/2007/03/the_boiledfrog_myth_hey_really.php|title=The boiled-frog myth: hey, really, knock it off!|accessdate=2009-06-24|date=16 September 2006|last=Fallows|first=James|work=The Atlantic}}</ref>。但是在克鲁格曼的专栏中，他描述“煮青蛙之前的宁静”并表示引用这个故事是好的，只要你指出这个故事不是真的<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jamesfallows.theatlantic.com/archives/2009/07/peace_on_the_boiled_frog_front.php|title=Peace on the boiled frog front|date=July 13, 2009|last=Fallows|first=James|work=The Atlantic}}</ref>。

=== 哲学 ===
在[[哲学|哲学]]中煮青蛙的故事被用来作为[[连续体谬误|连续体谬误]]的一种解释。它描述从假设的一堆沙中移除沙子，一次移除一粒，是否有当它不能再被定义为一个堆的特定点。<ref>{{cite journal|title=How to boil a live frog|last=Goldstein|first=Laurence|journal=[[Analysis_(journal)|Analysis]]|issue=266|doi=10.1111/1467-8284.00220|year=2000|volume=60|pages=170–178|quote=The art of frog-boiling is an ancient one, and the correct procedure will emerge in the course of considering an ancient puzzle, the so-called 'Paradox of the Heap' or Sorites.|ref=harv}}</ref>
