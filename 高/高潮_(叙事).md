'''高潮'''（climax）或'''轉折點'''（turning point）是[[敘事|敘事]]作品中緊張局勢的最高點或戲劇結局情節的開始<ref Name="Herrick">{{cite book |author = Robert Herrick | coauthors = Lindsay Todd Damon  | title = Composition and Rhetoric for Schools  | publisher = Scott, Foresman and Co.  | date=  1902  | location = Original from Harvard University  | pages = 382  | url = http://books.google.com/books?id=XwABAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA382&dq=Climax+(narrative)+-wikipedia&as_brr=3&ie=ISO-8859-1   | doi =  | id =  }}</ref><ref Name="Fletcher">{{cite book  | author = Jefferson Butler Fletcher | coauthors = George Rice Carpenter  | title = Introduction to Theme-writing  | publisher = Allyn & Bacon  | date=  1893  | location = Original from Harvard University  | pages = 84  | url = http://books.google.com/books?id=dP0AAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA84&dq=Climax+(narrative)+-wikipedia&as_brr=3&ie=ISO-8859-1  | doi =  | id =  }}</ref>。climax的詞源為希臘語「{{lang|el|κλῖμαξ}}」（拉丁轉寫klimax），意思是「樓梯」、「梯子」。

== 小說 ==
在散文小說作品，高潮往往像是古典喜劇，發生在文本或表演接近尾聲之時。

高潮包括三個要素。最重要的因素是，主角經歷變革。第二是主要角色發現一些關於自己和其他未知角色的事情。最後一個因素是揭示主題本身。
