'''馬賽語'''(Maasai、Masai) {{IPAc-en|ˈ|m|ɑː|s|aɪ}}<ref>Laurie Bauer, 2007, ''The Linguistics Student’s Handbook'', Edinburgh</ref> (''[[當地地名與外來地名|當地名]]'': ɔl Maa)為[[东部非洲|东部非洲]][[马赛人|马赛人]]所使用的語言、屬於[[東尼羅語系|東尼羅語系]](Eastern Nilotic languages)，通行於[[肯尼亚|肯尼亚]]南部及[[坦桑尼亚|坦桑尼亚]]北部，使用人數約800,000人。馬賽語接進肯尼亞中部[[桑布鲁人|桑布鲁人]]所使用[[馬語系|馬語系]](Maa languages)的[[桑布魯語|桑布魯語]](Samburu language, Sampur)、[[巴林戈湖|巴林戈湖]]東南部及南部所使用的[[查木斯語|查木斯語]](Chamus language，有時亦視為桑布魯語的方言)，及坦桑尼亚的[[帕拉庫尤語|帕拉庫尤語]](Parakuyu)。馬賽人，桑布魯人，伊爾查木斯人(il-Chamus)及帕拉庫尤人(Parakuyu)在歷史上均為相關的族群，它們的語言均通稱為「歐馬語」({{Unicode|ɔl Maa}}/)。

==語音系統==
馬賽語為"歐馬語"(ɔl Maa)的變異種，通行於[[肯尼亚|肯尼亚]]南部及[[坦桑尼亚|坦桑尼亚]]，有30個互相對立的[[音位|音位]]，音位以字母順序表達如次:''a'', ''b'', ''ch''(''sh'' 的變異), ''d'', ''e'', ''ɛ'', ''g'', ''h'', ''i'', ''ɨ'', ''j'', ''k'', ''l'', ''m'', ''n'', ''ny'', ''ŋ'', ''o'', ''ɔ'', ''p'', ''r'', ''rr'', ''s'', ''sh'' (為 ''ch'' 的變異), ''t'', ''u'', ''ʉ'', ''w'', ''wu'' (或 ''ww''), ''y'', ''yi'' (或 ''yy'')，及[[聲門塞音|聲門塞音]] ''<nowiki>'</nowiki>'' (或 ''ʔ'')。

[[聲調語言|聲調]]在馬賽語裡對於表達正確的語義而言是非常重要的。

For some speakers the voiced stops may be realized as [[implosive_consonant|implosive consonant]]s, but often the implosion is very light to nonexistent.

===元音===
Like the other Maa languages, Maasai has [[Advanced_and_retracted_tongue_root|advanced tongue root]] [[vowel_harmony|vowel harmony]]. There are nine contrasting vowels, with the vowel {{IPAslink|a}} being "neutral" for harmony.

==句型==
Word order is usually [[verb–subject–object|verb–subject–object]], though order can vary because tone is the most important indicator of subject versus object. What really determines order in a clause is topicality; order in the most simple clauses can be predicted according to the information structure pattern: [Verb – Most.Topical – Less.Topical]. Thus, if the object is highly topical in the discourse (e.g. a first-person pronoun), and the subject is less topical, then the object will occur right after the verb and before the subject.

The Maasai language has only two fully grammatical [[prepositions|prepositions]], but can use "relational nouns" along with a most general preposition to designate specific locative ideas. [[Noun_phrase|Noun phrase]]s begin with a [[demonstrative|demonstrative]] prefix or a gender-number prefix, followed by a quantifying noun or other head noun. Other modifiers follow the head noun, including [[possessive|possessive]] phrases.

In Maasai, many morphemes are actually tone patterns. For example:
