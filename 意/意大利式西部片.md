'''意大利式西部片'''（{{lang-en|Spaghetti Western}}，直譯為「'''意粉西部片'''」）是[[西部片|西部片]]的其中一種[[類型|類型]]，泛指一些出現在1960年代、由[[意大利|意大利]]人導演及監製（多與[[西班牙|西班牙]]或[[德國|德國]]聯合製片）的西部片。

此類電影拍攝成員如下：意大利人執導，演員及其餘皆為意大利及西班牙人，中間可能有過氣或初露頭角的[[荷里活|荷里活]]影星（如《[[獨行俠三部曲|獨行俠三部曲]]》中的[[奇連·伊士活|奇連·伊士活]]）參與演出。拍攝地點則多是看來與[[美國西南部|美國西南部]]很相似，但成本卻低很多的地方，如[[西班牙|西班牙]]的[[安達盧西亞|安達盧西亞]]及[[阿爾梅里亞|阿爾梅里亞]]或[[意大利|意大利]]的[[薩丁尼亞島|薩丁尼亞島]]及[[阿布魯佐|阿布魯佐]]。

因電影中的[[臨時演員|臨時演員]]多是說[[西班牙語|西班牙語]]的，此類電影常把部份故事設定在[[美國|美國]]和[[墨西哥|墨西哥]]的[[美墨邊境|交界]]，亦會用上不少如[[墨西哥革命|墨西哥革命]]等與[[墨西哥|墨西哥]]有關的題材。

1964年意大利人[[赛尔乔·莱翁内|赛尔乔·莱翁内]]在西班牙拍成了他第一部意大利式西部片《[[荒野大鏢客|荒野大鏢客]]》，并捧红了[[克林特·伊斯特伍德|克林特·伊斯特伍德]]。从此在意大利电影圈，许多导演都开始采用意大利演员，多选择西班牙为外景，拍摄这种表现美国西部和墨西哥蛮荒时代的草莽英雄片，并且大肆颠覆了传统电影的英雄形象，形成独具一格的暴力美学类型。

在[[赛尔乔·莱翁内|赛尔乔·莱翁内]]的西部片中，人物已不再善恶分明，而是沾染了黑色电影的脾性，反英雄，非主流，反乌托邦，颠覆传统。每个人都不是传统上的正面角色。

== 歷史 ==
<!---Originally, spaghetti westerns were characterized by their production in the Italian language, low budgets, and a recognizable highly fluid and [[minimalist|minimalist]] cinematography which eschewed (even "demythologized"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmsite.org/westernfilms5.html|title=Western Films-Sergio Leone's 'Spaghetti' Westerns|last=Dirks|first=Tim|publisher=American Movie Classics Company LLC.|accessdate=2009-02-24}}</ref>) many of the conventions of earlier Westerns. This was partly intentional and partly the context of a different cultural background.{{Citation needed|date=November 2009}}

A favorite locale in Andalusia was the [[Tabernas_Desert|Tabernas Desert]] of [[Almería_(province)|Almería]], with production at three main studios, [[Texas_Hollywood|Texas Hollywood]], [[Mini_Hollywood|Mini Hollywood]], and [[Western_Leone|Western Leone]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2009}}--->

== 主要電影 ==
{{Main|:en:List of spaghetti western films}}<!---{{main|意大利式西部片列表}}
The movie that qualifies as the first spaghetti western, ''[[Savage_Guns_(1961_film)|Tierra brutal]]'' (1961), showed no Italian involvement at all, being a British-Spanish coproduction, but was shot in Almería and featured the very heterogeneous cast typical of later movies in the genre (in this instance combining American actors [[Richard_Basehart|Richard Basehart]] and [[Alex_Nicol|Alex Nicol]] with the Spanish ''folclóricas'' [[Paquita_Rico|Paquita Rico]] and [[María_Granada|María Granada]]), and directed by English [[horror_films|horror films]] specialist, [[Michael_Carreras|Michael Carreras]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2009}}

The best-known and perhaps archetypal films were the "[[Man_with_No_Name|Man with No Name]]" trilogy (or "[[Dollars_Trilogy|Dollars Trilogy]]") directed by [[Sergio_Leone|Sergio Leone]], starring [[Clint_Eastwood|Clint Eastwood]] and with the musical scores of [[Ennio_Morricone|Ennio Morricone]]: ''[[A_Fistful_of_Dollars|A Fistful of Dollars]]'' (1964), ''[[For_a_Few_Dollars_More|For a Few Dollars More]]'' (1965), and ''[[The_Good,_the_Bad_and_the_Ugly|The Good, the Bad and the Ugly]]'' (1966). Atypically for the genre, the last had a relatively high budget, over a million [[United_States_dollar|United States dollar]]s.{{Citation needed|date=November 2009}} Leone's next film after the "trilogy" was ''[[Once_Upon_a_Time_in_the_West|Once Upon a Time in the West]]'', which is often lumped in with the previous three for its similar style and accompanying score by Morricone, though Eastwood was not involved.{{Citation needed|date=November 2009}}--->
