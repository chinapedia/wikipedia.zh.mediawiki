'''音區'''（{{lang|en|register}}、音域）或稱'''音高音區'''（{{lang|en|pitch register}}）在[[音位學|音位學]]中表示在一些語言裡的[[音節|音節]][[韻律_(語言學)|韻律]]特徵，比如[[聲調|聲調]]，元音[[發聲態|發聲態]]，[[喉化音|喉化音]]，或依賴於對方音調的類似功能。[[緬甸語|緬甸語]]、[[越南語|越南語]]和[[吴语|吴语]]裡均有如此的音區系統。

===越南語===
同樣，在一些[[越南語|越南語]]的聲調上在"音高"（pitch）有很大程度上的區分。比如，在越南北部<''ngã''>的聲調不同於<''sắc''>聲調、其原因是元音上的聲門調之顯現；然而，<''nặng''>和<''huyền''>的音節不同、主要出於短[[緊喉音|緊喉音]]（Creaky voice）元音與長[[氣聲|氣聲]]元音的區別。

===高棉語===
[[Khmer_language|Khmer]] is sometimes considered to be a register language. It's also been called a "restructured register language" because both its pitch and phonation can be considered allophonic: If they are ignored, the phonemic distinctions they carry remain as a difference in [[diphthong|diphthong]]s and [[vowel_length|vowel length]].
===亞洲語言之外===
An example of a non-Asian language with register distinctions is [[Latvian_language|Latvian]], at least in the central dialects underlying the standard form.  Long vowels in stressed syllables are often said to take one of three [[pitch_accent|pitch accent]]s, conventionally called ''rising'', ''falling'', and ''broken''.  However, the "broken tone" is distinguished not by pitch but by the presence of a glottalization, much like the ''ngã'' register of Northern Vietnamese.-->
