'''遠端腎小管性酸中毒'''({{lang|en|Distal renal tubular acidosis}}、{{lang|en|dRTA}}、或"1型腎小管酸中毒"(RTA 1))是RTA的傳統形式，為RTA第一個描述的病症。遠端RTA的特徵在於[[人体解剖学方位|遠端]][[腎單位|腎單位]]的[[集合管系統|集合管系統]](Collecting duct system)的"α-閏[[細胞|細胞]]"(alpha intercalated cells)酸分泌的失敗。這種酸分泌的失敗可能是由於多種原因，且會導致沒有能力來酸化尿液使其[[pH值|pH值]]小於<5.3>。

==症狀==
因為腎排泄是從身體消除酸的主要手段，因此朝向[[酸中毒|酸中毒]](Acidosis)的傾向論。這會導致dRTA的臨床特徵；<ref name=laing>{{cite journal |author=Laing CM, Toye AM, Capasso G, Unwin RJ |title=Renal tubular acidosis: developments in our understanding of the molecular basis |journal=Int. J. Biochem. Cell Biol. |volume=37 |issue=6 |pages=1151–61 |year=2005|pmid=15778079 |doi=10.1016/j.biocel.2005.01.002 |url=}}</ref>

The symptoms and sequelae of dRTA are variable and range from being completely [[asymptomatic|asymptomatic]], to [[loin|loin]] pain and [[hematuria|hematuria]] from [[kidney_stones|kidney stones]], to [[failure_to_thrive|failure to thrive]] and severe [[rickets|rickets]] in childhood forms as well as possible [[renal_failure|renal failure]] and even death.

dRTA commonly leads to sodium loss and volume contraction, which causes a compensatory increase in blood levels of [[aldosterone|aldosterone]].<ref name=Wein_Urology1045>{{cite book|last=Wein|first=Alan, J|title=Campbell-walsh Urology Expert Consult.|year=2011|publisher=W B Saunders Co|location=Philadelphia, PA|isbn=978-1-4160-6911-9|pages=1045|url=http://www.mdconsult.com/das/book/pdf/282455359-3/978-1-4160-6911-9/4-u1.0-B978-1-4160-6911-9..00038-4..DOCPDF.pdf?isbn=978-1-4160-6911-9&eid=4-u1.0-B978-1-4160-6911-9..00038-4..DOCPDF|edition=10th}}</ref> Aldosterone causes increased resorption of sodium and loss of potassium in the collecting duct of the kidney, so these increased aldosterone levels cause the hypokalemia which is a common symptom of dRTA.<ref name=Wein_Urology1045 />-->

The acidosis is variable, and one may have dRTA with [[alpha_intercalated_cell|alpha intercalated cell]] failure without necessarily being acidemic; this is termed ''incomplete dRTA''. The diagnosis of dRTA can be made by the observation of a urinary [[pH|pH]] of greater than 5.3 in the face of a systemic acidemia (usually taken to be a serum bicarbonate of 20 [[mmol|mmol]]/[[litre|l]] or less). In the case of an incomplete dRTA, failure to acidify the urine following an oral acid loading challenge is often used as a test.  The test usually performed is ''the short [[ammonium_chloride|ammonium chloride]] test'',<ref>{{cite journal |quotes= |last=Wrong |first= O|authorlink= |author2=Davies HEF |year= 1959|title=The Excretion of Acid in Renal Disease |journal=QJM |volume= 28|issue= 110|pages=259–313 |id= |url= |accessdate= |pmid= 13658353 }}</ref> in which ammonium chloride capsules are used as the acid load. More recently, an alternative test using [[furosemide|furosemide]] and [[fludrocortisone|fludrocortisone]] has been described.<ref>{{cite journal |author=Walsh SB, Shirley DG, Wrong OM, Unwin RJ |title=Urinary acidification assessed by simultaneous furosemide and fludrocortisone treatment: an alternative to ammonium chloride |journal=Kidney Int. |volume=71 |issue=12 |pages=1310–6 |year=2007 |pmid=17410104 |doi=10.1038/sj.ki.5002220 |url=}}</ref>

==治療==
This is relatively straightforward. It involves correction of the acidemia with oral [[sodium_bicarbonate|sodium bicarbonate]], [[Trisodium_citrate|sodium citrate]] or [[potassium_citrate|potassium citrate]]. This will correct the acidemia and reverse bone demineralisation. Hypokalemia and urinary stone formation and nephrocalcinosis can be treated with [[potassium_citrate|potassium citrate]] tablets which not only replace potassium but also inhibit calcium excretion and thus do not exacerbate stone disease as sodium bicarbonate or citrate may do.<ref>{{cite journal |author=Morris RC, Sebastian A |title=Alkali therapy in renal tubular acidosis: who needs it? |journal=J.
