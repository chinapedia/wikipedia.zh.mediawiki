《'''聖母悼歌'''》（[[拉丁文|拉丁文]]：Stabat Mater Dolorosa，或簡寫為 Stabat Mater），是13世紀[[天主教會|天主教會]]稱頌[[馬利亞_(耶穌的母親)|馬利亞 (耶穌的母親)]]的一首歌，其作者有幾種不同說法，包括{{link-en|聖法蘭西斯教團|Order of Saint Francis}}的{{link-en|雅各布内·達·托迪|Jacopone da Todi}}及[[教宗|教宗]][[依諾增爵三世|依諾增爵三世]]。該歌內容與[[痛苦聖母|痛苦聖母]]有關。<ref name="Sabatier, Paul 1919, page 286">Sabatier, Paul ''Life of St. Francis Assisi'' Charles Scribner Press, NY, 1919, page 286</ref><ref name="Mediaeval Church' page 96">''The seven great hymns of the Mediaeval Church'' by Charles Cooper Nott 1868 ASIN: B003KCW2LA page 96</ref><ref>p. 574, Alighieri, Durling, Martinez (2003) Dante, Robert M., Ronald L. Oxford ''The Divine Comedy of Dante Alighieri: Purgatorio Volume 2 of The Divine Comedy of Dante Alighieri'' Oxford University Press. "The Stabat Mater by the Franciscan Jacopone da Todi."</ref>

Stabat mater dolorosa<br>
juxta Crucem lacrimosa,<br>
dum pendebat Filius.

Cuius animam gementem,<br>
contristatam et dolentem<br>
pertransivit gladius.

O quam tristis et afflicta<br>
fuit illa benedicta,<br>
mater Unigeniti!

Quae mœrebat et dolebat,<br>
pia Mater, dum videbat<br>
nati pœnas inclyti.

Quis est homo qui non fleret,<br>
matrem Christi si videret<br>
in tanto supplicio?

Quis non posset contristari<br>
Christi Matrem contemplari<br>
dolentem cum Filio?

Pro peccatis suæ gentis<br>
vidit Iesum in tormentis,<br>
et flagellis subditum.

Vidit suum dulcem Natum<br>
moriendo desolatum,<br>
dum emisit spiritum.

Eia, Mater, fons amoris<br>
me sentire vim doloris<br>
fac, ut tecum lugeam.

Fac, ut ardeat cor meum<br>
in amando Christum Deum<br>
ut sibi complaceam.

Sancta Mater, istud agas,<br>
crucifixi fige plagas<br>
cordi meo valide.

Tui Nati vulnerati,<br>
tam dignati pro me pati,<br>
pœnas mecum divide.

Fac me tecum pie flere,<br>
crucifixo condolere,<br>
donec ego vixero.

Juxta Crucem tecum stare,<br>
et me tibi sociare<br>
in planctu desidero.

Virgo virginum præclara,<br>
mihi iam non sis amara,<br>
fac me tecum plangere.

Fac, ut portem Christi mortem,<br>
passionis fac consortem,<br>
et plagas recolere.

Fac me plagis vulnerari,<br>
fac me Cruce inebriari,<br>
et cruore Filii.

Flammis ne urar succensus,<br>
per te, Virgo, sim defensus<br>
in die iudicii.

Christe, cum sit hinc exire,<br>
da per Matrem me venire<br>
ad palmam victoriæ.

Quando corpus morietur,<br>
fac, ut animæ donetur<br>
paradisi gloria. Amen.

英譯

At the Cross her station keeping,<br>
stood the mournful Mother weeping,<br>
close to her Son to the last.

Through her heart, His sorrow sharing,<br>
all His bitter anguish bearing,<br>
now at length the sword has passed.

O how sad and sore distressed<br>
was that Mother, highly blest,<br>
of the sole-begotten One.

Christ above in torment hangs,<br>
she beneath beholds the pangs<br>
of her dying glorious Son.

Is there one who would not weep,<br>
whelmed in miseries so deep,<br>
Christ's dear Mother to behold?

Can the human heart refrain<br>
from partaking in her pain,<br>
in that Mother's pain untold?

For the sins of His own nation,<br>
She saw Jesus wracked with torment,<br>
All with scourges rent:

She beheld her tender Child,<br>
Saw Him hang in desolation,<br>
Till His spirit forth He sent.

O thou Mother! fount of love!<br>
Touch my spirit from above,<br>
make my heart with thine accord:

Make me feel as thou hast felt;<br>
make my soul to glow and melt<br>
with the love of Christ my Lord.

Holy Mother! pierce me through,<br>
in my heart each wound renew<br>
of my Savior crucified:

Let me share with thee His pain,<br>
who for all my sins was slain,<br>
who for me in torments died.

Let me mingle tears with thee,<br>
mourning Him who mourned for me,<br>
all the days that I may live:

By the Cross with thee to stay,<br>
there with thee to weep and pray,<br>
is all I ask of thee to give.

Virgin of all virgins blest!,<br>
Listen to my fond request:<br>
let me share thy grief divine;

Let me, to my latest breath,<br>
in my body bear the death<br>
of that dying Son of thine.

Wounded with His every wound,<br>
steep my soul till it hath swooned,<br>
in His very Blood away;

Be to me, O Virgin, nigh,<br>
lest in flames I burn and die,<br>
in His awful Judgment Day.

Christ, when Thou shalt call me hence,<br>
be Thy Mother my defense,<br>
be Thy Cross my victory;

While my body here decays,<br>
may my soul Thy goodness praise,<br>
Safe in Paradise with Thee.
