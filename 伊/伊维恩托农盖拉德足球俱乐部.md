'''伊維恩托農蓋拉德足球會'''（[[法語|法語]]：'''Evian Thonon Gaillard Football Club'''，{{IPA-fr|evjɑ̃ tɔnɔ̃ ɡajaʁ}}，簡稱'''Évian TG'''或更簡單的'''Évian'''）是原本位於[[法國|法國]]東南部鄰近[[瑞士|瑞士]]邊界及[[日内瓦|日内瓦]]的[[罗纳-阿尔卑斯|罗纳-阿尔卑斯]][[大区|大区]][[上萨瓦省|上萨瓦省]]的[[市镇_(法国)|市鎮]]蓋拉德（Gaillard）的[[足球會|足球會]]，於2007年搬遷到托農萊班（Thonon-les-Bains）。現時在[[法国足球乙级联赛|法国足球乙级联赛]]參賽。

伊維恩以「''Football Croix-de-Savoie 74''」的名義成立，其後經歷兩次合併。首次化身成為「FC蓋拉德」（''FC Gaillard''），存在於1924年至2003年之間，期間只獲得有限度的榮譽，僅於1999年奪得地區性聯賽冠軍。伊維恩在2007年成立後取得較大的成就，只花了3季便升上[[職業|職業]]聯賽。球隊於2008年贏得法國業餘足球聯賽冠軍，於2009/10年球季以[[法国足球丙级联赛|法丙]]冠軍身份升班[[法國足球乙級聯賽|法乙]]，隨即再奪冠而連續兩年升班到[[法国足球甲级联赛|法甲]]作賽<ref>{{cite news |language=zh-hk |url=http://hk.apple.nextmedia.com/template/apple/art_main.php?iss_id=20110614&sec_id=25391&subsec_id=6041&art_id=15338851&cat_id=628 |title=體育通識：伊維恩年年升班 |publisher=[[蘋果日報|''hk.apple.nextmedia.com'']] |date=2011-06-14 |accessdate=2011-06-14}}</ref>。

伊維恩原採用球會基地的[[約瑟夫-摩歷球場|約瑟夫-摩歷球場]]（Stade Joseph-Moynat）進行主場賽事，但於升班[[法乙|法乙]]後球場不符合[[法國職業足球聯賽|法國職業足球聯賽]]的規格，於2010/11年球季開始暫時遷往鄰近[[阿讷西|阿讷西]]的[[阿訥西體育公園|體育公園球場]]（Parc des Sports），球會正計劃興建新球場或改建約瑟夫-摩歷球場。在搬到現時的球場前，伊維恩曾尋求借用鄰近的[[日内瓦|日内瓦]]的[[日内瓦体育场|日内瓦体育场]]。

== 歷史 ==
Évian Thonon Gaillard Football Club was formed in 2003 under the name ''Football Croix-de-Savoie 74'' as a result of a merger between ''FC Gaillard'' and ''FC Ville-la-Grand''. Gaillard was founded in 1924 and spent most of its life playing in the ''Ligue Rhône-Alpes'', while Ville-la-Grand was founded in 1928. The new club finished 3rd in the [[Championnat_de_France_amateur|Championnat de France amateur]] (CFA) Groupe B section for the 2003–04 season. Normally, only the top club in each of the four amateur groups are promoted to the [[Championnat_National|Championnat National]]; however, both the 1st and 2nd placed clubs in the group were reserve sides of professional teams, namely [[Olympique_Lyonnais|Lyon]] and [[FC_Metz|Metz]]. As such, Croix-de-Savoie was promoted.<ref>{{cite news |title=French Football 2003/04 |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesf/fran04.html |work=[[RSSSF|RSSSF]]|date= |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref> The club's first season in National saw them narrowly avoid relegation, finishing 14th out of 20, two points above relegated 17th place [[Besançon_RC|Besançon]].<ref>{{cite news |title=French Football 2004/05 |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesf/fran05.html |work=[[RSSSF|RSSSF]]|date= |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref> The 2005–06 season was less successful; Croix-de-Savoie finished 18th with 41 points, one point behind [[SO_Châtellerault|SO Châtellerault]], thus falling back to the fourth division.<ref>{{cite news |title=French Football 3rd Tier 2005/06 |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesf/fran3-06.html |work=[[RSSSF|RSSSF]]|date= |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref> Croix-de-Savoie's average attendance also dropped from 933 to 716.<ref>{{cite news |title=European Football Statistics – France 2004/05 |url=http://www.european-football-statistics.co.uk/attn/2005/avefra.htm |work=European Football Statistics|date= |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref>

In 2007, the club underwent a further merger, this time with local team ''Olympique Thonon-Chablais''; the club's name changed once again, with it becoming known as ''Olympique Croix-de-Savoie 74''. Whilst the former club had been heavily associated with the [[Communes_of_France|commune]] of [[Gaillard|Gaillard]], the new club moved to the nearby [[Communes_(France)|commune]] of [[Thonon-les-Bains|Thonon-les-Bains]] because the stadium in Gaillard was, in August 2005, deemed unfit for use in the National division. The decision resulted in club being forced to move to [[Stade_Joseph-Moynat|Stade Joseph-Moynat]] in Thonon, a facility with 2,700 seats and a capacity of 6,000. The club returned to the Championnat National as the CFA Group B winners, for the 2008–09 season. In the summer of 2009, the president of the [[Groupe_Danone|Groupe Danone]], [[Franck_Riboud|Franck Riboud]], was made honorary president of the football club. Riboud yet again changed the name of the team to ''Évian Thonon Gaillard Football Club''. He also put money into the team in order to improve the youth system of the club and harbored aspirations of the side achieving promotion to [[Ligue_2|Ligue 2]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Historique |url=http://www.etgfc.com/club/lhistorique/ |work=Évian Thonon Gaillard FC|date= |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref> On 16 April 2010, the club completed the feat in Riboud's first season presiding over the club achieving promotion to [[Ligue_2|Ligue 2]], for the first time, following its 1–0 victory over [[Amiens_SC|Amiens]].

After earning promotion to [[Ligue_2|Ligue 2]] for the 2010–11 season, Évian were rumored to be pursuing a move to play its home matches at the [[Stade_de_Genève|Stade de la Praille]] in [[Geneva|Geneva]], Switzerland after it was determined that the club's current facility, the [[Stade_Joseph-Moynat|Stade Joseph-Moynat]], did not meet the [[Ligue_de_Football_Professionnel|Ligue de Football Professionnel]]'s (LFP) standards. [[Thonon-les-Bains|Thonon-les-Bains]], the [[Communes_of_France|commune]] where the club situates itself, is a few kilometers from the Swiss border and is only {{convert|34.6|km|mi}}, a 45 minute car drive, from the city of Geneva. It was reported that the club's president, Patrick Trotignon, had been in the process of advocating for the move since the beginning of the [[2009–10_Championnat_National|2009–10 Championnat National]] season just in case the club had achieved promotion to the second division. The vice-president of Swiss club [[Servette_FC|Servette FC]], who occupy the stadium, questioned the move citing possible schedule conflicts, as well as the health of the pitch if both clubs were to use the stadium on a weekly basis.<ref>{{cite news |title=Evian-Thonon-Gaillard au Stade de Genève, Servette ne peut pas s’y opposer |url=http://www.tdg.ch/actu/sports/evian-thonon-gaillard-stade-geneve-servette-ne-opposer-2010-04-14 |work=Tribune de Geneve|publisher=TDG|date=15 April 2010 |accessdate=20 May 2010 }}</ref> However, his claims were refuted by Benoît Genecand, who serves as president of Fondation du Stade de Genève (FSG), which owns and operates the facility. The club responded immediately to Genecand's comments via a [[press_release|press release]] posted on the club's official website.<ref>{{cite news |title=Servette FC et le Stade de Genève |url=http://www.servettefc.ch/fr/actualite/article-3090.html |work=Servette FC|publisher=Servette Football Club|date=15 April 2010 |accessdate=21 May 2010 }}</ref> Évian petitioned to the State Council of Geneva and obtained approval from the LFP for the move in early May. On 20 May 2010, Évian received a favorable ruling from the [[French_Football_Federation|French Football Federation]] (FFF) with the Federal Council voting in favor of the move. According to the federation, the move now had to be agreed upon by a [[UEFA|UEFA]] executive committee, which is composed of seventeen officials.<ref>{{cite news |title=Evian-Thonon-Gaillard se rapproche du Stade de Genève! |url=http://www.tdg.ch/geneve/sports/evian-thonon-gaillard-jouera-stade-geneve-2010-05-20 |work=Tribune de Geneve|publisher=TDG|date=20 May 2010 |accessdate=20 May 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Evian se rapproche de Genève|url=http://www.francefootball.fr/FF/breves2010/20100520_184115_evian-se-rapproche-de-geneve.html |work=[[France_Football|France Football]]|date=20 May 2010 |accessdate=20 May 2010 }}</ref> On 8 June, UEFA officially denied Évian's request to play at the Stade de la Praille meaning the club will likely play its home matches at the [[Parc_des_Sports|Parc des Sports]] in nearby [[Annecy|Annecy]].<ref>{{cite news |title=L'ETGFC jouera à Annecy  |url=http://www.francefootball.fr/FF/breves2010/20100608_111907_l-etgfc-jouera-a-annecy.html |work=France Football|publisher=France Football|date=8 June 2010 |accessdate=8 June 2010 }}</ref>

On 9 January 2011, Évian recorded an upset victory over the [[2009–10_Ligue_1|defending French champions]] [[Olympique_de_Marseille|Marseille]] in the [[Coupe_de_France|Coupe de France]]. The team defeated the Ligue 1 club 3–1 in the Round of 64.<ref>{{cite news |title=Marseille caught cold by Evian in Coupe  |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/news/story/_/id/863498/marseille-caught-cold-by-evian-in-coupe?cc=5901 |work=[[ESPN|ESPN]]|date=10 January 2011 |accessdate=15 January 2011 }}</ref> Strong form throughout the season saw Évian secure a second successive promotion as champions of Ligue 2.
