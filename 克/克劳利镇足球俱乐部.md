'''克劳利镇足球俱乐部'''（{{lang-en|'''Crawley Town Football Club'''}}）是位於[[英格蘭|英格蘭]][[東南英格蘭|東南部]][[西薩塞克斯|西薩塞克斯郡]][[英格蘭行政區劃|二級行政區]][[克劳利|克劳利]]的[[足球會|足球會]]，於1896年成立，初時稱為「克拉雷足球會」，直到1958年才加上「鎮」成為現今的名稱，1962年轉為半[[職業|職業]]球隊，克拉雷鎮[[格言|格言]]為[[拉丁語|拉丁語]]：「''NOLI CEDERE''」，意譯為「永不放棄」。於2011年由[[英格蘭足球議會全國聯賽|議會全國聯賽]]取得冠軍錦標而直接升班到[[英格蘭足球乙級聯賽|英格蘭足球乙級聯賽]]作賽，[[2011年至2012年英格蘭足球乙級聯賽|翌季]]背對背升班到[[英格蘭足球甲級聯賽|英格蘭足球甲級聯賽]]。

2011年1月29日在[[英格蘭足總盃|英格蘭足總盃]]第四圈作客1-0淘汰[[托奎联足球俱乐部|-{zh-hans:托奎联; zh-hk:托基聯;}-]]，成為近17年來首支晉級足總盃第五圈的非聯賽球隊<ref>{{cite news |language=en |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/fa_cup/9379079.stm |title=Torquay 0-1 Crawley Town |publisher=''[[BBC_Sport|BBC Sport]]'' |date=2011-01-29 |accessdate=2011-01-30}}</ref>；2月19日在第五圈作客0-1僅負於[[曼徹斯特聯足球俱樂部|曼聯]]被淘汰<ref>{{cite news |language=en |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/fa_cup/9401072.stm |title=Man Utd 1-0 Crawley Town |publisher=''[[BBC_Sport|BBC Sport]]'' |date=2011-02-19 |accessdate=2011-02-21}}</ref>。

成立於1896年的克拉雷鎮綽號紅魔鬼（Red Devils）， 近年亦因獲注資後大肆收購球員而被球迷及媒體戲稱為「非聯賽曼城」（The Manchester City of Non League）。

==主場球場==
[[File:BroadfieldStadium.jpg|thumb]]
克劳利镇在舊有的「''Town Mead''」渡過48年，直到1997年將球場出售給發展商後才離開。球隊隨即搬遷到距離市區兩[[英哩|英哩]]，新建可容4,996名觀眾(1,150座位)的「布罗德菲尔德体育场」（Broadfield Stadium），球場由「克劳利區議會」（Crawley Borough Council）所擁有。

2003/04年克拉雷鎮的冠軍球季，其主場對最終獲得亞軍[[韋茅夫足球會|韋茅夫]]（Weymouth）的賽事吸引破紀錄的4,522名球迷進場觀戰，克拉雷鎮以2-1取得勝利。卡維尼正準備重建其主場球場的東看台，增加2,150個座位及設置新的旋轉門、廁所和販賣區，同時改善球場的照明和排水能力，計劃順利的話工程會於2012年2月完成。

==財務危機==
Although Crawley had been in administration in the late 1990s, trouble began in March 2006 when Crawley Town players and staff were made to take a 50% pay cut and the entire squad was put up for sale, the club went into administration in June of that year.<ref name="news.bbc.co.uk">{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/crawley_town/4779696.stm | work=BBC News | title=Crawley squad have wages slashed | date=6 March 2006 | accessdate=30 April 2010}}</ref> It was revealed by [[The_Argus_(Brighton)|The Argus]] several days later that the Chairman Chas Majeed was an undischarged bankrupt and therefore banned from holding a high position within the club.<ref>http://archive.theargus.co.uk/2006/3/10/208759.html</ref>. Majeed later resigned from his post but remained on in some capacity.

The Reds fans started a "Red Card" campaign in order to remove Chas Majeed and his owner brother Azwar Majeed from the club. It was revealed that the club owed around £1.1million with nearly £400,000 owed to [[HM_Revenue_and_Customs|HM Revenue and Customs]], and the Majeeds claimed they were owed £700,000 by the club. By July of that year it was revealed that the debts were closer to £1.4 million, including money owed to current and former staff, and an offer of 25 pence in the pound was rejected by the creditors. With [[HM_Revenue_and_Customs|HM Revenue and Customs]] unwilling to move on their demand and being the biggest creditor (the Majeeds were unable to vote on the matter despite being owed money), it looked unlikely that the club would be saved.

By August 2006, only one bid had come in for the club which was from the current owners; it was rejected by 3 to 1, and the administrators were therefore obliged to liquidise the club and it was announced that the club would fold later that day.<ref name="clubsincrisis.com">http://www.clubsincrisis.com/Club_Detail.asp?Key=102</ref> However, a couple of days later, in one final attempt to rescue the club, the creditors met again to decide on a final offer. The creditors were split and so the administrator voted in Crawley Town's favour, allowing them to start the new season. The creditors would meet 14 days later to decide on an offer of 50 pence in the pound. The club was told that if the offer was rejected then the club would fold there and then, and there would be no going back.<ref name="clubsincrisis.com"/> By September 2006 the club's debt was at £1.8 million making former/current players and staff the biggest creditors and giving hope that a rescue bid would be accepted. The offer of 50 pence in the pound was later accepted and Crawley Town were able to continue playing for now, though this didn't stop [[HM_Revenue_and_Customs|HM Revenue and Customs]] from trying to wind up the SAGroup (Majeed's company) over unpaid taxes.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/crawley_town/5295456.stm | work=BBC News | title=Crawley creditors back rescue bid | date=30 August 2006 | accessdate=30 April 2010}}</ref>. Azwar Majeed was later jailed for tax fraud relating to his various other businesses.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/sussex/7988739.stm | work=BBC News | title=Ex-football boss jailed for fraud | date=7 April 2009 | accessdate=30 April 2010}}</ref>

In May 2007, it was confirmed that all of Crawley Town's debts had been cleared. However, complications arose, and, as a result, Crawley were given a six-point penalty for the new season and a transfer embargo was put in place due to financial irregularities.<ref>http://www.clubsincrisis.com/crawley_town.html</ref> It is believed that Crawley failed to confirm to the league that the debts had been paid.

After a short period of stability, Crawley Town ended up in the High Court in London to face a winding-up order on 17 February 2010; again this was due to money being owed to HMRC. The hearing was adjourned until 17 March 2010.<ref>http://www.thisissussex.co.uk/crawley/news/Crawley-Town-given-breathing-space-winding-order-adjourned/article-1841616-detail/article.html</ref> The latest setback appeared to be the end of Crawley Town, as the club has very few assets to sell in order to satisfy the debt. However, the case was later dismissed by the High Court as they proved the debt had been paid. <ref>http://footballmanagement.wordpress.com/2010/03/17/crawley-town-and-farnborough-in-court-today/</ref>

On 29th March 2010,  Crawley Borough Council agreed to lease The Broadfield Stadium to Crawley Town F.C., a move in recognition of the confidence in the new management and to help secure the long-term sustainability of the club. <ref>http://www.crawleyobserver.co.uk/news/BREAKING-NEWS-Crawley-Town-FC.6188154.jp</ref>

At a press conference on 1st July 2010, co owner Bruce Winfield stated that all of the clubs debts had been cleared. He further revealed that friends/business acquaintances of his had come onboard as passive shareholders thus meaning the club had ''money in the bank''.
