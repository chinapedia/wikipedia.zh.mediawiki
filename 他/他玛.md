'''他玛''' （{{lang-he|תָּמָר|Tamar|Tāmār|[[海枣|海枣]]}}）是《旧约圣经·创世纪》中记载的一个人物，是[[犹大_(雅各第四子)|犹大]]的儿媳，先后嫁给他的两个儿子，也是孪生子[[法勒斯|法勒斯]]和[[谢拉|谢拉]]的母亲<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章29-30节|}}</ref>。在《撒母耳記》中，有另一位[[他瑪_(大衛之女)|他瑪]]，是大衛的女兒，被兄長[[暗嫩|暗嫩]]強姦。

== 故事 ==
[[File:Rembrandt's_school_Tamar.JPG|thumb]]
在[[创世纪38章|创世纪38章]]中，记述了他玛首先嫁给了[[猶大_(創世記)|猶大]]的长子[[珥_(圣经人物)|珥]]<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章6节|}}</ref>；但是由于珥的邪恶（但是缺少进一步的详情），而被[[耶和华|耶和华]]杀死<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章7节|}}</ref>，于是犹大要求次子[[俄南|俄南]]与他玛[[性交|同房]]，以便为长子珥留下后代。<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章8节|}}</ref>。接下来的叙述表明俄南并不反对[[性交|性交]]本身，但是采取了[[性交中断|性交中断]]的方法，避免生下不属于自己的后代。耶和华憎恨俄南的做法，也使他死亡<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章9-10节|}}</ref>。这时犹大担心如果按风俗继续让第三子示拉与他玛同房，他玛将会令自己仅剩的一个儿子步哥哥後塵，因此暂用托词，让她先回娘家守[[寡妇|寡]]，等待示拉长大，再与她同房<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章11节|}}</ref>；可是他玛发现示拉已经长大，还没有实践诺言，前来迎娶她<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章14节|}}</ref>.

根据[[创世纪38章|创世纪38章]]的记载，犹大在自己的妻子去世之后<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章12节|}}</ref>，和朋友前往亭拿剪羊毛。他玛听到消息，就坐在往亭拿路上的[[伊拿印|伊拿印]]城门口，戴上面纱，假扮[[妓女|妓女]]。犹大来此[[召妓|召妓]]，由于他玛戴着面纱，他没有辨认出是自己的儿媳<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章15-16节|}}</ref> 。作為召妓的費用，犹大提出付给她一只山羊羔作为代价，但是他玛要求先以犹大的印、带子和杖作为抵押品<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章17-18节|}}</ref>；他玛因为这次与公公犹大的同房而[[怀孕|怀孕]]<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章18节|}}</ref>，但是当犹大后来送去羊羔时，发现她已经带着印、带子和杖离开<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章20节|}}</ref>。三个月以后，当犹大听说他玛在娘家守寡期间充当妓女，并且怀孕的消息，便下令以[[通奸|通奸]]罪名将她烧死<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章24节|}}</ref>；但是当他玛向他出示犹大给她的印、带子和杖，公开宣布孩子的父亲就是印、带子和杖的主人，于是犹大承认了她和孩子，并且承认他玛比自己更为公义，因为自己没有让幼子示拉迎娶他玛。<ref>{{bibleverse||创世纪|38章25-26节|}}</ref> 同時他玛使長子名份得以延續。

== 意义 ==

[[圣经批判|圣经批判]]者认为，这是一个[[原因论|原型神话]]，反映了[[犹大支派|犹大支派]]中人口的波动；[[文本批判|文本批判]]学者将文本归于Yahwist，尽管圣经学者将其当作关于部落国家不早于此<ref>J. A. Emerton：《犹大和他玛》</ref><ref>{{1911}}</ref>。许多学者认为珥和俄南的死反映了两个部落的灭绝<ref>J. A. Emerton：《犹大和他玛》</ref><ref>Cheyne，Black：《圣经百科全书》</ref>；“俄南”可能说明 一支以东部落称为“俄南”<ref>Cheyne，Black：《圣经百科全书》</ref>，在创世纪36章曾提到以东后裔<ref>创世纪36章23节</ref>；而“珥”也出现在[[历代志|历代志]]的家谱中<ref>历代志上4章21节</ref>，曾经是一个部落，后来包括在“示拉”部落中<ref>J. A. Emerton：《犹大和他玛》</ref><ref>Cheyne ，Black：《圣经百科全书》</ref>.

另一些学者则关注[[夫兄弟婚|夫兄弟婚]]制度，或者将其作为这种制度起源的原型神话，因为它集锦了各种婚姻案例：为了享乐而不要孩子（俄南）、拒绝完婚（示拉）、与亡夫兄弟以外的人进行夫兄弟婚行为<ref>J. A. Emerton：《犹大和他玛》</ref>；<!-- ; Emerton regards the evidence for this as inconclusive, though [[classical_rabbinical_literature|classical rabbinical writers]] argued that this narrative concerns the origin of levirate marriage<ref>''[[Genesis_Rabbah|Genesis Rabbah]]'' 85:6</ref>.许多学者，particularly in recent decades (as of 1980), have proposed that the narrative reflects an [[anachronism|anachronistic]] interest in the biblical account of [[大卫|大卫]]王, with the character of [[rape_of_Tamar|Tamar]] being the same<ref>J. A. Instead of 与学术立场,新教神学注释 generally treat the story as a [[character_study|character study]]，concluding that the story depicts Judah's moral growth as he learns to take responsibility for his actions, and overcomes his past misdeeds。In such commentaries, Judah's avoidance of marrying Tamar to Shelah is taken to be due to an abdication of responsibility, rather than concern for his son，since Tamar would, in the view of these commentaries, need a husband to care for her, and children to look after her in her old age；Judah's initial condemnation of Tamar's prostitution is seen as being an attempt to rid himself of her burden, rather than an act of moral indignation。另一方面，他玛, is interpreted as being forgiving and discreet rather than wily，bending over backwards to prevent Judah from losing his reputation，by obtaining children in secret rather than in public, and using the seal and staff as subtle hints of Judah's part in her pregnancy, rather than publicly accusing him; the [[Talmud|Talmud]] similarly argues that Tamar's actions were for the purpose of avoiding Judah being humiliated<ref>Berakot 43a</ref><ref>Sotah 12b</ref>, although the [[Genesis_Rabbah|Genesis Rabbah]] portrays her as boastful and unashamed in regard to the pregnancy itself<ref>Genesis Rabbah 85:11</ref>. 

The Protestant commentaries argue that when confronted with the truth, Judah finally stopped running away from his duties, and slowly began repairing he relationships he had damaged；Tamar is hence seen by these commentaries as a device employed by God for this purpose。According to the [[Talmud|Talmud]], Judah's confession of guilt itself atoned for some of his prior faults, and resulted in him being divinely rewarded by [[Jewish_Eschatology|a share in the future world]]<ref>''[[Sotah|Sotah]]'' 7b</ref>. While the Protestant commentaries argue that Tamar was a way for Judah to have non-Israelite children, and that Judah did not have further sexual relations with her after he discovered he had fathered her children <Gen 38:26>，the Genesis Rabbah and Talmud state that Tamar was an Israelite<ref>Genesis Rabbah 85:9</ref><ref>Sotah 10a</ref>, and that Judah ended up marrying her and had further sexual liaisons with her as a result<ref>Sotah 10b</ref>

==年代问题==

Together with the brief preceding narrative of the birth of Er, Onan, and Shelah, and the subsequent narrative of the birth of Pharez's children, the passage is often regarded as presenting a significant chronological issue, since it is surrounded by a narrative concerning Joseph; before the passage occurs, Joseph is described as being 17 years old<ref>{{bibleverse||Genesis|37:2|}}</ref>, and after the passage, Joseph is described as meeting up with Judah some 9 years<ref>{{bibleverse||Genesis|41:53|}}</ref><ref>{{bibleverse||Genesis|45:6|}}</ref> after Joseph had reached 30 years in age<ref>{{bibleverse||Genesis|41:46|}}</ref>. The gap, a maximum of 22 years, is somewhat small to contain within it Judah's first marriage, the birth of Er and Onan, Er's marriage to Tamar, Tamar's subsequent pregnancy by Judah, and the birth of Tamar's grandchildren; the passage is also widely regarded as an abrupt change to the surrounding narrative Joseph story. According to textual scholars, the reason for these features is that the passage derives from the [[Jahwist|Jahwist]] source, while the immediately surrounding narrative is from the [[Elohist|Elohist]], the two being spliced together at a later date<ref>[[Thomas_Kelly_Cheyne|Cheyne]] and [[John_Sutherland_Black|Black]], ''Encyclopedia Biblica''</ref><ref>''Jewish Encyclopedia''</ref><ref>[[Richard_Elliott_Friedman|Richard Elliott Friedman]], ''Who wrote the Bible?''</ref>.-->
