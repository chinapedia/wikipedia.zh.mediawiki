'''淤泥'''（{{lang|en|Silt}}），又稱'''沉泥'''或'''粉土'''，是[[泥土|泥土]]的基本組成成份之一。地質學中，淤泥是介於[[沙土|沙土]]及[[黏土|黏土]]之間，長約2到62微米、直徑4到9微米的一種{{tsl|en|granular material|顆粒狀物料}}，主要由[[石英|石英]]及[[长石|长石]]這兩種[[礦物|礦物]]組成<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Assallay |first1=A.M. |last2=Rogers |first2=C.D.F. |last3=Smalley |first3=I.J. |last4=Jefferson |first4=I. |year=1998 |title=Silt: 2-62um,9-4phi. |journal=''[[Earth-Science_Reviews|Earth-Science Reviews]]'' |volume=45 |pages=61-88 }}</ref>。淤泥可能以[[土壤|土壤]]的成份或[[悬浊液|悬浊]][[水體|水體]]內的[[沉積物|沉積物]]兩種形式出現。當河道泛濫，又或被[[山崩|山崩]]時的[[泥石流|泥石流]]帶動，淤泥會在流動時隨同水體在沿途沉積成為土壤。由於淤泥的[[比表面积|比表面积]]屬中度，非黏粘，有好像塑膠那樣帶彈性。當完全乾燥時，淤泥會變回粉塵般的幼細，但潮濕後立即又變回黏滑。在手持的放大鏡下，淤泥清晰可見，往往帶有尖銳的外表。當以牙齒咬或以舌頭觸摸時，這種棱角亦能感覺到，而這亦是分辨水中的淤泥或黏土的一種方法。

==粒徑大小的條件==
按[[巫登–溫特瓦分級|巫登–溫特瓦分級]]（Udden–Wentworth scale），淤泥的[[粒徑|粒徑]]屬於[[粉砂|粉砂]]級的3.9至62.5 µm之間，比[[黏土|黏土]]大，但比[[沙|沙粒]]小。[[國際標準化組織|國際標準化組織]]的[[ISO_14688|ISO 14688]]將淤泥的粒徑定為2 µm-6 µm 到 20 µm-63 µm，然後再分為幼粒、中等和粗粒三等。實際上，淤泥在化學上的成份與黏土完全不同；此外淤泥的[[粒徑|粒徑]]在各個方向都大致相同，這一點亦是與黏土不同。再者，淤泥的尺寸往往重疊，即有多種不同粒徑的粉砂混合在一起。反而黏土由通過靜電力保持在一起的薄板狀顆粒形成，因此具有內聚力；相反淤泥並沒有這種內聚力。根據[[美国农业部|美国农业部]]（[[USDA|USDA]]）的[[土壤質地分類系統|土壤質地分類系統]]（{{lang|en|Soil Texture Classification system}}）沙土和淤泥以0.05 mm的顆粒為分野<ref>{{cite web |url=http://soils.usda.gov/technical/handbook/contents/part618.html#43 |title=Particle Size (618.43) |work=National Soil Survey Handbook Part 618 (42-55) Soil Properties and Qualities |publisher=United States Department of Agriculture - Natural Resource Conservation Service |accessdate=2006-05-31 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060527111746/http://soils.usda.gov/technical/handbook/contents/part618.html#43 |archivedate=2006-05-27 |df= }}</ref>。這套由美国农业部開發的系統後來亦為[[联合国粮食及农业组织|联合国粮食及农业组织]]（[[FAO|FAO]]）所採納。
In the {{tsl|en|Unified Soil Classification System|}} (USCS) and the {{tsl|en|AASHTO|}} Soil Classification system, the sand–silt distinction is made at the 0.075 mm particle size (i.e., material passing the #200 <!-- [[sieve|sieve]] -->[[筲箕|筲箕]]). Silts and clays are distinguished mechanically by their <!-- [[plasticity_(physics)|plasticity]] -->[[塑性變形|塑性變形]].

== Environmental impacts ==
[[File:Burgwall_Jatzke3.jpg|thumb]] -->[[艾肖尔斯特|艾肖尔斯特]], <!-- [[Germany|Germany]] -->[[德国|德国]]]]
Silt is easily transported in <!-- [[water|water]] -->[[水|水]] or other liquid and is fine enough to be carried long distances by air in the form of <!-- [[dust|dust]] -->[[灰塵|灰塵]]. Thick deposits of silty material resulting from deposition by <!-- [[aeolian_processes|aeolian processes]] -->[[風蝕|風蝕]] are often called <!-- [[loess|loess]] -->[[黃土|黃土]]. Silt and clay contribute to <!-- [[turbidity|turbidity]] -->[[濁度|濁度]] in water. Silt is transported by <!-- [[stream|stream]] -->[[溪|溪]]s or by water currents in the <!-- [[ocean|ocean]] -->[[洋|洋]]. When silt appears as a pollutant in water the phenomenon is known as {{tsl|en|siltation|}}.

Silt, deposited by annual floods along the <!-- [[Nile_River|Nile River]] -->[[尼罗河|尼罗河]], created the rich, fertile soil that sustained the <!-- [[Ancient_Egypt|Ancient Egypt]] -->[[古埃及|古埃及]]ian civilization. Silt deposited by the <!-- [[Mississippi_River|Mississippi River]] -->[[密西西比河|密西西比河]] throughout the 20th century has decreased due to a system of <!-- [[levees|levees]] -->[[堤|堤]], contributing to the disappearance of protective <!-- [[wetlands|wetlands]] -->[[湿地|湿地]] and {{tsl|en|barrier islands|}} in the <!-- [[Mississippi_River_Delta|delta]] -->[[密西西比河三角洲|密西西比河三角洲]] region surrounding <!-- [[New_Orleans|New Orleans]] -->[[新奥尔良|新奥尔良]].<ref>{{cite web

In southeast Bangladesh, in the <!-- [[Noakhali_district|Noakhali district]] -->[[諾阿卡利縣|諾阿卡利縣]], cross dams were built in the 1960s whereby silt gradually started forming new land called "chars". The district of Noakhali has gained more than {{convert|73|km2|sqmi|0}} of land in the past 50 years.

With Dutch funding, the Bangladeshi government began to help develop older chars in the late 1970s, and the effort has since become a multi-agency operation building roads, <!-- [[culvert|culvert]] -->[[涵洞|涵洞]]s, embankments, cyclone shelters, toilets and ponds, as well as distributing land to settlers. By fall 2010, the program will have allotted some {{convert|100|km2|acre|sigfig=1}} to 21,000 families.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pulitzercenter.org/openitem.cfm?id%3D1973 |title= Bangladesh fights for survival against climate change|accessdate=October 22, 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100212215115/http://www.pulitzercenter.org/openitem.cfm?id=1973 |archivedate=February 12, 2010 }}</ref>

A main source of silt in urban rivers is disturbance of soil by <!-- [[construction|construction]] -->[[建筑施工|建筑施工]] activity.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6OtWpor6LnsC&q=main+source+of+silt+in+urban+rivers+is+disturbance+of+soil+by+construction+activity&dq=main+source+of+silt+in+urban+rivers+is+disturbance+of+soil+by+construction+activity&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjAuKX726PYAhXLyFQKHcaSA_oQ6AEILzAB|title=Planning for Urban Fishing and Waterfront Recreation|last=Leedy|first=Daniel L.|last2=Franklin|first2=Thomas M.|last3=Maestro|first3=Robert M.|date=1981|publisher=U.S. Department of the Interior, Fish and Wildlife Service, Eastern Energy and Land Use Team|language=en|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171224224857/https://books.google.com/books?id=6OtWpor6LnsC&q=main+source+of+silt+in+urban+rivers+is+disturbance+of+soil+by+construction+activity&dq=main+source+of+silt+in+urban+rivers+is+disturbance+of+soil+by+construction+activity&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjAuKX726PYAhXLyFQKHcaSA_oQ6AEILzAB|archivedate=2017-12-24|df=}}</ref>  A main source in rural rivers is <!-- [[erosion|erosion]] -->[[侵蚀作用|侵蚀作用]] from plowing of farm fields, <!-- [[clearcutting|clearcutting]] -->[[皆伐|皆伐]] or <!-- [[slash_and_burn|slash and burn]] -->[[刀耕火耨|刀耕火耨]] treatment of <!-- [[forest|forest]] -->[[森林|森林]]s.{{Citation needed|date=September 2008}}
