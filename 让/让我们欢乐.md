《'''让我们欢乐'''》（{{lang-he|הבה נגילה}}，''Havah Nagilah''）是一首传统的[[希伯来语|希伯来语]][[犹太人|犹太]][[民俗音樂|民俗音樂]]，它是[[犹太婚礼|犹太婚礼]]和{{link-en|犹太成人礼|Bar and Bat Mitzvah}}中经常演奏的一首乐曲。

==起源==
该曲旋律源自[[布科維納|布科維納]]（[[霍拉舞|霍拉舞]]的一个变种）舞曲<ref>A. Idelsohn "Jewish Music: its historical development", p.12</ref>，采用{{link-en|弗里吉亚调式|Phrygian dominant scale}}，歌词可能是 Abraham Zevi Idelsohn 于1918年为庆祝[[第一次世界大战|第一次世界大战]]期间[[英国|英国]]在[[巴勒斯坦|巴勒斯坦]]取得胜利以及《[[贝尔福宣言|贝尔福宣言]]》的签订所作。<ref>{{cite web|last=Yudelson|first=Larry|title=Who wrote Havah Nagilah?|work=RadioHazak|publisher=Larry Yudelson|url=http://www.radiohazak.com/Havahist.html|accessdate=2007-11-08|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080729133626/http://www.radiohazak.com/Havahist.html|archivedate=2008-07-29|deadurl=yes}}</ref><ref>In an appearance on [http://www.thejc.com/home.aspx?ParentId=m14s49s53&SecId=53&AId=56480&ATypeId=1 BBC Radio 4 ''Desert Island Discs'' on 28 October 2007], [[Joel_Joffe|Joel Joffe]] referred to his grandfather [[Abraham_Zevi_Idelsohn|Abraham Zevi Idelsohn]] as the author of "Hava Nagila", but in the [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/factual/desertislanddiscs_20071028.shtml programme notes] it says "Composer: Bashir Am Israelim", meaning that either this is an alias for Abraham Zevi Idelsohn, to whom Joffe was clearly referring in the programme, or (more plausibly) the programme notes contain a mis-transcription of "Shir Am Yisraeli", meaning "Israeli folksong".</ref>《[[希伯來聖經|希伯來聖經]]》诗篇118（24段）是这一歌曲歌词的来源。
