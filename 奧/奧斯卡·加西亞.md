'''奥斯卡·加西亚·祖耶特'''（{{lang-es|'''Óscar García Junyent'''}}，{{bd|1973年|4月26日|catidx=García, Óscar}}）是一名前[[西班牙|西班牙]][[足球|足球]][[運動員|運動員]]，司職[[中场#進攻中場|進攻中場]]，退役後轉任[[教練|教練]]，曾執教[[特拉維夫馬卡比足球會|特拉維夫馬卡比]]和[[英冠|英冠]]球會[[布莱顿足球俱乐部|白禮頓]]和[[沃特福德足球俱乐部|屈福特]]。

作為球員時，加西亞是多才多藝的進攻選擇，可以擔任[[中场#進攻中場|進攻中場]]或[[前鋒_(足球)#二傳型前鋒|影子前锋]]。在他14年長的職業球員生涯中主要效力母會[[巴塞罗那足球俱乐部|巴塞隆拿]]，具有一定的影響，亦曾為另外四支國內球隊上陣。加西亞在[[西甲|西甲]]的12個球季合共上陣169場及射入31球，除巴塞隆拿外，亦曾效力[[皇家西班牙人體育俱樂部|愛斯賓奴]]4個球季和[[阿爾巴塞特足球會|阿爾巴塞特]]及[[巴伦西亚足球俱乐部|華倫西亞]]各一個球季。

==生平==
=== 球員 ===
Born in [[Sabadell|Sabadell]], [[Province_of_Barcelona|Barcelona]], [[Catalonia|Catalonia]], Óscar made his professional debut with [[FC_Barcelona|FC Barcelona]], his local team. Between 1992 and 1994 he played in five [[La_Liga|La Liga]] matches for Barca who were champions each year and, after a loan at fellow top division [[Albacete_Balompié|Albacete Balompié]], he returned and was often used (with good results) in a variety of offensive roles: during the [[1995–96_La_Liga|1995–96 season]] he scored 10 league goals, the most in the squad, even though he only started 11 of his 28 appearances;<ref>[http://elpais.com/diario/2009/04/06/deportes/1238968819_850215.html "Cruyff me decía: 'Sal y liquida'" ("Cruyff told me: 'Get out there and finish them off'")]; [[El_País|El País]], 6 April 2009 {{es icon}}</ref> Barcelona came out empty in silverware, however.

With his role gradually diminishing, Óscar joined [[Valencia_CF|Valencia CF]] for one season, finishing off with ''Barça'' neighbours [[RCD_Espanyol|RCD Espanyol]] (teaming up again with brother [[Roger_García_Junyent|Roger]], for three seasons) and [[UE_Lleida|UE Lleida]] and retiring in June 2005 at the age of 32. On 7 January 2001, whilst playing for the second club against [[CD_Numancia|CD Numancia]], he was taken to hospital after swallowing his [[tongue|tongue]];<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/europe/1106156.stm Roma continue record start]; [[BBC_Sport|BBC Sport]], 8 January 2001</ref> in the following summer, he nearly signed for [[West_Ham_United_F.C.|West Ham United]], but an eventual deal fell through after a one week's trial and he returned to Espanyol,<ref>[http://www.elmundo.es/elmundodeporte/2002/07/21/liga/1027285227.html Óscar García no ficha por el West Ham (Óscar García does not sign with West Ham)]; [[El_Mundo_(Spain)|El Mundo]], 21 July 2002 {{es icon}}</ref> with whom he never scored more than one goal per season, during four years.

For [[Spain_national_football_team|Spain]], Óscar appeared for [[Spain_national_under-23_football_team|the nation]] at the [[1996_Summer_Olympics|1996 Summer Olympics]] in [[Atlanta|Atlanta]], netting twice for [[Football_at_the_1996_Summer_Olympics|the eventual quarterfinalists]].
