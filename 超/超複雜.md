《'''超复杂'''》（{{lang-en|''Complicated''}}）是加拿大唱作歌手[[艾薇儿·拉维尼|艾薇儿·拉维尼]]首张专辑《[[展翅高飞_(艾薇儿·拉维尼专辑)|展翅高飞]]》的{{tsl|en|lead single|首支单曲|首支单曲}}，发行于2002年5月14日{{r|itunes}}，由艾薇儿和创作团队{{tsl|en|The Matrix (record production team)||The Matrix}}（斯科特·斯波克、劳伦·克里斯蒂和格雷厄姆·爱德华兹）共同创作，由The Matrix制作。这支单曲在[[澳大利亚唱片业协会榜|澳大利亚榜单]]上蝉联六周冠军、在新西兰和墨西哥的榜单上蝉联九周冠军；在[[公告牌百强单曲榜|《公告牌》百强单曲榜]]上最高名列第二，仅次于[[耐利|耐利]]的《{{tsl|en|Hot in Herre||Hot in Herre}}》；在英国的榜单上最高达到第三位。根据该单曲的榜单表现，这首歌是艾薇儿最成功的单曲之一（仅次于2007年的单曲《[[女朋友_(艾薇兒·拉維尼歌曲)|女朋友]]》），它在当年发行的单曲中以超过300万的全球销量成为了2002年最成功的单曲之一。艾薇儿还以这首歌蝉联[[成人四十强单曲榜|成人四十强单曲榜]]榜首16周，打破了[[娜塔莉·安博莉亚|娜塔莉·安博莉亚]]的《{{tsl|en|Torn (Natalie Imbruglia song)||Torn}}》的记录{{r|tv.com}}。《超复杂》获得过“{{tsl|en|Grammy Award for Song of the Year|格莱美奖年度歌曲|年度歌曲}}”及“{{tsl|en|Best Female Pop Vocal Performance|格莱美奖最佳流行女歌手|最佳流行女歌手}}”两项[[格莱美奖|格莱美奖]]的提名。

== 背景与发行 ==
{{TransH}}
After being signed to {{tsl|en|Arista Records||Arista Records}} in November 2000 upon the authorization of the label's CEO, {{tsl|en|L.A. Reid||Antonio "L.A." Reid}}, Lavigne moved to New York with the assistance of Reid. There, she began working on her debut album, ''Let Go'', collaborating with a host of prime songwriters and producers.<ref name="PakDeal">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/bands/l/lavigne_avril/news_feature_071202/index.jhtml |title=Avril Lavigne: The Real Deal |last=Pak |first=SuChin |author2=D'Angelo, Joe |publisher=MTV |accessdate=14 March 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090312053148/http://www.mtv.com/bands/l/lavigne_avril/news_feature_071202/index.jhtml |archivedate=12 March 2009 |df=dmy }}</ref> For six months, the label set up Lavigne with two co-writers, who worked with her upon Arista's instructions. Reid expected Lavigne to record [[民俗音樂|folk]] songs because she auditioned to them in a "[[谣曲|balladic]], 'new [[乡村音乐|country]]'" type. However, the collective failed to click "with a girl who'd just discovered guitar-based rock".<ref name="WillmanAnti">{{cite news|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,384096,00.html|title=Avril Lavigne The Anti-Britney|last=Willman|first=Chris|date=1 November 2002|work=Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=14 March 2009}}</ref> For a year, nothing was working for Lavigne and was on the verge of getting dropped off Arista.<ref name="thematrix">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1470503/20030312/lavigne_avril.jhtml |title=Britney Spears, Avril Lavigne Fall into The Matrix |last=Corey |first=Moss |date=13 May 2003 |publisher=MTV |accessdate=14 March 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080502045319/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1470503/20030312/lavigne_avril.jhtml |archivedate=2 May 2008 |df=dmy }}</ref> The management pitched her songs written by other songwriters, but she declined, insisting she wanted to write songs herself.<ref name="KaufmanComp">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1454441/20020521/lavigne_avril.jhtml|title=It's Not 'Complicated' — 17-Year-Old Avril Lavigne Was Born To Rock|last=Kaufman|first=Gil|date=21 May 2002|publisher=MTV|accessdate=14 March 2009}}</ref> Lavigne relocated to Los Angeles, where she collaborated with songwriter-producer {{tsl|en|Clif Magness||Clif Magness}}, who gave her ample creative control in the writing process. Lavigne and Magness wrote "[[失去控制|Losing Grip]]" and "Unwanted", songs that she deemed reflective of her vision for the entire album.<ref name="EliscuSmells">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/little-miss-cant-be-wrong-20030320|title=Little Miss Can't Be Wrong|last=Eliscu|first=Jenny|date=20 March 2003|work=Rolling Stone|accessdate=16 March 2009}}</ref> However, Arista was not thrilled with the heavy-guitar laden songs that Lavigne was writing, prompting the label to look for other producers to match their demands.<ref name="JacksonVocals">{{cite web|url=http://mixonline.com/recording/applications/audio_recording_vocals/ |title=Recording Vocals |last=Jackson |first=Blair |date=1 November 2002 |publisher=Mixonline.com |accessdate=16 March 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090312092730/http://mixonline.com/recording/applications/audio_recording_vocals/ |archivedate=12 March 2009 |df= }}</ref>

Two years since she signed the deal, Lavigne came to the attention of the three-piece production team {{tsl|en|The Matrix (record production team)||The Matrix}}. Arista could not find the right direction for Lavigne, so the team's manager, Sandy Roberton, suggested that they work together.<ref name="BuskinMatrix">{{cite web|url=http://www.soundonsound.com/sos/apr06/articles/matrix.htm|title=The Matrix: Writing & Producing in LA|last=Buskin|first=Richard|date=April 2006|publisher=SOS|accessdate=18 March 2009}}</ref> According to member [[劳伦·克里斯蒂|Lauren Christy]], they had been listening to Lavigne's early songs and felt they contained "a [[菲丝·希尔|Faith Hill]] kind of vibe". As soon as they saw Lavigne coming into their studio, The Matrix felt that her musical direction was incongruous to her image and attitude.{{r|BuskinMatrix}} After talking to Lavigne for an hour, the singer said she wanted songs with [[朋克搖滾|punk rock]] inclinations.{{r|WillmanAnti}} They told her to come back the following day, and in the afternoon during that day, they wrote a song that evolved into "Complicated" and another song called "Falling Down". They played it to Lavigne when she came back the following day, inspiring her what path she should take.{{r|BuskinMatrix}}

When Josh Sarubin, the A&R executive who signed Lavigne to the imprint, heard the song, he knew it was right for her. Lavigne presented the song to Reid, who agreed the musical direction Lavigne and The Matrix were taking, and set "Complicated" as the album's {{tsl|en|lead single||lead single}}.{{r|BuskinMatrix}}

== 编曲与歌词 ==
{{TransH}}
Composed in the key of F major, "Complicated" is a song about how people can feign or pretend in front of others. Lavigne said about the song: "People sometimes bother me how they're not real and how they're just, like, putting on a face and being two-faced". Lavigne stated that she experienced this with both boyfriends and female friends.{{r|ytAvrilTalks}}

The video, directed by {{tsl|en|The Malloys||The Malloys}}, starts with Lavigne asking her bandmates if they want to "crash" the mall. They respond with enthusiasm, and skateboard there. The video features Lavigne and the band harassing shoppers and employees, generally causing havoc around the mall; for example, Lavigne watches her bandmates try on humorous clothing which is fittingly shown as Lavigne sings the line "You come over unannounced, dressed up like you're something else".

This is intercut with footage of Lavigne performing the song at a skatepark while playing the guitar, with her band performing with her. People can be seen skateboarding around Lavigne as she and her band perform the song. As the line "You fall and you crawl..." is sung, a skateboarder can be seen falling over.

The video was shot at Eagle Rock Plaza, Los Angeles, in 2 days. During the shooting, the mall remained open.{{r|paradise}}

In January 2018, the video reached over 200,000,000 views on YouTube.

== 专业评价 ==
{{TransH}}
===乐评===
The song received generally positive reviews from critics.  In 2009, ''[[滾石_(雜誌)|Rolling Stone]]'' readers voted "Complicated" as the eighth Top single of the decade.{{r|rStone}} "Complicated" also ranked at #197 in ''{{tsl|en|Blender (magazine)||Blender}}'' magazine's "The 500 Greatest Songs Since You Were Born".{{r|blender}} ''[[告示牌_(雜誌)|Billboard]]'' ranked it at No. 83 of the 'Top 100 Singles of the Decade'.{{r|Billboard}} In an {{tsl|en|AOL Radio||AOL Radio}} listener's poll, "Complicated" was voted Lavigne's sixth best song.{{r|radioblog}}

David Browne of ''[[娱乐周刊|Entertainment Weekly]]'' gave the song a B- and said "Avril Lavigne's not kidding about that title, she's the epitome of the blossoming anti-Britney movement".{{r|EWreview}} Christina Saraceno of [[Allmusic|Allmusic]] described 'Complicated' as "a gem of a pop/rock tune with a killer chorus" and noted similarities between it and the [[粉红佳人|Pink]] song "{{tsl|en|Don't Let Me Get Me||Don't Let Me Get Me}}".{{r|allmusicreview}} Saraceno highlighted the song as a "track pick" in a review of the album, ''Let Go''.{{r|allmusicreview}}

On a more negative note, Sal Cinquemani of ''[[偏锋杂志|Slant]]'' magazine described 'Complicated' as "infectious" and "more poser than punk".{{r|slantreview}} In a review of Lavigne's second album, ''[[酷到骨子裡|Under My Skin]]'', David Browne of ''Entertainment Weekly'' noted that "''Complicated'' felt like strung-together bits of [[艾拉妮絲·莫莉塞特|Morissette]] songs".{{r|entertainmentweekly}}

===商业表现===
As of September 2015 "Complicated" has sold 1.1 million digital copies in the US and over 3 million copies worldwide.<ref name="US Sales">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6685941/ask-billboard-avril-lavignes-best-selling-songs-albums |last=Trust |first=Gary |title=Ask Billboard: Avril Lavigne's Best-Selling Songs & Albums |work=[[告示牌_(雜誌)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[普羅米修斯環球媒體|Prometheus Global Media]] |date=6 September 2015 |accessdate=6 September 2015}}</ref>

The Canadian single of "Complicated" has shipped over 100,000 copies in Canada, where the song has been awarded the gold and platinum single certifications by the [[加拿大音乐协会|CRIA]] for, respectively, shipments of at least 50,000 and 100,000 singles shipped.The  single was certified 2× Platinum in [[澳大利亚唱片业协会|Australia]].{{r|ARIA}}

==奖项与提名==
{{TransH}}
Lavigne won {{tsl|en|MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist||Best New Artist in a Video}} at the 2002 [[MTV音樂錄影帶大獎|MTV Video Music Award]]s for "Complicated".{{r|mtv.com}} "Complicated" won in the {{tsl|en|Juno Award for Single of the Year||Single of the Year}} category at the 2003 [[朱諾獎|Juno Award]]s. In the United States, the song was nominated at the 2003 [[葛萊美獎|Grammy Award]]s for {{tsl|en|Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance||Best Female Pop Vocal Performance}} and {{tsl|en|Grammy Award for Song of the Year||Song of the Year}};{{r|delrock}} it lost both awards to [[诺拉·琼斯|Norah Jones]]'s "{{tsl|en|Don't Know Why||Don't Know Why}}". In Brazil it was nominated in the category of best international video at the 2003 MTV Video Music Brazil.{{r|areavip}}
