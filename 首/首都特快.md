'''首都特快'''（英文：'''Capitol Limited'''）是美鐵公司運行於華盛頓與芝加哥之間的兩列客車之一，運行距離為{{convert|764|mi|km}}，途經匹茲堡與克里夫蘭。另一列車則為行經辛辛那提與印第安納波利斯的[[紅雀號|紅雀號]]（Cardinal）。本列車由1981年開始營運，其名稱乃來自[[巴爾的摩與俄亥俄鐵路|巴爾的摩與俄亥俄鐵路]]公司的首都特快－－其於1971年美鐵公司成立時停駛。本列車於美鐵公司的車次編號為29次與30次車，其編號乃承繼已經停駛的國家特快（National Limited）。
於2011年度首都特快載客量超過225,000人，較前一年增加了3.5%，而總收入則為20,312,544美元，較2010年度增加了 9.3%。<ref name="FY2011"/>

== History ==
On October 1, 1981, Amtrak inaugurated its ''Capitol Limited''. It ran over the same route as the B&O's train east of [[Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania|Pittsburgh]], but west of Pittsburgh it ran combined with the Chicago-New York ''[[Broadway_Limited|Broadway Limited]]'' over the former [[Pennsylvania_Railroad|Pennsylvania Railroad]]'s [[Pittsburgh,_Fort_Wayne_and_Chicago_Railway|Pittsburgh, Fort Wayne and Chicago Railway]]. Its numbers, 440 (eastbound) and 441 (westbound), were derived from the ''Broadway Limited's'' 40 and 41 and the new train also used [[Heritage_Fleet|Heritage Fleet]] equipment.<ref>{{cite web | title=National Train Timetables | date=October 25, 1981 | accessdate=2010-05-22 | url=http://www.timetables.org/full.php?group=19811025&item=0033 | author=[[Amtrak|Amtrak]]}}</ref> The new train replaced the ''Broadway Limited'''s former Washington section which had diverged at [[Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania|Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]].<ref name="Sanders109">{{cite book | first=Craig | last=Sanders | year=2009 | publisher=Arcadia | title=Canton Area Railroads | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=IeEiaVoFAIwC&dq}}; 109.</ref>

In late 1984, the ''Capitol Limited'' re-equipped with new [[Amfleet|Amfleet]] II coaches but also lost its full diner east of Pittsburgh. Beginning with the October 26, 1986, timetable, Amtrak split up the ''Capitol Limited'' and ''Broadway Limited''. The former received new numbers, 29 and 30 (which formerly belonged to the defunct ''[[National_Limited|National Limited]]''), and a later schedule. The ''Capitol Limited'' continued to operate over the same route. With the change the ''Capitol Limited'' regained a full diner east of Pittsburgh, which it had lost in 1984.<ref name="welsh" />{{rp|146}}

On November 12, 1990, trains were rerouted west of [[Alliance,_Ohio|Alliance, Ohio]], due to [[Conrail|Conrail]]'s desire to abandon part of the former PFW&C in northwestern [[Indiana|Indiana]]; the ''Capitol Limited'' now uses the former Pennsylvania Railroad Cleveland and Pittsburgh (C&P) line north from Alliance through [[Hudson,_Ohio|Hudson, Ohio]], to Cleveland route. The ''Broadway Limited'' and its successor, the ''[[Three_Rivers_(Amtrak)|Three Rivers]]'', were re-routed over the B&O's Chicago-Pittsburgh route.<ref name="welsh" />{{rp|146–147}} In October 1994, Amtrak, with great fanfare, relaunched the ''Capitol Limited'' with a new bilevel [[Superliner_(railcar)|Superliner II]] consist.<ref>{{cite web | title=National Timetable | date=October 30, 1994 | accessdate=2010-05-23 | url=http://www.timetables.org/browse/?group=19941030n&item=0019 | author=[[Amtrak|Amtrak]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | title=Superliner introduces travel to Chicago-Toledo-Washington | first=David | last=Patch | publisher=''[[Toledo_Blade|Toledo Blade]]'' | date=October 26, 1994 | accessdate=2010-05-23 | url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=qkYxAAAAIBAJ&sjid=fQMEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5537,8029169&dq=amtrak+capitol+limited&hl=en}}</ref>

On February 16, 1996, an eastbound rush-hour [[MARC_Train|MARC]] commuter train headed to [[Washington_Union_Station|Washington Union Station]] [[1996_Silver_Spring,_Maryland_train_collision|collided]] with the westbound ''Capitol Limited'' near Georgetown Junction on a snow-covered stretch of track just west of [[Silver_Spring,_Maryland|Silver Spring, Maryland]]. Eleven people died aboard the MARC train in the accident. Three died of injuries suffered in the impact; the rest were killed by smoke and flames. The MARC engineer and two conductors were among the dead.<ref name="The Baltimore Sun">{{cite web | work=The Baltimore Sun | url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/1996-02-20/news/1996051133_1_passengers-signal-train-accident | title=Collision in Silver Spring MARC-Amtrak tragedy | date=February 20, 1996 | accessdate=April 24, 2012}}</ref>

=== Through cars ===
Between 1984 and 1986 and again from 1991 to 1993, the ''Capitol Limited'' exchanged a [[Chicago|Chicago]]-[[Miami,_Florida|Miami]] coach with the New York-[[Miami|Miami]] ''[[Silver_Star_(Amtrak)|Silver Star]]'' at Washington, D.C.

During 1997 and part of 1998, Amtrak operated the ''Capitol Limited'' in conjunction with the ''[[Southwest_Chief|Southwest Chief]]'', a daily Los Angeles–Chicago service. The two trains used the same [[Superliner_(railcar)|Superliner]] equipment sets, and passengers traveling on both trains could remain aboard during the layover in Chicago. Originally announced in 1996, Amtrak planned to call this through service the "''National Chief''" with its own numbers (15/16), although the name and numbers were never used. Amtrak dropped the practice with the May 1998 timetable.<ref>{{cite web | title=Amtrak National Timetable | date=November 10, 1996 | accessdate=2010-03-14 | url=http://www.timetables.org/full.php?group=19961110n&item=0040}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=Amtrak National Timetable | date=May 11, 1997 | accessdate=2010-03-14 | url=http://www.timetables.org/full.php?group=19970511n&item=0024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=Amtrak National Timetable | date=May 17, 1998 | accessdate=2010-03-14 | url=http://www.timetables.org/full.php?group=19980517n&item=0046}}</ref>

This route was mentioned amongst five others in the July 2010 issue of ''Trains'' magazine as slated for performance improvement,<ref name="Trains 2010">"Amtrak Trains Under the Microscope in 2010", ''Trains'', July 2010, 20.</ref> and as part of its federally mandated analysis of the worst-performing long-distance routes, Amtrak determined that reinstating a through-car connection with the [[Pennsylvanian_(Amtrak)|''Pennsylvanian'']] would result in the highest gain in monetary and customer service measurements of possible options.<ref name="trains web"/> To implement this, Amtrak plans to operate a [[Viewliner|Viewliner]] sleeper car, an [[Amfleet|Amfleet]] cafe car and two Amfleet coaches between Chicago and New York via the ''Capitol Limited'' and ''Pennsylvanian'' beginning sometime in 2011.<ref name="trains web">{{cite web|url=http://trn.trains.com/~/media/Files/PDF/CapLtdPIPsec210PRIIA9-30-10.ashx|title=PRIIA Section 210 FY10 Performance Improvement Plan Capitol Limited|publisher=Amtrak|work=Trains Magazine|accessdate=13 November 2010}}</ref> ''Trains'' magazine picked up on this in their January 2011 issue, citing that a switch would be re-installed to give the thru-cars access to parallel track. Also cited was the consist switch that would take place (shuffling sleeper and coach positions so that the transition sleeper was in the rear), an eight-hour layover on Sundays due to the ''Pennsylvanian'''s 1:20p departure (since eliminated), and the lack of Viewliner sleepers to accommodate the service (also cited are that the new ones would not roll out until 2012 at the very earliest).<ref>"Amtrak's Improvement Wish List", ''Trains'', January 2011, 20-21.</ref>

*美鐵，華盛頓端點區段，以及東北走廊的一部份。 
*華盛頓至匹茲堡：CSX 的都會區段（Metropolitan Subdivision）、坎伯蘭區段（Cumberland Subdivision）、坎伯蘭端點區段（Cumberland Terminal Subdivision）、拱心石區段（Keystone Subdivision）、匹茲堡區段（Pittsburgh Subdivision）、 匹茲堡與西方區段（P&W Subdivision)。
*匹茲堡至[[阿莱恩斯|阿莱恩斯]]：諾福克南方鐵路匹茲堡線（Pittsburgh Line）、韋恩堡線（Fort Wayne Line）

*克里夫蘭至芝加哥：諾福克南方鐵路的芝加哥線（Chicago Line）
*美鐵，芝加哥端點區段
