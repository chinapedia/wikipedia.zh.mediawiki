'''华沙新城区'''是[[波兰|波兰]]首都[[华沙|华沙]]的一个街区，历史可以追溯到15世纪，位于[[华沙老城|华沙老城]]以北，通过弗莱塔街相连（''ulica Freta'' ({{lang-en|Freta Street}})）， and is connected to it by ''ulica Freta'' ({{lang-en|Freta Street}}), <!-- which begins at the [[Warsaw_Barbican|Barbican]].  -->与华沙老城一样，新城区毁于[[第二次世界大战|第二次世界大战]]中[[纳粹|纳粹]]战火，在战后重建。

==History==
[[Image:Sigismundus_Augustus_Bridge_in_Warsaw.jpg|upright]]

The [[historic_district|historic district]] of the New Town was formed at the turn of the 14th century as an independent city.<ref name="perelki">{{en icon}} {{cite web |author=|url=http://www.um.warszawa.pl/v_syrenka/perelki/index_en.php?mi_id=64&dz_id=3|title=The New Town|work=www.um.warszawa.pl|publisher= |pages= |page= |date= |accessdate=2010-01-26}}</ref> The official recognition of the New Town occurred in 1408, when it was separated from the Old Town by an act issued by [[Janusz_I_of_Warsaw|Janusz I the Old]], [[Duke_of_Masovia|Duke of Masovia]].<ref name="wilson">{{en icon}} {{cite book |author=Neil Wilson, Tom Parkinson, Richard Watkins|coauthors=|title=Poland|year=2005 |editor=|page=67|pages= |chapter=|chapterurl= |publisher=Lonely Planet|location= |isbn=1-74059-522-X |url=|format= |accessdate=}}</ref> At that time the new city encompassed the territory of the New Town Market Square and streets - ''Freta, Kościelna, Koźla, Przyrynek, Stara'' and ''Zakroczymska''. It was independent from the Old Town authorities and had its own [[vogt|vogt]], council and a [[City_and_town_halls|town hall]]. In 1411 the [[St._Mary's_Church,_Warsaw|Parish Church of St. Mary]] was erected, and according to the 1546 mensuration there were 204 estates in the Warsaw New Town. The largest investment of that time was the [[Sigismund_II_Augustus|Sigismund Augustus]] bridge built between 1568-1573 by Erazm Cziotko (c. {{convert|500|m|ft|abbr=on}} long).<ref>{{pl icon}} {{cite web  |author = Jerzy S. Majewski |url = http://miasta.gazeta.pl/warszawa/1,97596,2049149.html |title = Most Zygmunta Augusta |work = miasta.gazeta.pl |publisher = |pages =  |page =  |date =2004-04-29 |accessdate = 2009-10-25}}</ref> Unfortunately it was destroyed in 1603 by the drifting ice floes. 

[[File:Marie_Curie_birthplace.jpg|upright]]'s Museum on ''Freta Street''.]]

The disastrous time of the Swedish-[[Brandenburg|Brandenburg]]ian invasion (1655–1660), left the predominantly timber buildings of the New Town burned, but because of those events many beautiful and more permanent buildings were erected (the Town Hall, built in 1680; [[St._Kazimierz_Church|St. Kazimierz Church]], 1688–1692; the [[Kotowski_Palace|Kotowski Palace]], 1682–1684; the Holy Spirit Church, 1707–1717; and the ornate chapel of the Kotowski family, constructed between 1691-1694) by the most prominent Warsaw architects (especially [[Tylman_van_Gameren|Tylman Gamerski]]).<ref name="wilson" /> After 1791, due to the tenets of the [[Constitution_of_May_3,_1791|Constitution of May 3, 1791]], the New Town was incorporated into the city of Warsaw. That is why the Town Hall was pulled down in 1818. 

During the [[Warsaw_Uprising|Warsaw Uprising]] (1 August – 2 October 1944) the New Town was completely destroyed due to the extensive [[bombardment|bombardment]] of the insurgent positions by the Germans.<ref name="wilson" /><ref name="hanson">{{en icon}} {{cite book |author=Joanna K. M. Hanson|coauthors=|title=The Civilian Population and the Warsaw Uprising of 1944|year=2004 |editor=|page=99|pages= |chapter=|chapterurl= |publisher=Cambridge University Press|location= |isbn=0-521-53119-5 |url=|format= |accessdate=}}</ref> Many historic edifices, that served as [[hospital|hospital]]s and [[wikt:shelter|shelter]]s for the inhabitants were razed to the ground.<ref name="wilson" /><ref name="hanson" /> The reconstruction of the New Town started in 1954, but some of the significant buildings were not restored by the [[People's_Republic_of_Poland|Communist authorities]] (e.g. Kotowski Palace).<ref>{{pl icon}} {{cite web|author = |url = http://www.varsovia.pl/varsovia/index.php-frame=main&mapa=0&item=3507&top=23.htm|title = Zespół kościelno-klasztorny Sakramentek na Rynku Nowego Miasta|work = www.varsovia.pl|publisher = |pages = |page = |date = |accessdate = 2008-07-09|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080613214004/http://www.varsovia.pl/varsovia/index.php-frame%3Dmain%26mapa%3D0%26item%3D3507%26top%3D23.htm|archive-date = 2008-06-13|dead-url = yes}}</ref>
