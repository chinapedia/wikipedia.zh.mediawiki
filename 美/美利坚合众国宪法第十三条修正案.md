'''美利坚合众国宪法第十三条修正案'''（{{lang-en|''Thirteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution''}}）旨在废除[[美國奴隸制度|奴隶制]]和{{link-en|强制劳役|involuntary servitude}}，除非是“依法判罪的人的犯罪的惩罚”。该修正案于1864年4月8日在[[联邦参议院|联邦参议院]]以三分之二多数通过，再于1865年1月31日在[[联邦众议院|联邦众议院]]通过，1865年12月6日获得[[美利坚合众国宪法第五条|宪法第五条]]所规定的四分之三多数州批准生效。1865年12月18日，[[美国国务卿|国务卿]][[威廉·H·苏厄德|威廉·H·苏厄德]]正式宣布修正案通过，成为[[南北战争|南北战争]]结束后通过的三条[[重建修正案|重建修正案]]的第一条。

最初制订的[[美国宪法|美国宪法]]中对奴隶制采取了默许的保护态度，如[[五分之三妥协|五分之三妥协]]中规定各州奴隶人口将按五分之三的比例折算成自由人口后用于确认该州在联邦众议院的代表席位数。在第十三条修正案以前[[美利坚合众国宪法第十二条修正案|最后一次有修正案通过]]已经过去了超过60年时间。虽然许多奴隶已由林肯在1863年的[[解放奴隸宣言|解放奴隸宣言]]中宣布恢复自由身，但他们战后的地位仍然不明。1864年4月8日，参议院通过了废除奴隶制的修正案。众议院一开始的投票没有通过，但之后还是在林肯政府的竭力推动下于1865年1月31日投票通过。几乎所有的[[北军|北方联邦州]]都迅速批准了修正案，多个南北边界以及南方部分“已经重建”的州也予以批准，让修正案成功地在这年结束前通过。

虽然修正案正式废除了全美各地的奴隶制，但诸如[[黑人法令|黑人法令]]、白人至上主义者的暴力行为和选择性执法导致非裔美国人继续受到强制劳役，这类情况又尤以南方州为甚。与另外两条重建修正案不同的是，第十三条修正案很少在判例法中引用，但也还是用于废除了劳役偿债制和一些基于种族的歧视行为。第十三条修正案与[[美利坚合众国宪法第十四条修正案|第十四条]]和[[美利坚合众国宪法第十五条修正案|第十五条修正案]]的另一个不同之处在于，这条修正案对个人行为仍然适用，并不局限于[[国家行为|国家行为]]。修正案还允许国会立法打击[[人口贩卖|人口贩卖]]等现代形式的奴隶制度。

== 美国奴隶制度 ==
{{Main|美国奴隶制度}}
[[Image:Cicatrices_de_flagellation_sur_un_esclave.jpg|thumb]]

美国建国前的13个北美[[殖民地|殖民地]]都存在奴隶制<ref>{{cite book |title=Law in America: A Short History |last=Friedman |first=Lawrence Meir |year=2004 |publisher=Random House |isbn=9780812972856 |page=69 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=vEyy573xiDIC&pg=PA69&dq=slavery+%22every+colony%22+United+states&hl=en&sa=X&ei=JJTBUeorj9j0BOKggZgO&ved=0CCwQ6AEwADgK#v=onepage&q=slavery%20%22every%20colony%22%20United%20states&f=false |accessdate=2013-09-25}}</ref>。1787年制订的美国宪法中虽未用到“奴隶制”一词，但包括了多处与其有关的条文。如[[美利坚合众国宪法第一条|第一条]]第二款第3节的五分之三妥协规定众议员名额的分配根据各州“自由人总数加上所有其他人口的五分之三予以确定”，这个“其他人口”就是指奴隶<ref name="USC25-8">{{cite book |title=美国宪政历程：影响美国的25个司法大案 |author=陈伟 |publisher=中国法制出版社 |isbn=7-80182-138-6 |chapter=第八章　引发美国内战的司法判决——斯科特诉桑弗特案(1857) |pages=一、美国宪法暗藏杀机|date=2004年1月}}</ref><ref name="USC25" /><ref name="Li1999" /><ref>{{cite book |author=Jean Allain |title=The Legal Understanding of Slavery: From the Historical to the Contemporary |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=n_KAvAjkEbsC&pg=PA117 |year=2012 |publisher=Oxford University Press |page=117}}</ref>。又如[[美利坚合众国宪法第四条|第四条]]第二款第3节中的{{link-en|劳役逃犯条款|Fugitive Slave Clause}}规定：“根据一州法律须在该州服劳役或劳动的人，如逃往他州，不得因他州的法律或规章而免除此种劳役或劳动”<ref name="USC25" /><ref name="Li1999" />。第一条第九款还规定国会只有在1808年后才能通过立法禁止“任何一州认为得准予入境之人的迁移或入境”<ref name="Foner">{{cite book |last=Foner |first=Eric |title=The Fiery Trial: Abraham Lincoln and American Slavery |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=earytjxi6pEC |accessdate=2013-06-04 |year=2010 |publisher=W. W. Norton |isbn=978-0-393-06618-0 |ref=harv}}</ref>{{rp|16}}。不过在[[美國憲法第五修正案|第五条修正案]]中有这样的规定：“无论何人……不经正当法律程序，不得被剥夺生命、自由或财产。”<ref name="USC25" /><ref name="Li1999" /><ref>{{cite book|author=Jean Allain|title=The Legal Understanding of Slavery: From the Historical to the Contemporary|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=n_KAvAjkEbsC&pg=PA119|year=2012|publisher=Oxford University Press|pages=119–120}}</ref>废奴主义者用这条修正案来攻击奴隶制，这也是1857年[[斯科特诉桑福德案|斯科特诉桑福德案]]中将奴隶视为财产的法律基础<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |page=14 |quote=Nineteenth century apologists for the expansion of slavery developed a political philosophy that placed property at the pinnacle of personal interests and regarded its protection to be the government's chief purpose. The Fifth Amendment's Just Compensation clause provided the proslavery camp with a bastion for fortifying the peculiar institution against congressional restrictions to its spread westward. Based on this property-rights centered argument, Chief Justice Roger B. Taney, in ''Dred Scott v. Sanford'' (1857), found the Missouri Compromise unconstitutionally violated due process.}}</ref>。[[白人優越主義|白人優越主義]]在当时美国文化上的普遍性也导致无论法律还是实践中奴隶制都获得了支持<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |pages=18-23 |quote=Constitutional protections of slavery coexisted with an entire culture of oppression. The peculiar institution reached many private aspects of human life, for both whites and blacks. [...] Even free Southern blacks lived in a world so legally constricted by racial domination that it offered only a deceptive shadow of freedom.}}</ref>。

从1777到1804年，所有的北方州都直接或逐步废除了奴隶制，但没有任何一个南方州跟风，并且其奴隶人口数量还在不断增长，并于1861年达到约400万人的峰值<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|14-16}}。由{{link-en|威廉·劳埃德·加里森|William Lloyd Garrison}}等人领导的[[廢奴主義|废奴主义运动]]在北方发展壮大，要求在全国范围内中止奴隶制度，导致南方和北方的关系变得日趋紧张。1836年，联邦众议院制定了反对废奴主义请愿和演讲的{{link-en|堵嘴规则|gag rule}}，试图扼杀[[约翰·昆西·亚当斯|约翰·昆西·亚当斯]]和其他废奴主义众议员的主张。认为不同种族应保持隔离的废奴主义者和担心获得自由的前景会助长奴隶叛乱的奴隶主组成了一个名为[[美国殖民协会|美国殖民协会]]的同盟，他们要求将自由的黑人和奴隶通过移民或殖民的方式送回[[非洲|非洲]]。这一观点得到了包括[[亨利·克莱|亨利·克莱]]在内的多位[[政治家|政治家]]支持，他们担心废奴主义运动会挑起一场[[内战|内战]]<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|20-22}}。1818年和1839年，联邦众议员{{link-en|阿瑟·利弗莫尔|Arthur Livermore}}和约翰·昆西·亚当斯分别提出了淘汰奴隶制的宪法修正案，但都没能赢得足够的支持<ref name="Vile">{{cite encyclopedia |year=2003|title=Thirteenth Amendment |encyclopedia=Encyclopedia of Constitutional Amendments, Proposed Amendments, and Amending Issues: 1789 - 2002 |publisher=ABC-CLIO |editor-first=John R. |editor-last=Vile |pages=449-452}}</ref>。

随着美国的继续扩张，新领土上的奴隶制问题成了国家面临的主要问题。南方州认为奴隶属于财产，因此可以像其它任何一种财产那样从一州移至他州<ref name="Goodwin">{{cite book |last=Goodwin |first=Doris Kearns |title=Team of Rivals: The Political Genius of Abraham Lincoln |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=ONhhui9SRsMC |accessdate=2013-09-25 |year=2005|publisher=Simon & Schuster|isbn=978-0-7432-7075-5}}</ref>{{rp|123}}。1820年的[[密苏里妥协|密苏里妥协]]将[[密苏里州|密苏里州]]接纳为一个蓄奴州，[[缅因州|缅因州]]为一个自由州，由此保持联邦参议院的地区平衡。1846年的一项拨款法案中附加了一个{{link-en|威尔莫特但书|Wilmot Proviso}}，其中规定在所有[[美墨戰爭|美墨戰爭]]中获得的土地上禁止施行奴隶制，这一规定多次经众议院投票通过，但都没能经过参议院这关<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|123}}，之后的[[1850年妥协案|1850年妥协案]]通过接纳作为自由州的[[加利福尼亚州|加利福尼亚州]]暂时化解了这个问题，并设立了一个更强有力的{{link-en|1850年逃奴追缉法|Fugitive Slave Act of 1850|逃奴追缉法}}，禁止在[[哥伦比亚特区|哥伦比亚特区]]进行奴隶贸易，并允许[[新墨西哥州|新墨西哥州]]和[[犹他州|犹他州]]在奴隶制问题上自决<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|59}}。

虽然有上述的折衷方案，但南方和北方的紧张局势在随后的十年中仍继续激化，这其中还受到了其它多方面因素的影响，包括1852年出版的反奴隶制[[小说|小说]]《[[汤姆叔叔的小屋|汤姆叔叔的小屋]]》<ref name="McPherson">{{cite book |last=McPherson |first=James M. |title=Battle Cry of Freedom: The Civil War Era |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=a3nX48n4oeIC |accessdate=2013-09-25 |year=1988 |publisher=Oxford University Press |isbn=978-0-19-503863-7}}</ref>{{rp|88-91}}，1854年[[堪萨斯州|堪萨斯州]][[堪薩斯內戰|废奴主义势力和支持奴隶制势力的对抗]]<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|146-150}}、1857年[[联邦最高法院|联邦最高法院]]在[[斯科特诉桑弗特案|斯科特诉桑弗特案]]中判定1850年妥协案违宪<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|170-177}}，废奴主义者[[约翰·布朗_(废奴主义者)|约翰·布朗]]于1859年试图在[[哈珀斯费里_(西弗吉尼亚州)|哈珀斯费里]]发起奴隶起义<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|201-206}}以及1860年[[美国总统选举|美国总统选举]]的结果：反对奴隶制的[[共和党_(美国)|共和党人]][[亚伯拉罕·林肯|亚伯拉罕·林肯]]当选成为新的总统。南方各州在林肯当选几个月后宣布脱离联邦，组建[[美利堅聯盟國|美利堅聯盟國]]，并随即挑起了南北战争<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|234-235}}。

== 通过 ==
[[File:James_Mitchell_Ashley_-_Brady-Handy.jpg|thumb]]

1863年1月1日，林肯利用总统战时所拥有的权力颁布了解放奴隸宣言，宣布南方所有叛乱州的奴隶立即恢复自由，但这并没有对那些选择继续效忠联邦的边界州中的奴隶现状产生影响<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|558}}。林肯接着又在1863年12月宣布大赦和重建，承诺只要南方州废除奴隶制，并搜集占其投票人口数10%的效忠誓辞，就给予一个重返联邦的机会<ref name="Vorenberg">{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002}}</ref>{{rp|47}}。南方州没有轻易接受这个机会，其奴隶制的地位仍不明朗。

在内战的最后几年里，北方联邦的国会议员就多种不同的重建提案进行了辩论<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|48-51}}，其中一些人提出以宪法修正案来在全国范围内永久性地废除奴隶制。1863年12月14日，来自[[俄亥俄州|俄亥俄州]]的联邦众议员[[詹姆斯·米歇尔·阿什利|詹姆斯·米歇尔·阿什利]]提出了一条这样的修正案<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ohiohistorycentral.org/w/Ashley,_James?rec=129 |title=James Ashley |work=Ohio History Central |publisher=Ohio Historical Society |accessdate=2013-09-25}}</ref>。[[艾奥瓦州|艾奥瓦州]]众议员{{link-en|詹姆斯·F·威尔森|James F. Wilson}}也很快提出了类似的提案。1864年1月11日，密苏里州联邦参议员{{link-en|约翰·B·亨德森|John B. Henderson}}提交了一份废除奴隶制宪法修正案的[[联合决议案|联合决议案]]。由[[伊利诺伊州|伊利诺伊州]]参议员{{link-en|莱曼·特朗布尔|Lyman Trumbull}}担任主席的[[美国参议院司法委员会|参议院司法委员会]]开始将各类相关的不同提议合并成修正案。

由[[马萨诸塞州|马萨诸塞州]]联邦参议员{{link-en|查尔斯·萨姆纳|Charles Sumner}}和[[宾夕法尼亚州|宾夕法尼亚州]]众议员{{link-en|赛迪斯·史蒂文斯|Thaddeus Stevens}}为代表的{{link-en|激进派共和党人|Radical Republicans}}希望可以通过一条影响更大的修正案<ref name="Tsesis">{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760}}</ref>{{rp|38-42}}。1864年2月8日，萨姆纳提交了这样一份修正案草案：

<blockquote>“法律面前，人人平等，任何人都不得将他人当做奴隶。国会有权以适当立法来保证美国境内任何地区对此宣言的执行。”<ref name="Stanley">{{cite journal |last1=Amy Dru Stanley |date=2010-06 |title=Instead of Waiting for the Thirteenth Amendment: The War Power, Slave Marriage, and Inviolate Human Rights |journal=American Historical Review |volume=115 |issue=3 |accessdate=2013-06-04}}</ref>{{rp|741-742}}<ref name="SocietyCommission1901">{{cite book |author=Michigan State Historical Society |title=Historical collections |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Yx0zAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA582 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |year=1901 |publisher=Michigan Historical Commission |page=582}}</ref></blockquote>

萨姆纳试图绕开特朗布尔控制的司法委员会来增加自己提议修正案获得通过的希望，但没能成功<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=52-53 |quote=Sumner made his intentions clearer on February 8, when he introduced his constitutional amendment to the Senate and asked that it be referred to his new committee. So desperate was he to make his amendment the final version that he challenged the well-accepted custom of sending proposed amendments to the Judiciary Committee. His Republican colleagues would hear nothing of it.}}</ref>。2月10日，司法委员会向参议院提交了一份基于阿什利、威尔森和亨德森草案的修正案<ref>{{cite web|url=http://13thamendment.harpweek.com/hubpages/CommentaryPage.asp?Commentary=05ProposalPassage |deadurl=yes |title=Congressional Proposals and Senate Passage |publisher=''Harpers Weekly'' |chapter=The Creation of the 13th Amendment |accessdate=2013-09-26 |archivedate=2013-01-15 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115121319/http://13thamendment.harpweek.com/hubpages/CommentaryPage.asp?Commentary=05ProposalPassage }}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=53 |quote=It was no coincidence that Trumbull's announcement came only two days after Sumner had proposed his amendment making all persons 'equal before the law.' The Massachusetts senator had spurred the committee into final action.}}</ref>。

委员会的版本采用了1787年《{{link-en|西北条例|Northwest Ordinance}}》的措辞，该条例当时主要对如今被称为[[美國中西部|美國中西部]]的地区适用，其中规定：“在上述任何地方，奴隶制和强制劳役都不得存在，但作为对于依法判罪的人的犯罪的惩罚除外。”<ref>{{cite journal |last=McAward |first=Jennifer Mason |url=http://www.columbialawreview.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/1769-1810.pdf |title=McCulloch and the Thirteenth Amendment |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=112 |year=2012 |page=1786 |quote=There shall be neither slavery nor involuntary servitude in the said territory, otherwise than in punishment of crimes, whereof the party shall have been duly convicted. |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6ehZI7PeC?url=http://www.columbialawreview.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/1769-1810.pdf |archivedate=2016-01-21 }}</ref>不过，司法委员会采纳亨德森的提议作为新草案基础时也做了一些改动，将其中规定的修正案只需参众两院过半数通过和三分之二的州通过即可生效的规定删除，所以修正案将仍像宪法第五条规定的那样需要两院三分之二多数通过，再由四分之三的州通过<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=54 |quote=Although it made Henderson's amendment the foundation of the final amendment, the committee rejected an article in Henderson's version that allowed the amendment to be adopted by the approval of only a simple majority in Congress and the ratification of only two-thirds of the states.}}</ref>。

联邦参议院于1864年4月8日以38票支持，6票反对通过了修正案。但两个月后联邦众议院的投票结果是93票支持，65票反对，距所需的三分之二多数还差13票，议员投票党派之争非常明显，共和党支持，民主党反对<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|686}}。前{{link-en|自由土壤党|Free Soil Party}}候选人{{link-en|约翰·C·弗里蒙特|John C. Frémont}}在1864年总统选举中威胁以第三方党派候选人参选反对林肯，不过这一次也站到了支持反奴隶制修正案的立场上。共和党方面虽然有林肯在接受总统候选人提名的信中表示支持修正案，但还是没有形成统一战线<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|624-625}}<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|299}}。弗里蒙特于1864年9月22日退出了选举，转为支持林肯<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|639}}。

===国会辩论===
由于没有南方州代表出席，国会也就没有多少议员从[[道德|道德]]或[[宗教信仰|宗教信仰]]角度来为奴隶制辩护。反对修正案的民主党议员通常是以[[联邦主义|联邦主义]]和{{link-en|州权|States' rights}}为理由展开辩论<ref name="Benedict">{{cite journal |last=Benedict |first=Michael Les |url=http://digitalcommons.law.umaryland.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=3535&context=mlr&sei-redir=1&referer=http%3A%2F%2Fscholar.google.com.libproxy.usc.edu%2Fscholar%3Fstart%3D40%26q%3Djohnson%2Bthirteenth%2Bamendment%2Bstate%2Bconvention%26hl%3Den%26as_sdt%3D0%2C36#search=%22johnson%20thirteenth%20amendment%20state%20convention%22 |title=Constitutional Politics, Constitutional Law, and the Thirteenth Amendment |journal=Maryland Law Review |volume=71 |issue=1 |date=2012-10-31 |accessdate=2013-09-26}}</ref>{{rp|179}}。一些议员辩称，这样的修正案违背了宪法的精神，已经变成了一场“革命”而不再是一个有效的“修正”了<ref>{{cite journal |last=Benedict |first=Michael Les |url=http://digitalcommons.law.umaryland.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=3535&context=mlr&sei-redir=1&referer=http%3A%2F%2Fscholar.google.com.libproxy.usc.edu%2Fscholar%3Fstart%3D40%26q%3Djohnson%2Bthirteenth%2Bamendment%2Bstate%2Bconvention%26hl%3Den%26as_sdt%3D0%2C36#search=%22johnson%20thirteenth%20amendment%20state%20convention%22 |title=Constitutional Politics, Constitutional Law, and the Thirteenth Amendment |journal=Maryland Law Review |volume=71 |issue=1 |date=2012-10-31 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |pages=179-180 |quote=(Benedict quotes Sen. Garrett Davis:) there is a boundary between the power of revolution and the power of amendment, which the latter, as established in our Constitution, cannot pass; and that if the proposed change is revolutionary it would be null and void, notwithstanding it might be formally adopted.}}Full text of Davis's speech, with comments from others can be found in ''[http://www.archive.org/stream/greatdebatesina17millgoog/greatdebatesina17millgoog_djvu.txt Great Debates in American History]'' (1918), ed. Marion Mills Miller.</ref>。还有一些反对者警告说，修正案将给予黑人彻底的公民权<ref name="Colbert">{{cite journal |last=Colbert |first=Douglas L. |title=Liberating the Thirteenth Amendment |journal=Harvard Civil Rights – Civil Liberties Law Review |volume=30 |year=1995}}</ref>{{rp|10}}。

共和党议员坚称奴隶制是野蛮的，为了民族进步有必要将之废除<ref name="Benedict" />{{rp|182}}。修正案的支持者还认为奴隶制对白人也存在负面影响，例如由于需要与强制劳役竞争而不得不接受较低的工资水平，以及在南方要求废除奴隶制的白人所遭受的压迫。支持者声称，终结奴隶制可以恢复蓄奴州以审查和恐吓手段所侵犯的[[美国宪法第一修正案|第一修正案]]等多项宪法权利<ref name="Colbert" />{{rp|10}}<ref>{{cite journal |last=TenBroek |first=Jacobus |title=Thirteenth Amendment to the Constitution of the United States: Consummation to Abolition and Key to the Fourteenth Amendment |journal=California Law Review |volume=39 |issue=2 |date=1951-06 |page=180 |quote=It would make it possible for white citizens to exercise their constitutional right under the comity clause to reside in Southern states regardless of their opinions. It would carry out the constitutional declaration "that each citizen of the United States shall have equal privileges in every other state." It would protect citizens in their rights under the First Amendment and comity clause to freedom of speech, freedom of press, freedom of religion and freedom of assembly.}}</ref>。

北方的共和党人和部分民主党人对废除奴隶制的修正案感到激动，并开始就此召开会议并发布决议<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|61}}。许多黑人，特别是南方的黑人，更侧重于把土地所有权和[[教育|教育]]视为解放的关键<ref name="TreleaseXvii">{{cite book |last=Trelease |first=Allen W. |title=White Terror: The Ku Klux Klan Conspiracy and Southern Reconstruction |location=New York |publisher=Harper & Row |year=1971 |page=xvii |quote=Negroes wanted the same freedom that white men enjoyed, with equal prerogatives and opportunities. The educated black minority emphasized civil and political rights more than the masses, who called most of all for land and schools. In an agrarian society, the only kind most of them knew, landownership was associated with freedom, respectability, and the good life. It was almost universally desired by Southern blacks, as it was by landless peasants the world over. Give us our land and we can take care of ourselves, said a group of South Carolina Negroes to a Northern journalist in 1865; without land the old masters can hire us or starve us as they please.}}</ref>。随着奴隶制开始在政治上显得不能成立，北方的民主党人开始逐渐宣布他们对修正案的支持，其中包括[[纽约州|纽约州]]联邦众议员{{link-en|詹姆斯·布鲁克斯|James Brooks (politician)}}<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |page=73 |quote=The first notable convert was Representative James Brooks of New York, who, on the floor of Congress on February 18, 1864, declared that slavery was dying if not already dead, and that his party should stop defending the institution.}}</ref>、[[马里兰州|马里兰州]]联邦参议员{{link-en|勒维迪·约翰逊|Reverdy Johnson}}<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |page=74 |quote=The antislavery amendment caught Johnson's eye, however, because it offered an indisputable constitutional solution to the problem of slavery.}}</ref>和纽约强大的{{link-en|政治机器|political machine}}[[坦慕尼协会|坦慕尼协会]]<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|203}}。

===国会通过===
[[File:AdoptionOf13thAmendment.jpg|thumb]]
林肯一直担心解放奴隶宣言会遭到推翻，或是在战后失效，因此将宪法修正案视为一个更可靠的解决办法<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|312-314}}<ref>{{cite book|first=David Herbert |last=Donald |title=Lincoln |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fuTY3mxs9awC |accessdate=2013-09-26 |year=1996 |publisher=Simon & Schuster |isbn=978-0-684-82535-9 |page=396}}</ref>。由于担心从政治上对自己不利，他对外宣称保持中立<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |page=48 |quote=The president worried that an abolition amendment might foul the political waters. The amendments he had recommended in December 1862 had gone nowhere, mainly because they reflected an outdated program of gradual emancipation, which included compensation and colonization. Moreover, Lincoln knew that he did not have to propose amendments because others more devoted to abolition would, especially if he pointed out the vulnerability of existing emancipation legislation. He was also concerned about negative reactions from conservatives, particularly potential new recruits from the Democrats.}}</ref>。不过，林肯在1864年的党纲中提出用宪法修正案来解决废除奴隶制的问题<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sewanee.edu/faculty/willis/Civil_War/documents/republican.html |title=Republican Party Platform, 1864 |author=Willis, John C. |publisher=University of the South |accessdate=2013-09-26 |quote=Resolved, That as slavery was the cause, and now constitutes the strength of this Rebellion, and as it must be, always and everywhere, hostile to the principles of Republican Government, justice and the National safety demand its utter and complete extirpation from the soil of the Republic; and that, while we uphold and maintain the acts and proclamations by which the Government, in its own defense, has aimed a deathblow at this gigantic evil, we are in favor, furthermore, of such an amendment to the Constitution, to be made by the people in conformity with its provisions, as shall terminate and forever prohibit the existence of Slavery within the limits of the jurisdiction of the United States. |deadurl=yes |archivedate=2013-03-29 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130329015755/http://www.sewanee.edu/faculty/Willis/Civil_War/documents/republican.html }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rmc.library.cornell.edu/vote/1864/index.html |title=1864: The Civil War Election |publisher=Cornell University |work=Get Out the Vote |year=2004 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |archivedate=2013-06-07 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130607064739/http://rmc.library.cornell.edu/vote/1864/index.html |deadurl=no |quote=Despite internal Party conflicts, Republicans rallied around a platform that supported restoration of the Union and the abolition of slavery. }}</ref>。赢得{{link-en|1864年美国总统选举|United States presidential election, 1864}}后，林肯将第十三条修正案的通过当成自己在立法倡议上的头等大事来抓，于国会还处于“跛脚鸭”时期时开始了他的努力<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|686-687}}<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|176-177, 180}}。民众对修正案的支持率开始攀升，林肯又于12月6日对发表的国情咨文中向国会呼吁，修正案的通过和生效只是时间问题，考虑到所有的因素，这个时间应该是越早越好<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |page=178 |quote=there is only a question of ''time'' as to when the proposed amendment will go to the States for their action. And as it is to so go, at all events, may we not agree that the sooner the better?}}</ref>。

林肯指示国务卿威廉·H·苏厄德和联邦众议员{{link-en|约翰·B·埃利|John B. Alley}}等人使用任何必要的手段来确保修正案通过，这些手段包括承诺政府公职、政治捐款及传出民主党议员有意改变投票立场的消息等<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|312-313}}<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|687}}。苏厄德有大笔资金直接进行[[贿赂|贿赂]]。重新在众议院提出修正案的阿什利也成功游说了多位民主党议员转为支持<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|687-689}}。众议员赛迪斯·史蒂文斯之后对此评价道：“19世纪最伟大的举措是在美国最纯洁的人的协助和教唆下，利用[[政治腐敗|腐败]]才通过的。”不过，林肯在这场交易中所扮演的确切角色仍然不明<ref>{{cite book|first=David Herbert |last=Donald |title=Lincoln |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fuTY3mxs9awC |accessdate=2013-09-26 |year=1996 |publisher=Simon & Schuster |isbn=978-0-684-82535-9 |page=554 |quote=the greatest measure of the nineteenth century was passed by corruption, aided and abetted by the purest man in America}}</ref>。

由于有民意上的支持，共和党人在1864年的国会两院换届选举中再度获胜，修正案通过的胜算因此进一步加大，议员们声称这毫无疑问是人民的意旨<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=187 |quote=But the clearest sign of the people's voice against slavery, argued amendment supporters, was the recent election. Following Lincoln's lead, Republican representatives like Godlove S. Orth of Indiana claimed that the vote represented a 'popular verdict . . . in unmistakable language' in favor of the amendment.}}</ref>。反对派则是由1864年民主党副总统候选人，联邦众议员{{link-en|乔治·H·彭德尔顿|George H. Pendleton}}带队<ref name="Goodwin" />{{rp|688}}。共和党则将其要求平等的激烈用词淡化，以求获得更多人的支持<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=191 |quote=The necessity of keeping support for the amendment broad enough to secure its passage created a strange situation. At the moment that Republicans were promoting new, far-reaching legislation for African Americans, they had to keep this legislation detached from the first constitutional amendment dealing exclusively with African American freedom. Republicans thus gave freedom under the antislavery amendment a vague construction: freedom was something more than the absence of chattel slavery but less than absolute equality.}}</ref>。为了安抚一些批评人士对修正案会造成现有社会结构分裂的担忧，一些共和党人还明确承诺修正案不会对父权体制构成任何不利影响<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=191-192 |quote=One of the most effective methods used by amendment supporters to convey the measure's conservative character was to proclaim the permanence of patriarchal power within the American family in the face of this or ''any'' textual change to the Constitution. In response to Democrats who charged that the antislavery was but the first step in a Republican design to dissolve all of society's foundations, including the hierarchical structure of the family, the Iowa Republican John A. Kasson denied any desire to interfere with 'the rights of a husband to a wife' or 'the right of [a] father to his child.}}</ref>。

1月中旬，[[美国众议院议长|众议院议长]][[斯凯勒·科尔法克斯|斯凯勒·科尔法克斯]]估计修正案距通过还差5票，阿什利于是推迟了表决<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|197-198}}。到了这个节骨眼儿上，林肯强化了对修正案的推动，直接向部分国会议员作出情感性的恳求<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |page=198 |quote=It was at this point that the president wheeled into action on behalf of the Amendment […] Now he became more forceful. To one representative whose brother had died in the war, Lincoln said, 'your brother died to save the Republic from death by the slaveholders' rebellion. I wish you could see it to be your duty to vote for the Constitutional amendment ending slavery.}}</ref>。1865年1月31日，众议院再次对修正案发起表决，各方均未知鹿死谁手。所有的共和党人都投了赞成票，还有16位民主党人也投了赞成票，修正案最终以119票赞成，56票反对获得通过，刚刚超过所需要的三分之二多数<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|313}}。众议院为此爆发了庆祝活动，一些议员当众流下了眼泪<ref name="Foner" />{{rp|314}}。黑人自之前一年开始获许旁听众议院的会议，这天许多前来的人们也在旁听席上欢呼<ref name="McPherson" />{{rp|840}}。

1865年2月1日，林肯在修正案上签字，令第十三条修正案成为唯一一条经总统签字并获得通过的[[美国宪法修正案|美国宪法修正案]]，之前[[詹姆斯·布坎南|詹姆斯·布坎南]]于1861年签置的一条支持奴隶制的修正案最终没能在国会通过<ref>{{cite journal |last=Harrison |first=John |title=The Lawfulness of the Reconstruction Amendments |journal=University of Chicago Law Review |volume=68 |issue=2 |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1600377?uid=3737800&uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102684005227 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |page=389 |quote=For reasons that have never been entirely clear, the amendment was presented to the President pursuant to Article I, Section 7, of the Constitution, and signed.|date=Spring 2001}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Thorpe |first=Francis Newton |url=http://archive.org/stream/constitutionalh00thorgoog |title=The Constitutional History of the United States, vol. 3: 1861 – 1895 |location=Chicago |publisher=Callaghan |year=1901 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |page=154 |quote=The President signed the joint resolution on the first of February. Somewhat curiously the signing has only one precedent, and that was in spirit and purpose the complete antithesis of the present act. President Buchanan had signed the proposed Amendment of 1861, which would make slavery national and perpetual.}}</ref>。第十三条修正案的存档副本上就带有林肯的签名，位于通常由众议院议长和参议院议长签名的位置下方，在日期的后面<ref>{{cite web |title=Joint Resolution Submitting 13th Amendment to the States; signed by Abraham Lincoln and Congress |work=The Abraham Lincoln Papers at the Library of Congress: Series 3. General Correspondence. 1837-1897 |publisher=Library of Congress |accessdate=2013-09-26 |url=http://memory.loc.gov/cgi-bin/ampage?collId=mal&fileName=mal3/436/4361100/malpage.db&recNum=0}}</ref>。2月7日，国会通过决议确认总统的签名是不必要的<ref>{{cite book |last=Thorpe |first=Francis Newton |url=http://archive.org/stream/constitutionalh00thorgoog |title=The Constitutional History of the United States, vol. 3: 1861 – 1895 |location=Chicago |publisher=Callaghan |year=1901 |accessdate=2013-09-26 |page=154 |quote=But many held that the President's signature was not essential to an act of this kind, and, on the fourth of February, Senator Trumbull offered a resolution, which was agreed to three days later, that the approval was not required by the Constitution; "that it was contrary to the early decision of the Senate and of the Supreme Court; and that the negative of the President applying only to the ordinary cases of legislation, he had nothing to do with propositions to amend the Constitution.}}</ref>。林肯共计签置了这条修正案的至少14份纪念性副本，2006年，其中的一份副本以180万[[美元|美元]]的价格成交<ref>{{cite web |author=Tammy Webber |url=http://www.boston.com/news/nation/articles/2011/12/06/lincoln_signed_copy_of_13th_amendment_restored/?page=2 |title=Lincoln-signed copy of 13th Amendment restored |publisher=Boston Globe |work=Associated Press |date=2011-12-06}}</ref>。

== 效果 ==
第十三条修正案从法律上禁止奴隶制和强制劳役，除非是依法对犯罪行为加以惩罚，还修正了宪法正文中对奴隶制的相关规定<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |pages=17, 34 |quote=It rendered all clauses directly dealing with slavery null and altered the meaning of other clauses that had originally been designed to protect the institution of slavery.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=13th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution |url=http://www.loc.gov/rr/program/bib/ourdocs/13thamendment.html |work=Primary Documents in American History |publisher=the Library of Congress |accessdate=2013-09-26 |archivedate=2013-05-16 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130516083851/http://www.loc.gov/rr/program/bib/ourdocs/13thamendment.html }}</ref>。虽然肯塔基州的大部分奴隶都已经解放，但仍有6.5至10万人直到12月18日宪法开始生效时才在法律上获得了自由<ref>{{cite book |author1=Lowell Harrison |author2=James C. Klotter |title=A New History of Kentucky |publisher=University Press of Kentucky |year=1997 |page=180 |url=http://books.google.ca/books?id=FdTIIEZ1k2QC&pg=PA180 |isbn=9780813126210 |accessdate=2013-09-26}}</ref><ref name="Forehand" />{{rp|82}}。而特拉华州有大量奴隶已经在内战期间逃亡，所以只有约900人从法律上获得了自由<ref name="Forehand" />{{rp|82}}<ref>{{cite encyclopedia |year=2011 |title =Delaware |encyclopedia=Black America: A State-by-State Historical Encyclopedia |publisher=ABC-CLIO |editor-first=Alan |editor-last=Hornsby |page=139 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fB3rQefP8wQC&pg=PA139&lpg=PA139&dq=%22thirteenth+amendment%22+Delaware+hundred&source=bl&ots=mz-bBgXQI3&sig=kP4IKzKaMmtAFBsqoRA9FRhwdWY&hl=en&sa=X&ei=dcm9UZLOFeXe0QG-w4GoDg&ved=0CHMQ6AEwCA#v=onepage&q=%22thirteenth%20amendment%22%20Delaware%20hundred&f=false |accessdate=2013-09-26}}</ref>。

随着第十三条修正案在国会通过，共和党逐渐开始担心国会中被[[民主党_(美国)|民主党]]主控的南方州议员席位将大幅增长，因为这些州原本大都有较大数量的黑奴。根据原本[[美利坚合众国宪法第一条|宪法第一条]]中的[[五分之三妥协|五分之三妥协]]，每名黑奴按五分之三个自由人计算，而在第十三条修正案通过后，所有黑奴都成了自由人，所以无论获得自由的黑人是否会投票，根据各州人口数分配的[[联邦众议院|联邦众议院]]议席都将出现戏剧性的增长<ref name="Goldstone">{{cite book|title=Inherently Unequal: The Betrayal of Equal Rights by the Supreme Court, 1865–1903|last=Goldstone|first=Lawrence|year=2011|publisher=Walker & Company|isbn=978-0-8027-1792-4|url=http://books.google.com/books/about/Inherently_Unequal.html?id=r3CTEefvbQUC|ref=harv |accessdate=2013-09-02}}</ref>{{rp|22}}<ref name="Stromberg">{{cite book |last=Stromberg |first=Joseph R. |title=A Plain Folk Perspective on Reconstruction, State-Building, Ideology, and Economic Spoils |publisher=Journal of Libertarian Studies |date=Spring 2002}}</ref>{{rp|111}}。共和党希望通过吸纳和保护新增黑人选民的选票来抵消民主党的增长。<ref name="Goldstone" />{{rp|22}}<ref>{{cite book |title=The Fourteenth Amendment: From Political Principle to Judicial Doctrine |last=Nelson |first=William E. |year=1988 |publisher=Harvard University Press |isbn=978-0-674-04142-4 |page=47 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=VMCXjRyyKTQC&pg=PA46&dq=thirteenth+amendment+%22three+fifths%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=Bz-xUemzGMX8rAGamYDgDQ&ved=0CEQQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=thirteenth%20amendment%20%22three%20fifths%22&f=false |accessdate=2013-09-03}}</ref><ref name="Stromberg" />{{rp|112}}

正式的解放并没有改变留在南方大多数黑人的经济状况。<ref>{{cite book |last=Trelease |first=Allen W. |title=White Terror: The Ku Klux Klan Conspiracy and Southern Reconstruction |location=New York |publisher=Harper & Row |year=1971 |page=xviii |quote=The truth seems to be that, after a brief exulation with the idea of freedom, Negroes realized that their position was hardly changed; they continued to live and work much as they had before.}}</ref>由于修正案仍然允许用强制劳役来惩罚犯罪行为，南方各州于是开始制订一系列环环相扣的法律来让黑人沦为刑事罪犯<ref name="Blackmon">{{cite book |last=Blackmon |first=Douglas A. |title=Slavery by Another Name: The Re-Enslavement of Black Americans from the Civil War to World War II |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=wchHxFsOREcC |date=2008-03-25 |publisher=Knopf Doubleday Publishing Group |isbn=978-0-385-50625-0 |accessdate=2013-09-27}}</ref>{{rp|53}}。这些在黑人获得解放后通过或更新的法律被统称为[[黑人法令|黑人法令]]<ref name="Stromberg" />{{rp|111}}。密西西比州是第一个通过这类法令的州，这项1865年通过的法律题为《赋予自由民公民权利的法案》（{{lang|en|An Act to confer Civil Rights on Freedmen}}）<ref name=Novak2>{{cite book |last=Novak |first=Daniel A. |title=The Wheel of Servitude: Black Forced Labor after Slavery |publisher=University Press of Kentucky |year=1978 |isbn=0813113717 |page=2}}</ref>，要求黑人工人在每年1月1日与白人农场主签订合同，否则将面临流浪罪的惩罚<ref name="Blackmon" />{{rp|53}}。黑人如果犯下轻盗窃罪、讲粗话或是在日落后出售[[棉花|棉花]]，都会被判处强制劳役<ref name="Blackmon" />{{rp|100}}。各州通过了新的，更严格的流浪法，选择性地针对那些没有白人保护的黑人<ref name="Blackmon" />{{rp|53}}<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|51-52}}，被定罪的黑人将被卖到农场、工厂、伐木营地、采石场和矿山<ref name="Blackmon" />{{rp|6}}。

1865年11月批准第十三条修正案后，南卡罗莱纳州议会立即开始制订黑人法令<ref>{{cite book |last=Vorenberg |first=Michael |accessdate=2013-09-25 |url=http://www.questia.com/read/105900244/final-freedom-the-civil-war-the-abolition-of-slavery |title=Final Freedom: The Civil War, the Abolition of Slavery, and the Thirteenth Amendment |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=9781139428002 |pages=230-231 |quote=The black codes were a violation of freedom of contract, one of the civil rights that Republicans expected to flow from the amendment. 有些州规定了长度没有限制的儿童“学徒”时期<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|50}}，有些法律表面看来不是特别针对黑人，而是对农场工人有影响，而绝大部分的农场工人又都是黑人。同时还有多个州通过法律主动防止黑人获得物业<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|51}}。

南方企业主开始重新启用一种名叫{{link-en|劳役偿债制|peonage}}的奴役系统，其手下工人为偿还被陷害而借下的贷款被迫无限期地为企业主工作<ref>{{cite journal |last=Wolff |first=Tobias Barrington |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1123649?uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102687014417 |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and Slavery in the Global Economy |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=102 |issue=4 |date=2002-05 |accessdate=2013-09-27 |page=981 |quote=Peonage was a system of forced labor that depended upon the indebtedness of a worker, rather than an actual property right in a slave, as the means of compelling work.  A prospective employer would offer a laborer a "loan" or "advance" on his wages, typically as a condition of employment, and then use the newly created debt to compel the worker to remain on the job for as long as the employer wished.}}</ref><ref name=Wolff982b />，利用南方的重建，企业主诱捕了南方的很大一部分黑人，令其身陷劳役偿债制中无法自拔<ref>{{cite journal |last=Wolff |first=Tobias Barrington |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1123649?uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102687014417 |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and Slavery in the Global Economy |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=102 |issue=4 |date=2002-05 |accessdate=2013-09-27 |page=982 |quote=Legally sanctioned peonage arrangements blossomed in the South following the Civil War and continued into the twentieth century. According to the Professor Jacqueline Jones, 'perhaps as many as one-third of all [sharecropping farmers] in Alabama, Mississippi, and George were being held against their will in 1900.}}</ref>。这些工人仍然一贫如洗，还被逼迫从事危险性较高的工作，并进一步在南方[[吉姆·克勞法|吉姆·克勞法]]的管控下合法地受到限制和迫害<ref name="Wolff982b">{{cite journal |last=Wolff |first=Tobias Barrington |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1123649?uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102687014417 |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and Slavery in the Global Economy |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=102 |issue=4 |date=2002-05 |accessdate=2013-09-27 |page=982 |quote=Not surprisingly, employers used peonage arrangements primarily in industries that involved hazardous working conditions and very low pay. While black workers were not the exclusive victims of peonage arrangements in America, they suffered under its yoke in vastly disproportionate numbers. Along with Jim Crow laws that segregated transportation and public facilities, these laws helped to restrict the movement of freed black workers and thereby keep them in a state of poverty and vulnerability.}}</ref>。劳役偿债制与奴隶制的不同点在于，前者不是严格的世袭制度，也不允许像后者那样完全把人当成财产来销售。但是，一个人的债务仍然可以进行销售，所以实际效果也和奴隶制的直接销售奴隶类似，整个系统的运作在许多方面都和南北战争前的奴隶制非常相似<ref>{{cite journal |last=Wolff |first=Tobias Barrington |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1123649?uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102687014417 |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and Slavery in the Global Economy |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=102 |issue=4 |date=2002-05 |accessdate=2013-09-27 |page=982 |quote=It did not recognize a property right in a human being (a peon could not be sold in the manner of a slave); and the condition of peonage did not work 'corruption of blood' and travel to the children of the worker. Yet the practice unquestionably reproduced many of the immediate practical realities of slavery—a vast underclass of laborers, held to their jobs by force of law and threat of imprisonment, with few if any opportunities for escape.}}</ref>。

==国会和行政执行==
{{link-en|1866年民权法|Civil Rights Act of 1866}}是国会首度行使第十三条修正案赋予的立法权，该法保证了黑人的公民权和平等法律保护，但没有保护投票权。修正案还成为多项{{link-en|自由民管理局法案|Freedmen's Bureau bills}}的授权基础。总统安德鲁·约翰逊否决了这些法案，但国会以绝对多数推翻了他的否决，通过了1866年民权法和第二部自由民管理局法案。<ref name="Vorenberg" />{{rp|233-234}}<ref>{{cite journal|last=DuBois |first=W. E. B. |url=http://www.theatlantic.com/past/docs/issues/01mar/dubois2.htm |title=The Freedmen's Bureau |accessdate=2013-09-27 |publisher=The Atlantic |date=1901-03 |pages=354-365 |volume=87 |issue=519 |deadurl=no |archivedate=2012-08-31 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120831043237/http://www.theatlantic.com/past/docs/issues/01mar/dubois2.htm }}</ref>

包括特朗布尔和威尔森在内的法案支持者认为，第十三条修正案的第二款授权联邦政府为各州的公民权利立法。其他人对此不同意，认为新出现的不平等与之前奴隶制所导致的不同<ref name="McAward" />{{rp|1788-1790}}。由于担心未来反对者会再次试图推翻现有立法，也为国会立法提供更充足的理据，国会和各州增加了进一步的宪法保护：1868年通过的第十四条修正案对美国公民作出了定义，并提供平等的法律保护，1870年通过的第十五条修正案则保护了黑人的投票权。<ref name="Goldstone" />{{rp|23-24}}

{{link-en|自由民管理局|Freedmen's Bureau}}在部分地区为受黑人法案管制的人们提供一定程度的支持<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |pages=50-51 |quote=Blacks applied to local provost marshalls and Freedmen's Bureau for help against these child abductions, particularly in those cases where children were taken from living parents. Jack Prince asked for help when a woman bound his maternal niece. Sally Hunter requested assistance to obtain the release of her two nieces. Bureau officials finally put an end to the system of indenture in 1867.}}</ref>，第十三条修正案也是该局在肯塔基州开展工作的法律依据。民权法案则通过允许黑人在联邦法院进行诉讼来绕过地方司法管辖所存在的[[种族主义|种族主义]]行径。<ref name="Forehand" />{{rp|99-100, 105}}

1870至1871年的执法法和1875年民权法案旨在打击白人至上主义者对黑人的暴力和恐吓行为，同时也部分起到了结束南方黑人奴隶处境的作用<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|66-67}}。不过，这些法律的效果由于政治意愿的减弱和联邦政府在南部失去了权威而削弱，特别是在共和党人为保住总统席位而达成{{link-en|1877年妥协|Compromise of 1877}}结束[[美國重建時期|重建]]以后<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |pages=56-57, 60-61 |quote=If the Republicans had hoped to gradually use section 2 of the Thirteenth Amendment to pass Reconstruction legislation, they would soon learn that President Johnson, using his veto power, would make increasingly more difficult the passage of any measure augmenting the power of the national government. Further, with time, even leading antislavery Republicans would become less adamant and more willing to reconcile with the South than protect the rights of the newly freed. This was clear by the time Horace Greely accepted the Democratic nomination for president in 1872 and even more when President Rutherford B. Hayes entered the Compromise of 1877, agreeing to withdraw federal troops from the South.}}</ref>。

=== 劳役偿债制法 ===
国会通过{{link-en|1867年劳役偿债制法|Peonage Act of 1867}}废止了劳役偿债制<ref name="Goluboff">{{cite journal |last=Goluboff |first=Risa L. |title=http://scholarship.law.duke.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1116&context=dlj |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and the Lost Origins of Civil Rights |journal=Duke Law Journal |volume=50 |year=2001 |accessdate=2013-09-27 |format=[[PDF|PDF]]}}</ref>{{rp|1638}}，特别禁止任何人以任何自愿或被迫的形式以做苦工来偿还任何债务或义务<ref>Soifer, “Prohibition of Voluntary Peonage” (2012), p. 1617.</ref>。

1939年，[[联邦司法部|联邦司法部]]建立了民权分支，主要注重于对第一修正案和劳工权利的保护<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1616}}。[[第二次世界大战|第二次世界大战]]也带来了国内和国外对奴隶制和强制劳役问题越来越多的关注，对[[极权主义|极权主义]]的推敲也逐渐增多<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1619-1621}}。美国力求以打击南方劳役偿债制来提高其在种族问题上的公信力和国际形象<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1626-1628}}。在[[联邦司法部长|联邦司法部长]][[弗朗西斯·貝弗利·比德尔|弗朗西斯·貝弗利·比德尔]]的带领下，民权部门援引第十三条宪法修正案和重建时期的立法来作为其行动的法律依据<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1629, 1635}}。

1947年，联邦司法部成功检控伊丽莎白·英格尔斯（{{lang|en|Elizabeth Ingalls}}）奴役家仆多拉·L·琼斯（{{lang|en|Dora L. Jones}}）。法院认为琼斯“是一个完全受被告意志操控的人，她没有行动自由，完全是在被告的控制之下进行服务，并且她对被告的服务是被强迫的。”<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1668}}第十三条修正案在这一期间受到了的关注大幅增加，但从1954年的[[布朗訴托皮卡教育局案|布朗訴托皮卡教育局案]]到1968年的{{link-en|琼斯诉阿尔弗雷德H·梅耶公司案|Jones v. Alfred H. Mayer Co.}}，修正案的风头都远远不及第十四条修正案<ref name="Goluboff" />{{rp|1680-1683}}。

====联邦司法部定义====
;劳役偿债制和强制劳役：
劳役偿债制指一个人因为债务支付问题而在非自愿情况下通过劳役来清偿债务的情况。强制劳役指一个人被他人以武力胁迫或是以武力相要胁，亦或被威胁受法律强制手段打击而被迫作他人的奴隶，或是被迫劳役和提供服务。这其中还包括他人使用武力或威胁使用武力亦或威胁通过法律强制手段而造成的“恐惧气氛”下被迫工作。<ref name="dojd">{{cite web|url=http://www.justice.gov/crt/about/crm/1581fin.php |title=INVOLUNTARY SERVITUDE, FORCED LABOR, and SEX TRAFFICKING STATUTES ENFORCED |publisher=U.S. Department of Justice |deadurl=no |accessdate=2013-09-27 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130729110337/http://www.justice.gov/crt/about/crm/1581fin.php |archivedate=2013-07-29 }}</ref>在1911年的{{link-en|贝利诉阿拉巴马州案|Bailey v. Alabama}}中，联邦最高法院裁定债役法（指强制他人参加劳役来偿还债务的法律）违反了第十三条修正案中禁止强制劳役的规定<ref>''Bailey v. Alabama'', {{ussc|219|219|1911}}</ref>。

一些学者和法院认为一些个人服务合同在违约补救措施中要求{{link-en|具体表现|specific performance}}的做法属于强制劳役的一种形式，不过其他法院和学者批驳了这一看法。在学术界和一些地方司法管辖区，这样的规则较为流行，但从未获得过上级法院的支持。<ref>{{cite web|url=http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1114799 |title=Specific Performance and the Thirteenth Amendment by Nathan Oman |publisher=SSRN |accessdate=2013-09-27 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120918013649/http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1114799 |archivedate=2012-09-18 |deadurl=no }}</ref>

;强迫劳动：
强迫劳动或服务包括：
#受到严重的身体约束或伤害的威胁而被迫参加劳动；
#以任何计划、方案或模式旨在让人相信，自己如果不参加某些劳动或服务，将会遭到严重的身体约束或伤害；
#滥用或威胁滥用法律或法律程序来迫使他人参加劳动。<ref name="dojd" /><ref>According to the Dept. of Justice, "Congress enacted § 1589 in response to the Supreme Court’s decision in ''United States v. Kozminski'', {{ussc|487|931|1988}}, which interpreted § 1584 to require the use or threatened use of physical or legal coercion. Section 1589 broadens the definition of the kinds of coercion that might result in forced labor."</ref>

==司法解读==
与另外两条重建修正案不同，第十三条修正案很少在之后的判例法中引用。正如[[历史学家|历史学家]]艾米·德鲁·斯坦利（{{lang|en|Amy Dru Stanley}}）所总结的那样：“除了曲指可数的几个推翻劳役偿债制、公然的强制劳役和某些情况下基于种族的暴力和歧视行为的里程碑式判决外，第十三条修正案从来都不是权利诉求的一个有力来源。”<ref name="Stanley" />{{rp|735}}<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-3425002519.html |title=Thirteenth Amendment (Judicial Interpretation) |author=Kenneth L. Karst |date=2000-01-01 |work=Encyclopedia of the American Constitution |publisher={{Subscription required|via=HighBeam Research}} |accessdate=2013-09-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150328142003/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-3425002519.html |archive-date=2015-03-28 |dead-url=yes }}</ref>

===黑人奴隶及其后裔 ===
1866年的美国诉罗德岛州案是有关第十三条修正案最早的案件之一，该案考验的是1866年民权法案中有关黑人可以在联邦法院申诉这一规定的合宪性。当时肯塔基州有法律禁止黑人到法庭作证指控白人，所以“非洲种族美国公民”南希·塔尔博特（{{lang|en|Nancy Talbot}}）只能到联邦法院起诉一名对她实施了[[抢劫|抢劫]]的白人。塔尔博特到联邦法院起诉后，肯塔基州最高法院裁定这一联邦法令违宪。担任肯塔基巡回法院法官的[[联邦最高法院大法官|联邦最高法院大法官]]{{link-en|诺亚·斯韦恩|Noah Swayne}}推翻了肯塔基州最高法院的判决，认为如果没有民权法中的这一执法手段，奴隶制不会真正被废除。<ref>{{cite web |url=http://zh.scribd.com/doc/20825887/United-States-v-Rhodes-27-f-Cas-785-1866 |title=United States v Rhodes, 27 f Cas 785 (1866) |accessdate=2013-09-27 |work=Scribd. |archivedate=2013-04-02 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130402232213/http://zh.scribd.com/doc/20825887/United-States-v-Rhodes-27-f-Cas-785-1866 }}</ref><ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|62-63}}<ref>{{cite article|author=Seth P. Waxman |archivedate=2013-07-02 |deadurl=no |url=http://scholarship.law.georgetown.edu/facpub/287/ |title=Twins at Birth: Civil Rights and the Role of the Solicitor General |publisher=Indiana Law Journal |volume=75 |year=2000 |pages=1302-1303 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702171706/http://scholarship.law.georgetown.edu/facpub/287/ }}</ref>。而在1867年的一个案件中，[[美国首席大法官|美国首席大法官]][[萨蒙·蔡斯|萨蒙·蔡斯]]下令恢复马里兰州一位前奴隶的自由，这位名叫伊丽莎白·特纳的女子被之前的主人以契约束缚，成为事实上的奴隶<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|63-64}}。

在1872年的布莱尤诉美国案中，联邦最高法院受理了另一个有关肯塔基州联邦法院民权法案的诉讼。约翰·布莱尤（{{lang|en|John Bylew}}）和乔治·肯纳德（{{lang|en|George Kennard}}）是两个白人，他们一起造访了住在木屋中的一户姓福斯特的黑人家庭。布莱尤显然因什么事情而对理查德·福斯特（{{lang|en|Richard Foster}}）非常生气，用斧头两次打中对方的头部。然后与肯纳德一起杀死了理查德的[[母亲|母亲]]莎莉·福斯特（{{lang|en|Sallie Foster}}）和[[父亲|父亲]]杰克·福斯特（{{lang|en|Jack Foster}}），还杀了其失明的祖母露西·阿姆斯特朗（{{lang|en|Lucy Armstrong}}），重伤了福斯特的两名幼女。肯塔基州法院不允许福斯特的孩子们出庭作证指控布莱尤和肯纳德。但联邦法院依据民权法案授权介入，判处两名被告[[谋杀|谋杀]]罪名成立。最高法院最终以5比2判决福斯特的孩子们也不能在联邦法院作证，因为只有在世的人才能够利用这一法案（意思是原告的家人如果没有死，才能亲自出庭做证，孩子并没有就长辈的谋杀罪作证的权利）。法院这一裁决意味着第十三条修正案没有允许联邦政府在谋杀案中进行司法补救。斯韦恩和{{link-en|约瑟夫·P.布拉德利|Joseph P. Bradley}}两名大法官投下了反对票，认为第十三条修正案如果要产生有意义的影响，就必须应对这类系统性的种族压迫。<ref>''Blyew v. U.S.'', {{ussc|80|581|1871}}</ref><ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|64-66}}

从技术上来说，布莱尤案是一个对州和联邦法院都有约束力的先例，这导致国会由第十三条修正案所赋予的权力受到明显削弱。最高法院之后继续坚持这一立场，在1873年的{{link-en|屠宰场案|Slaughter-House Cases}}中，法院支持了由州政府保障的白人屠夫垄断地位<ref>''The Slaughter-House Cases'', {{ussc|83|36|1873}}</ref>。在1876年的{{link-en|美国诉克鲁克香克案|United States v. Cruikshank}}中，法院无视了巡回法院法官有关第十三条修正案为依据的意见，免除了{{link-en|科尔法克斯大屠杀|Colfax massacre}}凶手的刑事责任，并裁定{{link-en|1870年执法法|Enforcement Act of 1870}}无效。<ref>''United States v. Cruikshank'', {{ussc|92|542|1876}}</ref><ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|66-67}}

[[File:John-Marshall-Harlan-sketch.jpg|thumb]]

在1883年的一组{{link-en|民权案件|Civil Rights Cases}}中，最高法院把5个有关{{link-en|1875年民权法案|Civil Rights Act of 1875}}的案件合并审查，该法禁止在“旅馆、地面或水域的公共交通工具、剧院和其它公共娱乐场所”的种族歧视行为。法院认为第十三条修正案对大部分非政府行为的种族歧视行径没有约束力。<ref name="Goldstone" />{{rp|122}}大法官布拉德利在多数意见中认为，第十三条修正案赋予国会取缔奴隶制的权力，但这种权力对个人行为无效，他还认为第十三条修正案中保护的公民基本权力与人们在社会中生活的社会权利有所区别<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|70}}。多数意见认为，1875年民权法案将导致有关奴隶制的争论深入到现实生活中的每一个角落，但人们有时候会因为个人生计、工作、业务等方面的需要来作出一些可能会被认为存在歧视的行为，据此而以法律加以限制和惩罚是不妥当的<ref>{{cite book|title=Appleton's Annual Cyclopædia and Register of Important Events of the Year ... |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=YZ5RAAAAYAAJ |accessdate=2013-09-27 |year=1888 |publisher=D. Appleton & Company |page=132}}</ref>。肯塔基州[[律师|律师]]出身的大法官[[約翰·馬歇爾·哈倫|約翰·馬歇爾·哈倫]]在目睹了有组织的种族暴力活动后改变了自己对民权法案的看法，他在法院唯一的反对意见中称，这种由团体或个人在行使公共或准公共职能时表现出的歧视是一种代表性的奴役制度，国会应当对其加以取缔。<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|73}}<ref>''Civil Rights Cases'', 109 U.S. 3 (1883)</ref>

在1896年的[[普莱西诉弗格森案|普莱西诉弗格森案]]中，普莱西的律师向法院申诉称[[种族隔离|种族隔离]]制度从实质上违反了第十三条修正案，但最高法院没有接受这一说法，以7比1的投票结果支持了州法律中根据[[隔离但平等|隔离但平等]]原则强制种族隔离的法律。法院认为，白人和有色人种由于肤色上永远都有显著的区别，仅仅是从法律上加以分隔并不会对两个种族在法律上的平等地位构成威胁，也不意味着强制劳役会重新出现。<ref name="Goldstone" />{{rp|162, 164-165}}哈伦大法官再次写下了自己孤独的异议，称再也不应该用这种所谓“平等”的伪装去继续误导任何人了。<ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|78}}<ref>''Plessy v. Ferguson'', {{ussc|163|537|1896}}</ref>

在1906年的{{link-en|霍奇斯诉美国案|Hodges v. United States}}中，法院推翻了一条联邦法规，该法规中对任何两个或更多数目的人阴谋损害、欺压、威胁或恐吓任何人自由行使或享受宪法和美国法律保障的权利或特权的行为提供了惩罚依据。在该案中，阿肯色州的一组白人密谋以暴力手段阻止8名黑人到一家木材厂工作，这些白人被联邦大陪审员定罪。最高法院判决该联邦法规没有第十三条修正案的授权。认为这不过是个人行为，不受宪法修正案的限制。哈伦再次写下反对意见，坚持自己有关第十三条修正案的保护应当不只是“人身自由”而已。<ref>Hodges v. United States, {{ussc|203|1|1906}}</ref><ref name="Tsesis" />{{rp|79-80}}法院又在1922年的科里根诉巴克利案判决中再次重申了霍奇斯案的意见，称第十三条修正案对[[地役权|地役权]]不适用<ref>''Corrigan v. Buckley'', {{ussc|271|323|1926}}</ref>。

联邦民权法案在南方的实施造就了不计其数的劳役偿债制案件，经历了漫长的司法之路。最高法院在1905年的克莱亚特诉美国案中裁决，劳役偿债制属于强制劳役。法院认为，虽然雇员证词中称，有些工人是自愿签订合同，但劳役偿债制从本质上就是非自愿的。<ref name="Wolff">{{cite journal |last=Wolff |first=Tobias Barrington |url=http://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1123649?uid=2&uid=4&sid=21102687014417 |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and Slavery in the Global Economy |journal=Columbia Law Review |volume=102 |issue=4 |date=2002-05 |accessdate=2013-09-27}}</ref>{{rp|983}}<ref>''Clyatt v. United States'', {{ussc|197|207|1905}}</ref>

==== 琼斯案及后续 ====
法律史将1968年的{{link-en|琼斯诉艾尔弗雷德·H·迈耶公司案|Jones v. Alfred H. Mayer Co.}}视为第十三条修正案判例法的一个转折点<ref name="Tsesis3" /><ref name="Colbert" />{{rp|2}}。最高法院在该案判决认为国会可以“合理”地对个人的种族歧视和强制劳役行为作出限制<ref name="Tsesis3">{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |page=3 |quote=After Reconstruction, however, a series of Supreme Court decisions substantially diminished the amendment's significance in achieving genuine liberation. The Court did not revisit the amendment's meaning until 1968, during the heyday of the Civil Rights movement. In ''Jones v. Alfred H. Mayer'', the Court found that the Thirteenth Amendment not only ended unrecompensed, forced labor but that its second section also empowered Congress to develop legislation that is 'rationally' related to ending any remaining 'badges and incidents of servitude'.}}</ref>。琼斯夫妇居住在密苏里州的[[聖路易斯縣_(密蘇里州)|聖路易斯縣]]，他们起诉的是当地一家拒绝卖房子给他们的[[房地产|房地产]]公司。法院的判决是以7比2的投票结果作出的。判决书中称内战结束后用来限制黑人自由行使权利的黑人法令是奴役系统的替代品，将黑人排除在白人社会以外。这种因他人肤色而拒绝其购买房产意向的做法无疑也是奴隶制的遗留。判决书中还表示，无论北方还是南方的黑人公民，都视第十三条修正案为自由的保证，这些自由包括他们的来去自由和买卖自由。如果国会连黑人用同样的钱买到和白人同样东西，住在白人能住的房里这样的权利都不能保证，那么修正案也就成了一张白纸。如果国会连做为一个自由公民的这一点权利都保护不了，那么第十三条修正案就做出了一个这个国家无法遵守的承诺。<ref>{{cite web |author=Alison Shay |url=https://lcrm.lib.unc.edu/blog/index.php/2012/06/17/remembering-jones-v-alfred-h-mayer-co/ |title=Remembering Jones v. Alfred H. Mayer Co. |publisher=Publishing the Long Civil Rights Movement |date=2012-06-17 |accessdate=2013-09-28 |quote=...Just as the Black Codes, enacted after the Civil War to restrict the free exercise of those rights, were substitutes for the slave system, so the exclusion of Negroes from white communities became a substitute for the Black Codes. And when racial discrimination herds men into ghettos and makes their ability to buy property turn on the color of their skin, then it too is a relic of slavery. Negro citizens, North and South, who saw in the Thirteenth Amendment a promise of freedom—freedom to “go and come at pleasure” and to “buy and sell when they please”—would be left with “a mere paper guarantee” if Congress were powerless to assure that a dollar in the hands of a Negro will purchase the same thing as a dollar in the hands of a white man. At the very least, the freedom that Congress is empowered to secure under the Thirteenth Amendment includes the freedom to buy whatever a white man can buy, the right to live wherever a white man can live. If Congress cannot say that being a free man means at least this much, then the Thirteenth Amendment made a promise the Nation cannot keep. |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928013303/https://lcrm.lib.unc.edu/blog/index.php/2012/06/17/remembering-jones-v-alfred-h-mayer-co/ |archivedate=2013-09-28 }}</ref>这一案件的裁决也就将美国当代社会中的种族主义问题与历史上的奴隶制问题联系了起来<ref name="Colbert" />{{rp|3-4}}。

琼斯案的先例之后被用来支持国会保护外来务工人员和打击性拐卖活动的行动<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |page=3 |quote=The Court's holding in ''Jones'' enables Congress to pass statutes against present-day human rights violations, such as the trafficking of foreign workers as sex slaves and the exploitation of migrant agricultural workers as peons.}}</ref>。第十三条修正案的直接执法权与第十四条修正案形成了鲜明的对比，后者只能对抗政府行为下的体制性歧视，而前者可以针对个人和团体行为<ref>{{cite book |last=Tsesis |first=Alexander |title=The Thirteenth Amendment and American Freedom: A Legal History |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |year=2004 |isbn=0814782760 |pages=112-113 |quote=... the Thirteenth Amendment remains the principal constitutional source requiring the federal government to protect individual liberties against arbitrary private and public infringements that resemble the incidents of involuntary servitude. Moreover, the Thirteenth Amendment is a positive injunction requiring Congress to pass laws to that end, while the Fourteenth Amendment is 'responsive' to 'unconstitutional behavior.'}}</ref>。

===其他强制劳役案件===
对于不是黑人（非裔）后裔美国公民的强制劳役案件，最高法院给予了较为狭隘的解释。在1897年的罗伯逊诉鲍德温案中，几位水手拒绝履行出海义务后被强迫上船，返回后因未履行职责而遭到起诉，依据是一项联邦法律。他们于是向法院起诉，称该法律违反第十三条修正案中的强制劳役禁令。法院的多数意见认为，第十三条修正案不是用来对这类一直存在特殊性的工作加以干涉的。“在知情和愿意的情况下进入的受束缚状态不能被称为非自愿的”，士兵、海员等工作属于这些特殊性的例外。哈伦大法官像多个案件中一样独自给出了异议，对第十三条修正案的保护给予较为宽泛的解释。“一个人立约为他人的私事提供个人服务的情况，从他被迫违背其意愿继续这种服务之时起，就变成了非自愿束缚的情况。”<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mgyj.com/american_studies/2009/second/second05.html |title=美国法律对劳工自由流动所加限制的历史演变——“自由资本主义阶段”之真实性质疑 |author=韩铁 |chapter=三、美国劳工自由流动法律保障的逐步建立 |publisher=美国研究 |date=2009-02 |accessdate=2013-09-28}}</ref><ref name="Wolff" />{{rp|977}}<ref>''Robertson v. Baldwin'', {{ussc|165|275|1897}}</ref>

在1918年包括阿维尔诉美国案在内的一系列{{link-en|选择性法律草案案件|Selective Draft Law Cases}}中，最高法院认为征兵制不属于强制劳役<ref>''Selective Draft Law Cases'', {{ussc|245|366|1918}}; ''Arver v. United States'', {{ussc|245|366|1918}}; ''Grahl v. United States'', Wangerin v. United States'', {{ussc|245|474|1918}}; ''Wangerin v. United States'', {{ussc|245|474|1918}}; ''Kramer v. United States'', {{ussc|245|478|1918}}; ''Graubard v. United States'', {{ussc|245|366|1918}}</ref>。在1988年的{{link-en|美国诉科兹明斯基案|United States v. Kozminski}}中<ref name="Koz">United States v. Kozminski, {{ussc|487|931|1988}}</ref>，最高法院认为以心理胁迫达成的强制劳役不受第十三条修正案限制<ref>{{cite web|archivedate=2013-01-14 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130114125541/http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/GPO-CONAN-1992/html/GPO-CONAN-1992-10-14.htm |url=http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/GPO-CONAN-1992/html/GPO-CONAN-1992-10-14.htm |title=Thirteenth Amendment—Slavery and Involuntary Servitude |work=GPO Access |publisher=U.S. Government Printing Office |page=1557 |accessdate=2013-09-28 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author=Risa Goluboff |year=2001 |title=The 13th Amendment and the Lost Origins of Civil Rights |publisher=Duke Law Journal |volume=50 |issue=228 |page=1609}}</ref>。这一判决将第十三条修正案禁止的强制劳役范围限制在：1、以实际武力相威胁；2、以政府实际制订的法律相威胁；3、对未成年人或移民、智商不健全人士进行欺诈或欺骗来加以奴役<ref name="Koz" />。

[[美国联邦上诉法院|美国联邦上诉法院]]先后在多个案件中裁决一些高校要求学生提供{{link-en|社区服务|community service}}后才能毕业的做法不违反第十三条修正案。<ref>{{cite journal |last=Loupe |first=Diane |date=2000-08 |title=Community Service: Mandatory or Voluntary? – Industry Overview |journal=School Administrator |page=8 |archivedate=2011-07-08 |deadurl=yes |url=http://www.aasa.org/SchoolAdministratorArticle.aspx?id=14442/ |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708133632/http://www.aasa.org/SchoolAdministratorArticle.aspx?id=14442 }}</ref>

== 之前提出过的第十三条修正案 ==
[[美利坚合众国宪法第十二条修正案|美利坚合众国宪法第十二条修正案]]通过后，一直过了超过60年才有了第十三条修正案的通过。在这段时间里，国会还曾提出过两条宪法修正案，但均未获得足够州的批准。

* {{link-en|贵族头衔修正案|Titles of Nobility Amendment}}由联邦参议院1810年4月26日通过，同年5月10日在联邦众议院通过，但一共只得到了12个州的批准。该修正案规定，如果任何一位美国公民接受别国的贵族头衔或是在没有得到国会同意的情况下接受任何别国的报酬，则将被剥夺其公民身份。<ref>{{cite encyclopedia |year=2009 |title=Titles of Nobility |encyclopedia=Encyclopedia of the United States Constitution|publisher=Infobase|last=Mark W. Podvia |editor-first=David Andrew Schultz |pages=738-739 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=f7m713xwK58C&pg=PA739&dq=%22Titles+of+Nobility%22+1810&hl=en&sa=X&ei=lguuUYfrJ9Kj4APRqYGwDA&ved=0CEMQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=%22Titles%20of%20Nobility%22%201810&f=false |accessdate=2013-09-28}}</ref>
