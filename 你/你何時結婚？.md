《'''你何時結婚？'''》（{{lang-ty|Nafea faa ipoipo?}}）是1892年法國畫家[[保羅·高更|保羅·高更]]繪製的一幅油畫，原先由魯道夫·施特赫林借給[[瑞士|瑞士]]的[[巴塞爾美術館|巴塞爾美術館]]展覽，2015年2月被出售給未知的客戶，據稱很可能是[[卡塔爾博物館管理局|卡塔爾博物館管理局]]，金額據估計超過2億美金，使其一躍成為[[最昂贵绘画列表|世界上最昂貴的畫作]]之一。實際上一度有媒體認爲它的交易金额为3亿，打破當時的記錄<ref name="BBC">{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-31183733 |title=Gauguin painting breaks sale record at nearly $300m |accessdate=7 February 2015 |publisher=BBC News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.chinanews.com/gj/2015/02-08/7044693.shtml |title=最昂贵画作：法国画家高更油画拍出3亿美元(图)|publisher=中新網|date=2015-02-08}}</ref>，但後來發現並沒有。<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/03/arts/design/lawsuit-reveals-gauguin-painting-was-not-worlds-most-expensive.html|title=Lawsuit Reveals Gauguin Painting Was Not World’s Most Expensive|last=Haigney|first=Sophie|newspaper=''[[The_New_York_Times|The New York Times]]''|date=July 3, 2017|accessdate=July 3, 2017}}</ref>

==背景==
1891年高更首次旅行至[[塔希提|塔希提]]，他寄希望於在這裡創作出純粹、純潔的藝術，<ref name="GI">{{cite web | url=http://www.galleryintell.com/artex/nafea-fas-ipoipo-will-marry-paul-gauguin/ | title=Nafea fas ipoipo (When will you marry) by Paul Gauguin | work=galleryIntell | accessdate=7 February 2015}}</ref>雖然他最終發現此地已被殖民地化，且有至少三分之二的當地人已經被歐洲人帶來的疾病殺死，但高更仍然在此地以當地女性為主題、繪製出了一系列名作。<ref name='"Graves"'>{{cite web|last1=Graves|first1=Jen|title=You May Be Infected Already|url=http://www.thestranger.com/seattle/you-may-be-infected-already/Content?oid=12587203|website=thestranger.com|publisher=[[The_Stranger_(newspaper)|The Stranger]]|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6WCGlQDkJ?url=http://www.thestranger.com/seattle/you-may-be-infected-already/Content?oid=12587203|archivedate=2015-02-08|deadurl=no|access-date=2015-02-09}}</ref>

==畫作==
[[File:Gauguin_-_Kauerndes_Mädchen_von_Tahiti_-_1892.jpg|thumb]]]]

畫面背景以綠、黃和藍色構成，其中是[[熱帶|熱帶]]塔西提島景色，用色明亮。畫中最前面的女性穿著著當地的傳統服裝，半跪在地上。後面的女子穿著的服飾受到了西方社會的影響，帶有高領。毛雷爾（Naomi E. Maurer）認為後者的手勢是佛教的[[手印|手印]]，帶有恐嚇或警告的意味。<ref name= "Maurer">{{cite book|title= The Pursuit of Spiritual Wisdom: The Thought and Art of Vincent van Gogh and Paul Gauguin|first= Naomi E.|last= Maurer|url={{Google books|wf_PoXKhggkC|page=148|plainurl=yes}}|page=148|ISBN= 9780838637494}}</ref>
