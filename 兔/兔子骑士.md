'''兔子骑士'''（{{lang-en|Knights of the Hare}}，或译“野兔骑士”）据称是由[[英国国王|英国国王]][[爱德华三世|爱德华三世]]创立的一支12人（一说为14人{{refnest|group="注释"|确切的人数依据不同参考来源，有12人<ref name="Bailey"/><ref name="bartelby"/>和14人<ref name="biedenfeld"/><ref name="james"/>两种说法。}}）的[[骑士团|骑士团]]<!-- was a [[骑士团|骑士团]] of twelve to fourteen<ref name="number"/> knights that was allegedly created by the King [[爱德华三世|爱德华三世]].--><ref name="Bailey"/><ref name="bartelby"/>。实际上，这一名词是[[百年战争|百年战争]]初期一次意外事件的幽默引申。<!--  In fact, the title is a humorous reference to an incident during the early stages of the [[百年战争|百年战争]].-->

==兔子骑士的起源==
在百年战争的初期，爱德华三世和法国的[[腓力六世_(法兰西)|腓力六世]]均在法国聚集军队，但他们并没有参与任何决定性战役。1339年10月23日，双方军队在[[拉夫拉芒格里|拉夫拉芒格里]]和[[比龙福斯|比龙福斯]]之间的地区形成互相对望之势<ref name="Roger">[[#Roger2000|Rogers (2000), pp. 169–172]]</ref>，在此时，在两军中间空地上被发现有一只野兔开始奔向法军阵地。随着兔子的临近，法军阵地出现了巨大的骚动。一些阵地后方的法军部队误将骚动当成是英军发起突然袭击的信号并准备投入战斗。骚动同样也惊动了英军阵地，{{le|埃诺伯爵，威廉二世|William II, Count of Hainaut}}迅速的册封了14名{{le|侍从|squire}}为骑士<ref>[[#Barber1974|Barber (1974), pp. 41-42]]</ref>。事后发现并没有遭到敌袭，而是一只兔子引起的骚动。由此之后，人们便称这些骑士为“兔子骑士”<ref name="james"/><ref name="neilland"/>。<!--
At the beginning of the [[百年战争|百年战争]] Edward III and [[腓力六世_(法兰西)|腓力六世 (法兰西)]] both assembled armies in France, but they did not engage in any decisive battle. On 23 October 1339, both armies were formed in sight of each other in the fields between [[La_Flamengerie|La Flamengerie]] and [[比龙福斯|比龙福斯]],<ref>{{cite book |title= War Cruel and Sharp|last= Rogers|first=Clifford |authorlink=Clifford Rogers |coauthors= |year=2000 |publisher=Boydell |location= Woodbridge|isbn=0851158048 |page= |pages=169–172 |url= |accessdate=}}</ref> when a hare, caught in the field between them, started running towards the French lines. The French troops greeted the approaching hare with a lot of noise. Some troops at the rear of the French formation mistook the noise as a sign of an imminent English attack and prepared for battle. As a preparation for the battle the [[William_II,_Count_of_Hainaut|William II, Count of Hainaut]] quickly knighted 14 distinguished [[squire|squire]]s as was the common chivalric custom.<ref>[[#Barber1974|Barber (1974), pp. 41-42]]</ref> However the English attack never came and, after the reason for that became clear, those knights became known as the ''Knights of the Hare''.<ref name="james"/><ref name="neilland"/>-->

由于这一队骑士团是在法兰西被建立的，因此有人怀疑英王爱德华三世是否真的按照正式的程序成立该团<ref name="biedenfeld"/>。之所以会有这样的混乱，可能是因为爱德华三世在战斗之前也曾册封过一些士绅为骑士<ref>[[#Roger2000|Rogers (2000), pp. 170-171]]</ref>，其中包括后来鼎鼎大名的{{le|约翰·简都斯爵士|Sir John Chandos}}<!--As the knights were created on the French side, it is doubtful however, whether Edward III really created a formal order for those knights.<ref name="biedenfeld"/>  The confusion may have occurred because Edward III also knighted a number of esquires before the battle,<ref>[[#Roger2000|Rogers (2000), pp. 170-171]]</ref> including the later famous soldier [[Sir_John_Chandos|Sir John Chandos]]--><ref>[[#Sumption1990|Sumption (1990), p. 287]]</ref>。
